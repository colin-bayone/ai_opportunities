DalTile: Tile Manufacturing Modernization

Opportunity Overview · April 2026
BayOne

DalTile is a division of Mohawk Industries, the world's largest flooring manufacturer. The target facility is in Mesquite, Texas. Our contact is Mahesh Adnani, an IT leader responsible for manufacturing technology. Colin and Mahesh have a long-standing personal relationship from their time working together at Coherent Corporation, where they built an identical manufacturing visibility platform.

The Problem

DalTile operates highly automated tile manufacturing plants producing 400,000 square feet of tile per day per plant. Despite this scale, they have no way to track yield, throughput, or traceability throughout the manufacturing process. There is no work order level lineage connecting raw material input to finished goods output. Production is measured only in aggregate (total square feet pressed, total kiln output), with no ability to trace a specific product through individual pieces of equipment.

This lack of traceability creates downstream consequences. Quality testing happens only at the very end of the line, so defects go undetected until the damage is locked in. Waste is unmeasured at every stage except the final sort. The legacy software system is written in a 1980s programming language and is now unmaintained. The result is that DalTile cannot identify where yield is lost, cannot attribute waste to specific process steps, and has no mechanism for reducing production costs at scale.

What BayOne Would Deliver

Two interrelated projects, starting with one production line:

  • Manufacturing Visibility Platform: A custom-built system that tracks each work order through every stage of production, showing real-time yield, waste, and equipment utilization.
  • Smart Factory, Sensors, and Quality Detection: Cameras and sensors deployed on the production line to detect defects earlier in the process, reducing waste of materials and improving yield. Includes industrial IoT sensor architecture and edge computing for factory floor reliability.
