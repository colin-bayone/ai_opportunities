## 1. Opportunity Landscape Overview

This internal BayOne planning call surfaced multiple potential engagement areas at Tailored Brands. The call participants assessed each opportunity for viability, competitive positioning, and alignment to BayOne's capabilities. The discussion revealed a client organization that is early in its AI journey, actively modernizing its engineering function, and operating without adequate QA coverage -- all of which create openings for BayOne.

The key dynamic framing the entire assessment: Siva (SVP Engineering) was brought in to strengthen a "weak" engineering function. He has recruited his own leadership team from Albertsons and is consolidating control over all technology and engineering. Budget approval flows through Carl (CPO). BayOne already has placement relationships across product management and UI/UX, providing an insider vantage point.

## 2. Code Modernization Opportunity

### What Was Discussed

Code modernization was identified as an active initiative at Tailored Brands. The BayOne account team (Suraj and Anuj) reported that this came up directly in a recent meeting with Kieran Dharmavarbu (Senior Director, UI).

**Key exchange (Suraj reporting on the Kieran meeting):**
> "Core modernization is something that they are currently starting on."

When asked about competition, Suraj clarified that there is already an existing services company working with them on the coding side. He could not immediately recall the name but had it in his notes. Later in the call, the company was identified as **Commerce Tools** -- though based on transcript context and the speech-to-text correction note, this is the e-commerce platform Commerce Tools, and the relationship appears to be that Tailored Brands uses Commerce Tools as their commerce platform, and there is an existing services vendor doing code work around it.

**Suraj on the competitive situation:**
> "This piece he says is sort of taken care of. But he was curious to understand how we would go about it, what are we thinking. Definitely always looking to learn."

**Colin's assessment of the code modernization angle:**
> "Same thing with code modernization. If you say that they have this effort going, even if it's a vendor or an internal thing, whatever the case, if they're not handling QA at the same time as that, that's crazy."

Colin specifically positioned code modernization as differentiated through BayOne's QA-integrated approach:
> "Why would you go and do all this work with the code and not verify it at the same time? So this is a good one. We can even give them exactly what we pitched to Cisco."

### Connection to Cisco Engagement

Colin explicitly tied the Tailored Brands code modernization opportunity to existing BayOne work at Cisco. The Cisco engagement involves CI/CD pipeline improvement and code modernization with integrated QA. Colin noted:
> "We can even give them exactly what we pitched to Cisco. So we have that. That's well understood."

He later reinforced this: "If this is UI/UX development or testing, that's fine, because that's part of the scope of work for Cisco. So that's work that we can definitely do."

This means BayOne has a ready-made pitch and methodology that can be adapted from the Cisco NX-OS engagement for Tailored Brands' retail context.

### Colin's Framing of Code Modernization Specifics

Colin speculated on the nature of the modernization effort:
> "What almost always comes is when they're talking about code modernization, what code are they referring to? Because that could be their internal stuff that they have for any internal process automation, internal systems, things like that. Or it could be code that's for one of their external facing products."

He specifically called out the Men's Wearhouse app as a likely target:
> "I'm no expert on Men's Wearhouse, but... I imagine they haven't [modernized], for instance. I imagine they need to keep that app up to date. They want to have new features in. That's probably where this is going."

He also assessed the likely nature of the work:
> "I think for code modernization, it's probably more 'we have legacy code, and this is messy over time. Let's go ahead and clean it up.'"

### Competitive Assessment

The existing services vendor was described as pre-dating the current leadership team:
> "They have to give permission, so all of the coders and Kieran meet with them, and they work closely with that company that does some of that work too... it was there before all these guys showed up, even before Carl."

For contracting specifically, Infosys was identified as BayOne's competitor, though with a minimal footprint: "Infosys only has two or three. Two offshore with Kieran and one onshore with Kieran." BayOne is already actively displacing Infosys -- Suraj described moving one of Infosys's contractors to BayOne due to poor treatment by Infosys.

### Viability Assessment

Colin rated this opportunity favorably:
> "That's a good starting point for a conversation. It's a low number of people too. It's a high work output. Also easier to source."

The combination of existing methodology (from Cisco), low headcount requirement, high output visibility, and an absent QA function makes this an attractive entry point.

## 3. AI Initiatives: Colin's Internal vs. External Framework

Colin introduced a two-bucket framework for categorizing AI opportunities at Tailored Brands. This framing was presented as a deliberate analytical tool for the BayOne team.

**Colin's exact framing:**
> "Just to break it down into two buckets, because this will help you. Internal AI initiatives, like things that they're doing like supply chain stuff, where if they want to improve on internal -- like for instance, on the QA side, I think the code modernization, this falls into the [internal] term. Even though it might touch external things, it's still internal control. The output product remains the same. If it happens faster or better, that's one thing, but still the output doesn't change. External-facing ones are exactly like you said. If they want something on their website that's like an AI-powered feature, that goes into this bucket."

### Internal AI Bucket

Colin placed the following into the internal bucket:

1. **Code Modernization QA (AI-assisted testing):** Generating tests as part of the code modernization effort. Colin specifically noted that even if Siva does not control QA organizationally, he can still control test generation within his engineering scope: "These guys are just hitting the key and debugging and testing and recording. So that could still be part of this."

2. **Customer Intelligence / Customer Segmentation:** Colin acknowledged these are common across retail: "With these internal ones, these are pretty much some of them are in-industry, customer segmentation, even though it's 90% of the same industry to industry, it's still more heavy, more data available."

3. **Supply Chain Optimization:** Mentioned as a category of internal AI but not elaborated on in detail during this call.

4. **Business Intelligence / Analytics:** Colin flagged the data ownership question as critical: "The rest of these, this will fall into the business intelligence space, but also comes back to this -- where's the data? Who owns that? What's their role in this whole process?"

**Colin's assessment of internal AI opportunities:**
> "The internal ones are good. The internal ones are almost always good, especially if they're just starting this out, because the fact is it's 2026 -- that's going to [be late] to the game for a lot of people. They're probably feeling that too."

### External AI Bucket

The primary external-facing AI initiative surfaced was:

1. **AI-Powered Shopping / Suit Picker for Weddings:** Suraj relayed this from the Kieran meeting: "Kieran also mentioned that they want to have the shopping... done by AI quicker versus having [the] customer go and do it manually... They're trying to bring in their large contracts for weddings and all of that... through AI. So that way whoever the coordinator is doesn't have to manually put in. But they already have all of this where it mixes and matches all the suits for the wedding... But they want AI to come in and make it easier for their large customers."

**Colin's candid assessment of external AI initiatives:**
> "From the features that are like more commercial ones, that's where we're going to want to know what their year plan is on this. Because they're always going to have like these kind of fleeting things like 'we want this on the website,' 'we want to have this in the app,' where 'we want to have like a suit picker.' Things like that. Short-term projects."

He expressed skepticism about sustainability:
> "You tend to get stuck in POC lane with this, and it doesn't always go somewhere unless there's a person behind it in the org that has actual power to move it."

> "Usually those are very much owned by that person internally. They're probably going to [lean] more staff and solutions on that side, in my opinion."

The external bucket was clearly assessed as lower priority and higher risk compared to the internal bucket.

## 4. The QA Gap as Entry Point

### The Problem

The absence of QA within Siva's organization was identified as one of the most significant findings of the call. Multiple participants confirmed this gap:

**Suraj on Kalyan's background at Albertsons:**
> "His biggest pet peeve, I can tell you, is no QA. The shift left has not been active at Albertsons. He's been breached. He lost his battle. He burned out. Siva knows him and pulled him out, basically."

**Suraj confirming QA is outside Siva's org:**
> "Siva does not have QA."

The QA function reports to Vijay Kampole (Senior Director, Houston), with Manoj Thora under him. Siva has been trying to bring QA under his organization but "he's losing the battle." There appears to be organizational friction: "Vijay is more worried at this point about losing his QA area to Siva."

**Colin's assessment of the shift-left gap:**
> "And again, the shift left has not happened either, from what I can tell."

### Colin's Strategic Reframing

Colin made a critical strategic observation about how BayOne can work around the organizational QA ownership problem. Even though Siva does not control QA, the AI-assisted code modernization approach effectively gives him control over test generation:

> "One thing that we can do for them, because this is where they should look naturally. If you're writing the code, you should be [testing it]. But the thing is that the QA teams don't necessarily need to do anything other than verify that [the tests] actually ran and happened. But generating the test can still happen here. So even though he doesn't have control over QA, he effectively has control over QA. Because these guys are just hitting the key and debugging and testing and recording. So that could still be part of this."

This reframing turns the organizational obstacle (Siva cannot get QA under his control) into an opportunity (BayOne delivers AI-generated testing as part of the code modernization scope, bypassing the QA ownership fight entirely).

**Colin on the competitive angle:**
> "Even if someone else has already started on it, maybe they haven't looked at it from that angle, maybe he doesn't realize that that [QA] can get unstuck in that way."

### Why Code Modernization Without QA Is Problematic

Colin was blunt about this:
> "If they're not handling QA at the same time as [code modernization], that's crazy."

> "This is a good way to draw kind of a foil between how we would do it versus how someone else is doing it. QA should happen as a part of this stuff."

The existing code modernization vendor apparently is not incorporating QA into their approach, which creates a natural differentiator for BayOne.

## 5. Kalyan: The New AI Director Who Admitted He Doesn't Know AI

### Background

Kalyan was recently appointed Director of AI by Siva. His background is in merchandising and tech engineering, with some DevOps exposure. He came from Albertsons, where BayOne had placed people under him.

**Suraj on the appointment:**
> "Siva has made him, until a week ago, a director of AI now."

### The Admission

A significant development was shared from the morning of the call:

**Suraj:**
> "I just heard this morning from them that Kalyan literally admitted in the meeting that he doesn't know anything about AI."

Suraj offered some nuance: "Whether it's a front, I'll be very lazy. Whether it's a front or not a front, you know." (Meaning: it is unclear whether Kalyan is being strategically humble or genuinely lacks AI knowledge.)

Additional context: BayOne's own product manager placed at Tailored Brands was being asked to take on AI work. Suraj intervened with Siva:
> "We have a product manager right now over there... They love him, but they're trying to get him to do more AI work and he knows AI. So he's kind of pushing himself as AI until I had to jump in and talk to Siva a couple nights ago, and said, this guy's going to want more money."

As of the morning of the call: "He's actually asking our own product manager as of this morning about the AI questions."

### Colin's Assessment of This Dynamic

Colin saw this as a textbook opening for BayOne:

> "If you have someone that's new, that's maybe a little bit out of their water, that's a ton of pressure... it doesn't make up for actually having done stuff like this."

> "From a functional standpoint, that's where our opportunity comes in, because he'll be looking to, as a partner, partner with somebody to solve this problem."

Colin characterized people in Kalyan's position:
> "Typically, guys like this, if they don't have experience, they're good idea guys, but they don't actually do it. They usually struggle there to gain any momentum traction. Don't really have much to go from. Which is a good place for us."

### Organizational Nuance

Despite Kalyan's title as Director of AI, Kieran Dharmavarbu is expected to be the actual driver of AI initiatives:

**Suraj:**
> "Kieran is going to be pretty much over him. And Kieran won't advertise [it]. He's super, super humble person."

Kieran was also described as not deeply experienced in AI: "He's extremely sharp. Kieran has managed teams of over 140 contractors, FTEs... he's big on iOS, Android, all the mobile technology." But AI functional experience is not part of his background.

## 7. Data and Analytics Gap

Colin repeatedly flagged a notable absence in the organizational picture: there is no visible data/analytics leadership or ownership within Siva's organization.

**Colin:**
> "If you don't have an operational team here, then this data [box], which is visibly absent from this, this is very silent. Who's doing what? Where's the ownership line? And are they doing something for one project on [one] team or are they thinking a little bit bigger?"

This observation was framed as both a risk (AI initiatives require data infrastructure) and an opportunity (BayOne could help fill the gap). The question of "where's the data, who owns that?" was raised multiple times as something that must be answered before any AI initiative can move forward.

BayOne does have a product manager placed at Tailored Brands who "works on analytics and insights right now and works with data" -- this person could provide intelligence on the data landscape.

## 8. Budget and Commercial Dynamics

### Budget Situation

- Tailored Brands' engineering budgets were approved as of February 1, 2026
- Suraj estimated approximately $2 million in prior year budget "just for a few areas"
- Contractor budget was described as unclear: "I don't know about contractor budget yet"
- SOW approvals flow through Carl (CPO): "All of our SOWs are approved by Carl for budget"
- Siva was described as resourceful with funding: "Siva will find the budget when there's a good initiative opportunity that saves them time and takes care of needs"

### Engagement Strategy: Incremental, Not Big-Bang

Suraj was emphatic about the approach:
> "They don't want you to come in and just say 'well, we've done this, we can do it all for you'... Break it up in the pieces. Don't give [them] the millions of dollars. Break it up in the pieces technically."

Colin agreed, recommending listening over pitching for the initial meeting:
> "I would actually go lighter on case studies, more on just listening. I think we should not pitch. We should just listen and brainstorm, that's all."

### Ego Management

The account team warned about the client's sensitivity:
> "We have to make sure we stroke their egos. Very much where, don't tell us we don't know this, but yet they don't know this, so we have to make sure."

> "Siva's very protective, and these guys are very smart... These guys are pretty smart. So even though they might not know a lot about AI, they'll take what you have and then brainstorm with it."

Suraj specifically coached Colin:
> "Just the way you approach it will be a little gentle approach. Does that make sense?"

## 10. Opportunity Prioritization (Synthesis from Call)

Based on the discussion, the opportunities ranked by apparent viability and BayOne readiness:

### Tier 1: Highest Immediate Viability

1. **QA-Integrated Code Modernization** -- Existing initiative, no QA coverage, BayOne has ready methodology from Cisco, low headcount/high output, clear differentiator vs. current vendor. Colin called this "a good starting point for a conversation."

2. **AI Practice Partnership (Supporting Kalyan)** -- New director who admitted he lacks AI knowledge, organizationally incentivized to show results, BayOne positioned as the experienced partner. Colin: "That's where our opportunity comes in, because he'll be looking to partner with somebody to solve this problem."

### Tier 2: Strong but Requires More Intelligence

3. **Internal AI Initiatives (Customer Intelligence, Supply Chain)** -- Aligned with Tailored Brands' published strategic priorities, BayOne has general capabilities, but data ownership and infrastructure questions remain unanswered.

4. **QA Function Build-Out** -- Siva wants QA but is losing the organizational battle. If BayOne can help him get it through the code modernization back door (AI-generated tests), this becomes a sustained engagement.

### Tier 3: Opportunistic / Higher Risk

5. **External AI Features (Suit Picker, AI Shopping)** -- Client interest exists but Colin assessed these as prone to "POC lane" and dependent on internal champion with organizational power. Lower priority unless a specific funded initiative emerges.

6. **Omnichannel Orchestration** -- Listed as a strategic priority but described as "fairly legacy." BayOne will gain intelligence through the incoming UI architect.
