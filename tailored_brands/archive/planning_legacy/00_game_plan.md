## Meeting Strategy Framework

### Philosophy (From Internal Meeting)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Listen and brainstorm | Pitch heavily |
| Ask questions | Tell them they don't know things |
| Go lighter on case studies | Overwhelm with capabilities |
| Break proposals into small pieces | Propose millions |
| Stroke egos carefully | Be aggressive |
| Let them share first | Pull information out |

### Conversation Flow

1. **Opening:** Let Siva give overview of their initiatives
2. **Listening:** Take notes on priorities, pain points, roadmap
3. **Clarifying Questions:** Show understanding, not superiority
4. **Brainstorming:** "What if..." framing, not "You should..."
5. **Next Steps:** Specific follow-ups, not vague promises

### Key Talking Points by Topic

**If AI comes up:**
- "We've been helping [similar clients] think through AI initiatives"
- "What's been the biggest challenge so far?"
- "How are you thinking about build vs. buy?"

**If QA/Testing comes up:**
- "That's interesting - we've seen the shift-left challenge elsewhere"
- "What's worked so far? What hasn't?"
- "Have you looked at test generation as a way to unblock this?"

**If Code Modernization comes up:**
- "How's the Commerce Tools engagement going?"
- "Are they covering the validation/testing piece?"
- "What's the timeline pressure looking like?"
