MEETING PHILOSOPHY

Approach: Listen First, Don't Pitch

This is a discovery meeting. The goal is to understand their problems deeply, not to demonstrate our capabilities. Siva will share openly - let him.

Do

  • Listen and brainstorm together
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Let them share first
  • Stroke egos carefully
  • Propose small pieces later
  • Show curiosity, not superiority

Don't

  • Pitch capabilities heavily
  • Tell them they don't know things
  • Overwhelm with case studies
  • Propose millions immediately
  • Be aggressive
  • Position as replacement for anyone

Key insight from internal team: "These guys are pretty smart. Even though they might not know a lot about AI, they'll take what you have and brainstorm with it. It's a good thing - but approach it gently."

Discovery Questions

Organized by topic. Lead with curiosity, not expertise.

About QA/ Testing

  1. How are you thinking about the testing piece alongside code modernization?
  2. What's the validation strategy for the Commerce Tools work?
  3. Has shift-left come up in your discussions? What's been the challenge?
  4. Who owns test automation today?

About AI

  1. What's been the experience with Bold Metrics so far?
  2. How are you thinking about expanding AI beyond inventory?
  3. What does success look like for Kalyan's new role?
  4. How do you see AI fitting into the wedding business specifically?

About Their Priorities

  1. What are the top 2-3 initiatives for FY26?
  2. How did budget allocation shake out after Feb 1?
  3. What's the biggest blocker right now?
  4. Where do you need to move fastest?

About Partnership

  1. What would an ideal partner look like for this?
  2. How do you prefer to work with external teams?
  3. What's worked well in the past? What hasn't?
  4. How do you measure success with your vendors?
