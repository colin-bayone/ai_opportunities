## The 24-Hour Update Expectation in Colin's Own Words

The most consequential structural change introduced in Set 14 is the 24-hour update expectation that Colin states in the middle of the standup. It warrants direct quotation because the exact phrasing matters for how the team should interpret it going forward. Colin: "Starting next week, if the only update we have is I'm looking into it after more than 24 hours has passed, There will be a problem. So I'm not trying to make anyone feel bad here, but I need to be realistic with this because everyone has access to the same tools is my point." The implication is not that updates must include completed work within 24 hours. The implication is that updates must demonstrate substantive investigation within 24 hours. "Looking into it" by itself fails that bar. A summary of what was tried, what was learned, and what the next concrete step is would satisfy it. Colin's pairing of this expectation with the aggressive AI tool usage directive in item 130 is deliberate. The argument is that the team has Codex, Claude Code, and Claude in the browser available, and that a well-written prompt to any of those tools can produce a substantive first-pass deep-dive analysis in well under an hour. There is therefore no 24-hour window in which a topic cannot have been meaningfully advanced. The firm warning is what Colin himself labels it, and it is the defining accountability pivot of Set 14.
