## Dynamics and Observations

1. **Colin drives all coordination.** Every directive, deadline, celebration, and course correction originates from Colin. No peer-to-peer coordination visible in the chat except Namita connecting with Vaishali and Saurav's technical questions to Srikar.

2. **Namita and Srikar are the discovery engines.** They are the ones meeting Cisco counterparts and reporting back. Saurav builds independently. This is a healthy division but creates a dependency: if Namita or Srikar do not report, Colin has no visibility.

3. **Saurav's Wall-E deployment signals readiness.** He renamed the bot from Volley to Wall-E, added it to the team space, and demonstrated it scraping all 45 messages into a database. This is not just a POC; it is a working tool deployed in the team's own environment.

4. **Askari's silence is notable.** One message in 16 days, no task assignments, no meeting participation reported, not part of either sub-team. This needs to be addressed.

5. **Response latency varies.** Colin and Namita respond within minutes to hours. Srikar responds same-day. Saurav's activity is bursty (concentrated on 4/10). Askari and Vaishali are effectively not participating in the chat.
