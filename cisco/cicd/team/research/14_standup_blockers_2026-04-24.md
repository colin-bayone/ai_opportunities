## Internal Only Blockers

The items in this section are internal accountability concerns raised by Colin during the standup. They must not be mirrored in any client facing artifact, including the Friday afternoon sync sheet prepared for Srinivas. They exist in this document as an internal record of the accountability pivot Colin made explicit on the call.

### Execution Gap and Pace

The most consequential internal blocker is a pattern, not a single item. Colin named it directly during the standup. Srikar received the CAT MCP direction on Wednesday, installed it, hit an NX repository permission gate, reached out to Niloy, and then stopped. Colin's response on the call captured the concern precisely. "He's going to not want to say that we, you know, thirty-six hours later, we only have, you know, an install attempt. Even if there was some issue, he's going to want to say, you know, well, in the meantime, we could have been exploring, we could have been doing that gap analysis." The install was perhaps ten minutes of effective work. The thirty-six hours between receiving the direction and the standup did not yield the gap analysis, the categorization mapping, or any other artifact that would have survived an access block.

Namita's pattern is adjacent. Colin sent the SBOM reference link on Monday. By Friday, the link had been read but not fully exploited, and the PR to PR trace that the link was intended to unlock is still open. Colin's response was explicit. "This is stuff that, I mean, between the two things, this background research, the point of this team is not to have just meetings with Justin. The point is to get the work done. And if I'm the one sending the link and all we're doing is reading the link, we're wasting time." He went further and named the core identity problem. "We have access to all the AI tools under the sun. So we can be able to research this. We can have an answer on this. I'm going to tell you right now, if I were to write a prompt to Claude, I could have an answer to this before the meeting."

Colin extended the observation to the team as a whole. "We've kind of been stagnating for two weeks now internally. I get that these meetings are happening. I know they take time to do. I know that we have some background to do. But, you know, it's kind of in the same boat, where I'm not seeing progress internally and that needs to change." The two week window he referenced covers Main Set 10 through the current standup, and it coincides with the period in which the team has been heavily engaged with Justin on dependency graph concepts and with Srinivas on MCP direction. The meetings have been productive for information flow. They have not produced deliverables at the pace Srinivas expects.

### Looking Into It Mode

Colin named the specific symptom that he does not want recurring with Srinivas. "Starting next week, if the only update we have is I'm looking into it after more than twenty-four hours has passed, there will be a problem." This is the internal accountability line. The external posture for the afternoon sync remains diplomatic, but internally the standard tightens on Monday.

The failure mode Colin is guarding against is the team presenting Srinivas with a week of meetings and a week of reading material but no working artifact. Srinivas does not reward exploration. He rewards functioning software in his hands. The looking into it state is the default state of the team two weeks running, and Colin has now explicitly disallowed it.

### AI Team Identity Drift

The third internal blocker is identity. Colin's framing is sharp. "We're the AI team. We have to use AI more than anyone else. And we have to use it in the best way to accomplish our problems the fastest. So if we're not doing that, or if we're manually looking into things, that immediately takes us out of being the AI team and puts us into the non-AI team." This is not a rhetorical flourish. It is a charter statement. The engagement exists because Srinivas wanted delivery faster than his internal teams could manage, not because he needed software developers. If the team's delivery cadence looks like a software team's delivery cadence, the engagement loses its reason for existing.

This concern connects to the execution gap above. The tools available to the team, Codex, Claude Code, Claude in the browser, and the assorted skills and agents already in the repo, are collectively capable of producing the SBOM analysis, the CAT MCP gap analysis, and the PR to PR trace plan in hours, not days. The team has not yet been using them at that intensity.

## Strategic Blocker: Reuse of Poorly Built Internal Artifacts

One item sits between the external and internal categories because it is both an execution issue and a strategic risk. Colin named it on the call. "The consistent problem that I'm seeing is that he keeps on asking us to use things that people already built that might have not have been built properly that aren't really meant for any specific purpose." The CAT MCP is the current instance. Naga's Pulse and Scribbler were the prior instance, already deferred in Main Set 13 after BayOne analysis showed they were not production services. The pattern threatens to repeat on the remaining eleven MCP categories if Srinivas's default is to point the team at pre-existing marketplace artifacts without a fit for purpose evaluation.

The mitigation path is to produce the gap analysis on every such artifact before investing in integration. This moves the team from consumer of unclear tools to evaluator of unclear tools, which is a more defensible posture with Srinivas and preserves the right to recommend a replacement build when the existing artifact does not fit.

## Escalation and Accountability Plan

Colin's escalation plan is two pronged and time boxed. On the external blocker side, he will meet Mahaveer today in person. If the permanent ADS provisioning is not reflected in the portal by end of day, he will escalate to Anand. This is the first time the ADS blocker has moved out of the team tier and into director tier escalation, and it is justified by the three week age of the dependency and by the fact that the deployment target for next Friday cannot be met without resolution.

On the internal blocker side, Colin indicated a structural change starting Monday. "Next week, I'm going to just assign issues formally on GitHub this time. And we'll track status that way so we can stay on top of this." This formalizes the accountability surface. Combined with the twenty-four hour looking into it ceiling, it moves the team from meeting driven cadence to issue driven cadence. The expectation is that each issue has a named owner, a twenty-four hour update cycle, and a concrete deliverable rather than an exploration scope.

The external posture for the Friday afternoon sync stays in deferred-term framing. The internal posture starting Monday is direct, time boxed, and deliverable oriented.
