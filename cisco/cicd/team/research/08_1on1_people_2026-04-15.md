## Dynamics Observed

### Colin in escalation mode

Colin took the laptop situation personally as a procurement and partner-relationship failure, not just an operational inconvenience. He said literally, "this is like the third time in a row that IT has been stupid" and "that is a problem, because I am going to tell ask for any of us what he wants to do." He offered unprompted to buy Saurav a replacement Mac under the BayOne SAL (Standard Approval Limit) and make Cisco pay for it, or at minimum, hold Cisco IT's two-month repair estimate up to Srinivas and Anand as evidence of a systemic problem. His framing was explicitly about protecting the team and about engagement leverage ahead of the renewal: "we have that renewal coming up and I want him to be aware, you know, hey, these are silly things that have nothing to do with us."

The meeting revealed Colin's coaching style on how to write escalation content. He was specific: no color commentary, fact-driven, include the two-month repair estimate in bold, document what work was lost, and phrase the impact as "I will have to redo that work if it is not returned." He explicitly told Saurav, "do not be yourself here" when Saurav's instinct was to downplay the impact ("I already was working on on my Bay One machine, so I did not really lose anything"). This is the third instance in the engagement of Colin coaching Saurav to be more assertive in written escalations.

### Saurav's instinct to protect others

Saurav repeatedly hesitated when Colin suggested naming individual Cisco IT support representatives in the escalation email. He said, "I do not want to get those support guys in a mess like for this case. For them, it is like a regular thing, and certainly there is an escalation for your ticket to a VP, so yeah, that might be problematic for them." Eventually he and Colin landed on including the ticket number but not the individual names, since "it was not like a kind of yanking me around, it was just kind of the way it is structured." This shows a diplomatic instinct that aligns with Colin's own coaching style from prior sets (architecture-diagrams-first, let smart people draw their own conclusions).

### Saurav's architectural thinking maturing

When the conversation pivoted to the Srinivas deliverable, Saurav independently proposed the same decoupled architecture pattern he had outlined on Apr 13 (Set 07): convert the current WebEx scraper from a bot to a service app, add an MCP layer, then a bot or downstream app on top. He extended the argument with a new and substantive detail on security: scrape once at space level and share the data across users rather than duplicating per-user, so that "in case of any attack, so the blast radius is only that group." This is the first time any team member has framed the architecture in terms of blast-radius containment rather than just compute efficiency. Colin's response was to add security, privacy, and access control to the three-slide architecture framework Saurav is producing for Srinivas.

### The "do not tell the others" aside

Colin was candid about the staffing rationale he had decided the day before in the Apr 14 EPNM one-on-one (Set 07a): he is moving Saurav toward cross-project work on EPNM while keeping Srikar and Namita CI/CD-only. He told Saurav directly: "I do not want Srikar and Namita Vaishali to know that because they are going to say, hey, why am I not involved there? And I am like, because you guys have laptops number one and number two, I want you to keep going on here, especially the two that are in person." This is the first written record of the staffing split from Saurav's perspective and confirms the Apr 14 rule (Set 07a, item 2).
