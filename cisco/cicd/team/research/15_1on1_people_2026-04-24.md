## Dynamics Observed

### Colin's register: candid coaching with personal accountability

Colin's register in this 1:1 is materially warmer than Team Set 14's morning standup. The accountability moments delivered to Namita in the morning ("pick up the pace now") give way to a personal-accountability framing in this private setting: "the gap is also in me because I shouldn't have let you have the chance to have that happen. You know, that's the problem on my side. I should have protected you in that way by having something there and communicating it better. And I didn't do that. That's my failure here, because I should have."

This is the pattern of a leader who delivers public accountability cleanly and then in private takes ownership of the structural gap that allowed the issue to occur. The two are not contradictory; they reinforce each other.

The two anecdotes Colin shares (industrial automation Raspberry Pi over-reach as a college sophomore; Azure chatbot deployment that triggered a CIO call) function as relationship-building moments and as normalization: "everyone makes mistakes; it has to happen once."
