## Dynamics Observed

Colin's register was warm and coaching. He opened with small talk about Srikar's weekend (San Francisco, badminton) and shared a bit about Pittsburgh weather. When the conversation turned to technical content, he opened with a psychologically safe framing: "no one knows everything. If we talk about something and you do not feel confident in it, it is totally fine. Don't think that I'm sitting here. I always hated that. I once had a senior guy that was like, oh, you don't know every single detail of every single aspect of AI. And I'm like, it's literally impossible." This is a deliberate contrast to the harsher "they would not pass an interview with me" register from Team Set 10's internal debrief. The Team Set 10 register was an internal-only processing voice; the Set 11 register is the coaching voice Colin uses with his own team.

### Teaching-to-delivery gap (retrospective context)

This 1:1 was Colin's investment in Srikar taking over the drill-down deliverable. Per Team Set 13 (Apr 22, retrospective), Colin reported that Srikar produced "zero progress in 36 hours" after this session, and a formal written-strike warning was threatened. Colin ended up building the full nxos-issue-categorizer skill himself over the weekend and the days following. The teaching session was well-intended; the execution did not follow. This is a pattern observation to carry forward, not a criticism of this specific meeting: coaching-heavy enablement does not always produce downstream delivery, particularly when the learner is new to the tool and the topic.
