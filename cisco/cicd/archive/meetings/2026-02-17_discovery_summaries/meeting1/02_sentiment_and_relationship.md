# Sentiment & Relationship Notes: Meeting 1

Capturing tone, enthusiasm, and relationship-building moments from the kickoff meeting. Both Anand and Srinivas expressed significant positive sentiment about the engagement.

---

## Overall Tone

**Meeting Energy:** Warm, collaborative, optimistic. Despite Bazel rollout stress, both Cisco leaders made time and expressed genuine enthusiasm. The meeting ran 2x longer than scheduled (30 min → 60 min) because they wanted to continue the conversation.

---

## Anand (Director)

### Enthusiasm for Partnership

**On BayOne's potential impact:**
> "We really want these things to kind of help us a lot. And we are interested to kind of work in this to make it happen."

**On reducing his team's burden:**
> "If we do our job right, your job will be easier. Yeah, that's how I get ready with the maintenance overload or operational overheads. And that way I can spend time on the development rather than working on this work."

*Interpretation: Anand sees this as genuine relief for his team, not just another vendor project.*

### Flexibility and Trust

**On timeline flexibility:**
> "I know practically it's never possible to start quarter here and there, so we understand that, and we're very flexible on that. So whenever we get started, the quarter starts then."

**On first-time challenges:**
> "I think first time it's always a challenge... We can start it off."

*Interpretation: Anand is being realistic and accommodating, not holding BayOne to unrealistic expectations.*

### Commitment to Unblocking

**On ensuring questions get answered:**
> "The thing where I want to make sure that if you have a question, you're getting the answer. And sometimes you post a question, people could not respond... I'll try to make sure that I'm paying attention."

**On escalation:**
> "If something is not moving, then I will expedite."

*Interpretation: Anand is personally invested in making this work, not delegating and disappearing.*

### Openness About Challenges

**On informal culture:**
> "We are used to very informal... But today I don't think there is [formal tracking]. So that's good enough."

**On onboarding pain (agreeing with Srinivas):**
> "So we need to create a, because now that we have so many contractors... we need to figure out how to scale out, right?"

*Interpretation: Anand is honest about gaps rather than defensive. He sees this as an opportunity to improve.*

## Srinivas (Senior Engineering Manager)

### Genuine Excitement About Collaboration

**On working relationship:**
> "Once you start working, it's engineer-to-engineer conversation. You'll find a good partner."

**On treating BayOne as colleagues:**
> "Once you are onboarding, you are my friend. So I'll treat you the way or treat me the same way as a colleague. So that's the expectation."

**On being part of the team:**
> "It'll be like you'll be also be part and parcel of the heated discussions. You'll agree, disagree, and then right, wrong."

*Interpretation: Srinivas is not treating this as a vendor relationship. He wants real collaboration.*

### Openness to Being Challenged

**On correction and feedback:**
> "So anytime I myself deviate, you have to correct me. I say that, hey, Srini, you are wrong. And obviously, if you fail here, please feel free to correct me."

> "You can always correct me saying that you are wrong. I don't mind in the private or for me it doesn't matter. From the technical point of view, it doesn't matter if you can catch me anywhere, even in peak meetings."

*Interpretation: Srinivas actively wants pushback. This is rare and valuable.*

### Vision and Ambition

**On building for the future:**
> "Anything we do should be future proof and ready to enable the agentic infrastructure. So while you are solving your current need, we may be solving another agentic infrastructure behind the scenes."

**On leveraging BayOne:**
> "I want to leverage you guys while you are here so that any infrastructure, any requirement we give, always put two hats when you evaluate."

*Interpretation: Srinivas sees BayOne as a strategic asset, not just task execution.*

### Enthusiasm for DeepSight

**Showing the platform (with pride):**
> "Let's go here. You can see that this is already live... Nice, very nice."

**On what's coming:**
> "We already have almost like 8 or 10 apps, but we're going to release one at a time. So this week we launched Triage... end of this week we're gonna launch Runbook."

*Interpretation: Srinivas is proud of what the team has built and excited to show it off.*

### Confidence in BayOne

**On timeline:**
> "My expectation is within like two months, we should have an app running live here. Because all the infrastructure is already built for you."

**On simplifying the work:**
> "So the work becomes simplified... the idea is to develop an AI app pretty fast."

*Interpretation: Srinivas trusts that BayOne can deliver quickly once onboarded.*

### Self-Awareness

**On his pace:**

## Summary

This was not a typical vendor kickoff. Both Anand and Srinivas expressed:

1. **Relief** - They need help and are glad it's coming
2. **Trust** - Willing to share internal information and be flexible
3. **Investment** - Want BayOne embedded in the team, not siloed
4. **Alignment** - Values match around quality and doing things right
5. **Excitement** - Proud of DeepSight, eager to show it, optimistic about what's next

The engagement starts from a position of mutual respect and shared goals. This is a strong foundation.
