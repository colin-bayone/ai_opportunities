## Communication Pattern Analysis

### Anand Singh — Escalating Directness

| Date | Message | Tone |
|------|---------|------|
| Feb 10 | "Let us use this Webex group for this engagement." / "Please get started" | Enthusiastic, directive |
| Feb 17 | "This is right webex group" | Casual confirmation |
| Mar 3 | "How is it going? is everyone on board?" | Checking in, patient |
| Mar 12 | "This is loosing steam." | Concerned, first negative signal |
| Mar 23 | "What are next steps?" (asked twice) | Pressing, repetitive |
| Mar 26 | Adds Shih-Ta Chi, asks if Divakar is back | Taking action to unblock |
| Mar 27 | "Can we come up with a tentative plan?" | Requesting structure |
| Mar 31 | "Any update on this?" | Terse, final patience |

Anand's messages shorten over time. Feb 10 has two multi-sentence messages with specific directives. Mar 31 is five words. The pattern is a sponsor who started engaged and directive, gave space, then watched nothing happen for seven weeks.

### Colin (You) — Detailed, Constructive, Proactive

Colin sends the longest messages in the chat. Every response includes:
- A positive framing ("Good news across the board," "No steam lost here")
- Specific detail on what has happened
- An organized list of what is needed next
- An offer to travel or be flexible

Colin's messages on Mar 13 and Mar 25 are structurally identical: status update, access list, offer to do more. This consistency demonstrates professionalism but also reveals that the underlying situation has not changed between those dates.
