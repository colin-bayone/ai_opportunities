## Key Observations for Engagement Strategy

1. **Anand is the single most important person in this engagement.** He holds the budget, defines the working model, and sets philosophical constraints. Understanding his priorities, past experiences with vendors, and internal pressures will be critical.

2. **Colin and Sarang are the knowledge gatekeepers.** Access to them (and their willingness to engage) will determine how quickly BayOne can ramp up. Their "limited bandwidth" is a risk factor -- if they are too busy, discovery stalls.

3. **The missing dedicated Cisco engineer is a dependency.** Until this person is identified and committed, the discovery phase lacks a full-time internal partner. This should be tracked as a risk.

4. **Zahra's role boundaries are unclear.** Is she purely account management, or will she be involved in delivery oversight? Understanding this affects how Colin Moore (BayOne) and the technical team should communicate and escalate.

5. **No Cisco executive sponsor above Anand is visible.** We do not know who Anand reports to or who ultimately approves the budget. If Anand cannot unilaterally approve $150-$200K, there is an unseen decision-maker.

6. **No BayOne technical leadership is visible in this email.** The email does not mention who from BayOne's technical side (e.g., Colin Moore) will lead the discovery work. This is a gap that the next phase of planning should address.
