## What the Team Got Wrong or Underestimated

1. **Foundational-first expectation**: The team prepared a complete log analysis architecture (ingestion, parse, chunk, cascade) without first asking whether Srinivas expected a foundational dependency model before log analysis. This cost the team a full section of presentation time and resulted in a redirect.
2. **WebEx screen-share reliability**: Namita's most technical section was repeatedly interrupted by screen-sharing failures. No backup plan was in place for this failure mode. Colin bridged by sharing from his side but time was lost. Future meetings should pre-share key diagrams via Teams/chat so a screen-share failure does not foreclose the architecture discussion.
3. **Time management**: The back half of Set 05's prep agenda (scope alignment, access items, decisions requested) was lost. No priority-ordering within the agenda was visible; all sections were treated as equal priority. Srinivas's time constraint was known (he had a next meeting) but the agenda was not reordered to put highest-value items first.
4. **Pulse-not-deployed as leverage**: Team Set 09 revealed that Pulse is not deployed. Set 12 did not use this finding in the Srinivas meeting (it would have been a clean entry into the scope-alignment discussion). This is a missed opportunity — but defensible given the compressed meeting time.
