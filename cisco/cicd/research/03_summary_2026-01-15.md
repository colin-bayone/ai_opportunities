## Key Findings

1. **BayOne lost $500K on a prior Cisco project.** The project had undefined scope and was structured as fixed-price. After 6-8 months, both sides pulled the plug. This directly produced BayOne's current engagement rule: undefined scope means time-and-material only, never fixed-price. This lesson validates the CICD engagement's discovery-first, quarterly-budget structure.

2. **Cisco vendor selection is relationship-driven.** The Abhay/Mahesh story confirms that Cisco decision-makers choose vendors they know personally, even when a better option exists. BayOne's existing relationship with Anand is the CICD engagement's most valuable asset. Expansion beyond Anand's group will require building new internal champions.

3. **BayOne has a replicable managed services playbook.** The testing engagement case study (enter via relationship, introduce process and tooling, build trust through reporting, achieve managed services status, expand scope) maps directly to how the CICD engagement could evolve from discovery through to a sticky, multi-year model.

4. **Colin's bandwidth is a recognized constraint.** Rahul is actively coaching Colin to delegate and hire so that he can sustain multiple engagements. Two strategic hires are being pursued for BayOne's AI practice. Until these materialize, Colin is the single point of failure for CICD technical leadership.

5. **A two-week Bay Area trip is planned for February.** Colin will be on-site during the weeks of February 9 and 16, with an AQO event on the 19th-20th. This trip likely serves as the next major touchpoint for CICD planning, Cisco relationship-building, and EPNM positioning.

6. **Mahesh is a potential Cisco internal champion.** Previously unknown to the CICD research, Mahesh is a Cisco person who regularly expressed to Rahul that BayOne should have been selected over a competitor. If connected to Anand's organization, this is a relationship asset worth developing.
