## Analysis

### Colin's Escalation Style

- Direct but diplomatic. Opens with the problem, frames it clearly ("hardware gap is the only thing standing between us and getting real work started"), provides context ("past the two-month mark"), offers two solutions, and closes with eagerness.
- The self-procurement offer is strategic: it demonstrates BayOne's willingness to invest and removes Cisco's excuse.
- "Every time I set an expected start date based on what the Cisco team communicates, that date ends up moving" — this is the closest Colin has come to expressing frustration to Cisco. It's measured but firm.

### Anand's Response Pattern

- 9-minute response time on the hardware escalation. He is responsive to direct asks.
- "Zahra just messaged me" — Zahra coordinated the escalation simultaneously through her channel.
- Mar 26 redirect to WebEx group: Anand wants visibility. He doesn't want issues resolved in private DMs where the team can't see them. This is good management — he wants the group space to be the single thread.

### What This Tells Us

1. **Colin has direct access to Anand.** The private DM channel works. Anand responds quickly and acts.
2. **Zahra and Colin coordinated the Mar 17 escalation.** Both messaged Anand simultaneously — a pincer movement.
3. **Anand prefers the group channel.** His Mar 26 redirect suggests he wants all requests visible so he can hold his team accountable.
4. **Hardware delay was Cisco's fault.** Equipment "had not yet been ordered" despite promises. This is not a BayOne problem.
5. **The private channel is for escalations, not routine.** Only two interactions in the full period, both about unblocking.
