## Pulse and Scribbler: Explicit Scope Deferral

The scope clarification that emerged in this meeting is the cleanest articulation yet of what BayOne is and is not responsible for. Early in the meeting, Colin Moore raised the Pulse and Scribbler access question on behalf of Srikar Kondapaneni, noting, "Last time I was asking about the pulse and the scribbler. But if we still need those, I think we can get the access to those." Srinivas Pitta's response was direct and left no room for interpretation: "No, I mean you guys are not part of that technique right now. I think NAGA misunderstood the original thing. So there are a lot of other trials in pipeline. So what you guys do will be involved in those pieces."

This statement is significant for three reasons. First, it confirms what the BayOne team had suspected since the early-April conversations: Naga's framing of Pulse and Scribbler as a BayOne-owned track was never aligned with Srinivas Pitta's intent. Second, it identifies the misalignment as Naga's misunderstanding rather than as a scope change, which removes any implication that BayOne failed to execute on something that was asked. Third, it leaves the door open for future involvement ("what you guys do will be involved in those pieces"), but on Srinivas Pitta's timing rather than Naga's.

Later in the meeting, Colin Moore returned to this topic to make sure nothing was left implicit. He asked, "one of the items we talked about earlier, I know this was maybe a difference with Naga, but for the Pulse and Scribbler, just to confirm, do you want us to hold off on pursuing that further?" He then distinguished the two subcomponents precisely: "I think for one of them, WebEx scraping, that is fine. I think for the other one, it was a matter of using Whisper versus using the Cisco transcripts. Do you still want us to do that analysis?"

Srinivas Pitta's answer reinforced the deferral and added concurrency reasoning. He said, "where did he commit though on that one easy part of Scribbler or I forgot. Yes part of Scribbler. Okay, let me talk to him. We need to bring him also on board first." He then turned to the workload argument: "Let us solve this problem then before we probably take that up right because you have anyway too many things on your hand right now." He followed with a reminder of priorities: "I want to at least get one thing going. Because the PR analysis is pending, right? I mean the CI thing, what is the problem we need to solve?" The final directive was unambiguous: "for now, hold on. We will bring you guys, but for now, hold on to that." Colin Moore closed the exchange by explaining his reason for surfacing the question, "I just did not want to forget it," to which Srinivas Pitta replied, "Yeah, just make a note and maybe remind me once we have something outcome, then we will say, okay, what about this kind of a thing."

The net effect is that the Pulse and Scribbler items are no longer open scope questions for BayOne. They are tracked, not active. Any Whisper-versus-Cisco-transcripts analysis is explicitly out of scope until Srinivas Pitta reopens it.
