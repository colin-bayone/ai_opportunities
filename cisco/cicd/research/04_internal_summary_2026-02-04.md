## Key Findings

1. **The VP is Venkat.** Anand reports to Venkat. Venkat overrides procurement, has Guhan relationship ($7M R&D), and is actively championing BayOne. This resolves the unnamed VP from Set 02.

2. **Srinivas's trust is in Colin, not BayOne.** Direct quote from prior meeting: "full trust and confidence." Zahra: "it's not me, it's not Rahul — it's essentially you." Colin is the single most critical asset in this engagement.

3. **Pricing strategy going to Anand today:** $150-200K range, two options presented with similar price points to nudge toward the premium, no pros/cons to encourage off-the-cuff decision.

4. **One on-site person is the compromise.** Two only justified by expansion business. The on-site person will be senior but functionally a relationship manager — keeping Srinivas happy while the real work happens offshore.

5. **BayOne has severe organizational problems.** No structure, bad proposals, unprepared client calls, too many people in meetings, perception gap. Zahra and Colin are forming a direct channel to work around the dysfunction.

6. **The budget "drop" from $150-200K to $100K is likely not a drop.** $100K is what Cisco allocated. $150-200K is what Zahra is asking for. Rahul may have reported the Cisco figure while Zahra was always planning to negotiate above it.

7. **Guhan expansion is real.** Venkat is setting up a Colin/Guhan/Venkat meeting. Guhan wants technical substance, not sales. This is the highest-value expansion opportunity identified so far.
