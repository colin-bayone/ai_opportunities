# About This Documentation

## What This Is

A detailed account of our journey on the BayOne–Cisco EPNM-to-EMS classic view proof-of-concept engagement. It is a technical document that captures the full methodology and approach used across the engagement: how discovery was conducted, how context was assembled and organized, how repository analysis was performed, how cross-cutting synthesis was produced, how scope was disciplined, and how the work was prepared for execution inside Cisco's infrastructure.

The document is written as a series of comprehensive, chapter-length markdown files. Each chapter covers a specific phase or discipline of the work. Readers can work through them in order for the complete narrative or navigate via the table of contents to a specific area.

## What It Covers

- The methodology applied end-to-end — from first contact through discovery, proposal, handoff-package preparation, execution-session preparation, and the working patterns that held throughout.
- The approach to reading a large, multi-product, multi-repository codebase without source access through systematic analysis of structural artifacts.
- The conventions and patterns that emerged for keeping scope tight, surfacing uncertainty honestly, distinguishing observation from conclusion, and writing material that downstream sessions can use directly.
- The architecture of the handoff artifacts the engagement produced: the numbered handoff documents, the kickoff materials, the repository analysis bundle, the cross-repository mapping, the primers, and the scratch materials that supported them.
- Technical specifics on both products — EPNM and EMS / CNC — at the level necessary to understand why the approach took the shape it did.

## Who It Is For

Two primary audiences:

- The BayOne AI practice internally. The documentation is a complete map of how this engagement was run, reusable and refinable for future engagements with similar shape (legacy-to-modern UX conversion, AI-accelerated modernization, large enterprise codebases).
- The Cisco team, so they can fully understand the detailed process BayOne has applied to their work. The documentation provides visibility into the rigor, the decision points, and the operating patterns at a level deeper than the engagement artifacts alone convey.

## What It Is Not

- Not a commercial narrative. Commercial terms, pricing, financial arrangements, and internal business mechanics are deliberately excluded.
- Not a timeline document. It does not track calendar dates of work, elapsed time per phase, or effort estimates.
- Not a metrics report. It does not enumerate lines of code produced, token counts, or other quantitative measures of throughput.
- Not a prescribed playbook to be followed step by step. It is a record of the methodology as applied here, useful as a reference and a baseline for adaptation, not a rigid recipe.

## How It Is Structured

Three foundational documents sit at the top of the set:

- This document, explaining what the documentation is and what it contains.
- An authoring-rules document, capturing the conventions that all contributors to the documentation follow.
- A table-of-contents document, listing the chapters and recording the current state of the documentation at the end of each working session so that continuity across sessions is maintained.

The chapters follow. Each chapter is book-length when necessary, not a short summary. Chapters cover phases of the work (discovery, handoff-package assembly, repository analysis, synthesis, flag review, execution preparation) and cross-cutting disciplines (scope control, uncertainty management, operating patterns) as the material warrants.

## Continuity

The documentation is produced across multiple working sessions. At the end of each session, the table of contents is updated with the current state and a date stamp so any subsequent contributor can see what has been covered, what remains, and where to pick up. Contributors are expected to read the foundational documents before adding content.
