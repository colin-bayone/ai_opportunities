## Context

Colin and Rahul discussed whether to adjust the $500K pricing and how to present it to Cisco. This call happened 4 days after the pricing model was built (Set 04) and 5 days after the scope reframe (Set 03).

## Key Decision: Do Not Inflate the Price

Colin raised the question: "Should we go higher than 500K because if they push us down on pricing?"

Rahul's answer: No. Instead, bound the scope clearly with assumptions and add a change request clause. This protects BayOne from procurement pressure without inflating the number.

**Rahul's exact guidance (closely paraphrased):**
- Write up the assumptions clearly
- Have a change request possibility for any scope changes
- "Request vendor to protect us from procurement meeting us down on pricing"
- Clear bounds on assumptions + standard change request clause = price protection without inflation

Colin agreed and committed to sending a simple one-pager to Cisco that day.

## Venkat Positioning

Rahul characterized Venkat as "more like a doer, not somebody whose own strategy." This is relevant context for how BayOne positions itself at different levels of the Cisco hierarchy.
