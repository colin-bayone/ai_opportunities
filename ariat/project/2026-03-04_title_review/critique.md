# Critique: Slide Title Options

## Anti-Pattern Analysis

### Slide 1: General AI Capabilities

| Option | Verdict | Issue |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "Our AI Practice" | FAIL | Too casual, "Our" is weak opener |
| "From Strategy to Production" | PASS | Existing BayOne tagline, proven |
| "Enterprise AI That Ships" | FAIL | "Ships" is colloquial (anti-pattern #7) |
| "AI Capabilities Overview" | WEAK | "Overview" is filler, adds nothing |
| "AI Capabilities" | WEAK | Generic, no differentiation |
| "BayOne AI Capabilities" | WEAK | Company name in title is redundant (slide already branded) |

**Observation:** BayOne's existing deck does not use "AI" in most titles. They use "Intelligence" or domain names. Consider: what does the capabilities deck call this type of slide?

**From deck:** Slide 02 is "The AI Execution Gap" - positions the PROBLEM. Slide 03 is "Our Approach" - positions the SOLUTION.

**New direction:** Follow the deck pattern. Lead with problem or approach, not "capabilities."

### Slide 2: Enterprise AI (HR/Finance/Legal/Marketing)

| Option | Verdict | Issue |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "AI for Enterprise Functions" | WEAK | "Functions" is bland |
| "Intelligent Operations" | FAIL | Buzzword, vague |
| "AI Across the Enterprise" | WEAK | Generic, could mean anything |
| "Departmental AI Transformation" | FAIL | "Transformation" overused (anti-pattern #8) |
| "Scaling with AI" | FAIL | Abstract, inspirational (anti-pattern #8) |
| "Enterprise AI Applications" | WEAK | "Applications" is generic |

**Observation:** None of these are good. They all try to be clever without being specific.

**From deck:** Slide 06 is "Enterprise Automation" - direct, describes the domain.

**New direction:** Consider "Operational Intelligence" or follow the deck's "[Domain] [Capability]" pattern. Or be explicit: "AI for HR, Finance, Legal, Marketing" - sometimes direct is better than clever.

### Slide 3: AI for Testing/QA

| Option | Verdict | Issue |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "AI-Accelerated Quality Engineering" | PASS | Professional, elevates QA to QE |
| "Intelligent Test Automation" | WEAK | "Intelligent" is buzzword territory |
| "From Manual to Machine" | FAIL | Contrastive framing (anti-pattern #1) |
| "AI for Quality Assurance" | PASS | Simple, direct |
| "AI for Quality Engineering" | PASS | Simple, direct, uses elevated "QE" |

**Observation:** The "AI for X" pattern works here because it directly addresses their pain point.

**From deck:** Slide 05 is "Developer Productivity" - uses domain name without "AI for."

**New direction:** Consider "Quality Engineering" alone (no "AI for" prefix) to match deck pattern. Or "Quality Intelligence" to use their "Intelligence" convention.

### Slide 4: Culture & Skills

| Option | Verdict | Issue |
|--------|---------|-------|
| "AI for People Development" | WEAK | "People Development" sounds HR-jargon |
| "Building Culture at Scale" | PASS | Concrete, addresses their challenge |
| "AI-Powered Talent Transformation" | FAIL | "AI-Powered" buzzword, "Transformation" overused |
| "Growing Skills, Building Culture" | PASS | Mirrors CEO language directly |
| "AI for Skills and Culture" | WEAK | Generic structure |

**Observation:** The CEO's exact words were "grow skills" and "build culture." Using their language shows listening.

**From deck:** No direct equivalent. Closest is "Developer Productivity" for skills.

**New direction:** "Talent & Culture" follows the deck's "[X] & [Y]" pattern (like "Data & Analytics"). Or keep CEO's language: "Skills & Culture Development."

## Professional Standards Check

### Does it pass the Tuesday Test?

> "Would this sound natural if read aloud to the client?"

Testing the best candidates:
- "From Strategy to Production" - YES, it is their tagline
- "Enterprise Automation" (from deck) - YES
- "Quality Engineering" - YES
- "Building Culture at Scale" - YES, addresses their specific challenge
- "Talent & Culture" - YES

### Does it match BayOne's voice?

BayOne titles are:
- 2-4 words
- Noun phrases (no verbs)
- Domain-focused
- Use "&" for compounds
- Use "Intelligence" instead of "AI" sometimes

---

## Verdict: NEEDS REVISION

The brainstormed options are mostly generic or violate anti-patterns. Need new options that:

1. Follow BayOne's existing title patterns from the capabilities deck
2. Are 2-4 words maximum
3. Use noun phrases, not "AI for X" everywhere
4. Consider "Intelligence" as alternative to "AI"
5. Match the confidence level of existing deck
