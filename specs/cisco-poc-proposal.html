Executive Summary

BayOne proposes a three-week proof of concept engagement to demonstrate our codebase modernization methodology. This engagement will provide Cisco with direct visibility into our analytical approach, delivery quality, and operating cadence prior to committing to the full modernization effort.

The POC can be executed against the MDS Element Manager codebase or against a comparable legacy Java application provided by BayOne. Both approaches demonstrate identical methodology and deliverable quality.

02

Scope

The proof of concept applies our modernization workflow to a representative slice of code. This includes automated codebase analysis, knowledge graph generation, and sample code conversion through our agentic pipeline.

Codebase Options

OptionDescriptionPrerequisites
Option AAnalysis and conversion performed on a designated portion of the MDS Element Manager codebaseRepository access, designated Cisco point of contact
Option BAnalysis and conversion performed on a comparable legacy Java application provided by BayOneNone

Option A provides findings directly applicable to the MDS modernization project. Option B allows immediate commencement without access dependencies.

Out of Scope

Hardware integration and SNMP communication validation are not included in the POC. The focus is on demonstrating the codebase analysis and conversion workflow. Hardware interface testing would occur during the full engagement with appropriate access to MDS switches or simulation environments.

06

Expectations

This proof of concept demonstrates methodology, tooling, and delivery standards on a bounded slice of work. Findings and converted code from a small portion of the codebase are directionally useful but not a substitute for the full engagement.

Complete modernization requires system-wide architectural coordination, end-to-end conversion, integration validation, and hardening. The POC provides a representative sample of how that work is executed.

The POC phase involves standing up the workflow, learning context, and producing demonstration-quality deliverables. Once the full system is deployed and we move into execution mode, velocity increases significantly as the team, tooling, and codebase understanding are all in place.
