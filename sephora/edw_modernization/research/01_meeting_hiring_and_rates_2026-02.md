## 1. Rate Benchmarks and Thresholds

### The Core Rate Framework

Mani has communicated a clear rate structure to his team leads. The key benchmarks discussed in this meeting:

- **Nearshore rate band: ~$75 - $105/hr.** Mani specifically references engineers from emerging talent hubs (Oklahoma, Kansas City, Utah) coming in at nearshore rates, with personalization team members at "around 75" from those locations. (Lines 298-299)
- **In-office rate band: $105 - $115/hr.** If a resource is physically in-office, rates between $105 and $115 are acceptable "depending on how experienced they are." (Lines 326, 329)
- **Hard ceiling: No more $120/hr.** The old onsite rate of $120 is explicitly dead. Mani states: "120 is something that we are not going anymore." (Lines 327-328)
- **Some in-office resources already under $100/hr.** Mani mentions "few people in the office who is coming from San Jose. They are less than like 100 bucks an hour." (Lines 293-295)

### The In-Office vs. Remote Rate Rule

This is a binary policy that Mani has instructed all four of his hiring managers to enforce:

- **At $100-$105/hr and above, the resource MUST be in-office.** Exact quote: "100 to 105 and above, they are actually like I [told] the team to be like they should be in the office. If it is not, if the people are not in the office, then actually like, we cannot go with that rate anymore." (Lines 311-312)
- **Below ~$100/hr, remote/nearshore is fully acceptable.** Mani is explicitly "not worried about whether they are in the country or outside the country" as long as the rate aligns. (Lines 289-290)
- **This is the standard across all four hiring managers.** Mani confirms: "when you talk to Andrew, Ram, Rizwan, Grishi, like this is the message that they will be also like [enforcing]." (Line 313)

Zara's internal interpretation during the call: for senior-level people, target around $110 (down from the old $120 onsite benchmark). Mani confirms that framing. (Lines 323-326)

### Rate Implications for BayOne

Zara explicitly states this rate intelligence is critical for pipeline building: "That would be our Bible." She notes: "no one's giving us [direction to] come down and we can, obviously... but we want to stay competitive." (Lines 318-320) This means BayOne has been pricing at or above these thresholds and needs to recalibrate sourcing to stay within Sephora's new parameters.

## 3. Anti-Moonlighting and Offshore Quality Concerns

### Domestic Moonlighting: Not a Problem

Mani states clearly that moonlighting (double-dipping) is not an issue he is seeing with domestic/nearshore resources: "I'm not seeing any double dipping, anything like... they are working out very well." (Line 301)

### India Moonlighting: Known Problem

Zara confirms BayOne's own experience aligns: "We've faced those moonlighting issues in India where people are double dipping, triple dipping, not so much here and nearshore." (Line 302) This is a shared understanding between vendor and client.

### Offshore Quality: "Bodies, Not Problem Solvers"

Mani makes a pointed statement about offshore talent quality: "You definitely find better talent here and nearshore because India is just like sometimes feels like bodies than actual problem solvers." (Line 306)

He adds the churn problem: "And there's such a churn." (Line 307)

### Strategic Implication

This combination of factors -- moonlighting risk, quality perception, and churn -- means Mani has a clear preference hierarchy: **domestic nearshore > onsite > offshore**. Price being equal, he will take the nearshore domestic resource every time. Offshore is acceptable but carries skepticism.

## 8. Key Takeaways for BayOne Positioning

1. **Recalibrate pricing immediately.** The $120/hr onsite rate is dead at Sephora. Target $105-$115 for in-office senior resources, and $75-$105 for nearshore/remote. This is not a suggestion -- Mani has instructed all four hiring managers to enforce this.

2. **Domestic nearshore is the sweet spot.** Mani is actively experiencing success with Oklahoma/Kansas City/Utah talent at ~$75/hr with no quality or moonlighting concerns. BayOne should source from these geographies or match those rates.

3. **Canada is in play.** Explicitly confirmed as acceptable, expanding the nearshore talent pool.

4. **The in-office gate is firm.** Above $100-$105/hr, the resource must be physically in Sephora's office. No exceptions communicated. Remote resources above that threshold will be rejected.

5. **Offshore carries a stigma.** Mani's "bodies, not problem solvers" comment and the shared acknowledgment of moonlighting issues mean offshore placements will face extra scrutiny. BayOne should lead with domestic/nearshore and position offshore carefully.

6. **Build the AI pipeline now.** Mani's request for "full stack with AI" and "AI data processing" profiles is a pre-pipeline ask -- he wants BayOne ready when the requisitions formalize. This is a differentiation opportunity, especially given BayOne's AI practice under Colin.

7. **Re-engage Ram and Andrew urgently.** Two of four hiring managers have gone quiet. Ram is back around the 19th. Andrew's SOW needs follow-up. These represent near-term revenue that is slipping.

8. **Protect the Grishi pipeline.** Candidates are on hold, not rejected. Stay warm and be ready to re-activate when Grishi's hiring freeze lifts.

9. **Find a home for the two nearshore developers.** Satish has budget uncertainty. Explore other managers and teams within Sephora, leveraging the Sithara-to-Alan placement as a precedent.
