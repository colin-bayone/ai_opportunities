## 1. Proposal Requested: Three Options with Flexibility

Mani explicitly asked for a structured proposal with exactly three engagement options. He was specific about not wanting it overloaded:

**Key statement (line 449-451):** "So let me see that we do 3 options. Don't over [do it], like 7 options, that would be too much. Maybe just restricted to maybe do 3 options."

**Key statement (line 452-453):** "And then maybe like one type of way would go for the option, another category for another option. And then actually, like, what kind of [resources] that [BayOne] can bring in?"

### The Three Options Mani Outlined

Mani described the options himself, giving Colin a clear framework for the proposal structure:

1. **Option A: BayOne takes a track entirely.** Mani said (line 379): "One option is for you to take it entirely." This means BayOne would own end-to-end delivery of an entire category/track of the EDW modernization, from analysis through implementation.

2. **Option B: Collaborative architecture and design, BayOne implements.** Mani described a model where the existing cross-functional "table" (Databricks, Microsoft, data platform team, data engineering team) does the architectural design collectively, and then BayOne takes on the implementation work. (Lines 379-382): "2nd or same piece to work out the architecture and design everything together... the architectural design is kind of like done by the [team], and then you can take [the] implementation."

3. **Option C: Flexible staffing / supplementation.** Mani emphasized that not everyone needs to be full-time, and he wants to see flexibility in how BayOne can resource the engagement. (Lines 445-448): "Even if we go like a staff [model], not everybody wants like full-time. And we can bring in like people for a couple of hours... it's very flexible... I would love if you're going to those options, see what kind of flexibility that you can have."

### Cross-Cutting Requirement: Different Categories per Option

Mani suggested that each option could target a different category of the modernization (line 452): "Maybe like one type of way would go for the option, another category for another option." This implies the proposal should not just show three pricing tiers for the same scope, but rather three genuinely different engagement models applied to different tracks of the EDW work.

## 2. The "Investment" Ask: Confidence-Building Measure

This was one of the most significant signals in the meeting. Mani explicitly asked to see what BayOne is willing to invest to earn the engagement. This is a well-known pattern in enterprise consulting: the client wants to see the partner put skin in the game.

**Key statement (line 394-396):** "I would love seeing that with what kind of like investment that [BayOne] can do. Together, confidence, [it's] not [just] a [confidence measure], but... what kind of investment that you guys can do. So if you can bring that point of view also in the proposal, that would be good."

### What This Implies

Colin had already set this up by offering a confidence-building proposal (lines 385-388): "From the proposal standpoint, I can give you maybe 2 different types of proposals... One is to build your confidence because I know we're new to you... So if there's something smaller that would be still useful to you, then we could do to start, even if that's at [reduced] cost."

Mani's response was enthusiastic (line 389): "Yes, it'd be interesting then."

The "investment" language means Mani wants to see at least one of the following in the proposal:
- A pilot delivered at reduced cost or at-cost to demonstrate capability
- A proof-of-concept where BayOne absorbs some of the risk/cost
- Some form of upfront value delivery before full commercial terms kick in

This is not a request for free work. Mani used the word "investment," which implies he understands there will be a commercial relationship, but he wants BayOne to demonstrate commitment and confidence in their own ability to deliver. The proposal must explicitly address this.

## 5. Staffing and Supplementation Offer

Beyond the AI-assisted modernization work, Colin made a separate value proposition around team supplementation.

### The Problem Colin Identified

Colin articulated the classic modernization staffing squeeze (lines 259-264): during a major modernization, the existing team is pulled into the new work, but the business still needs its current reports maintained, new reports built, and existing reports fixed. The bench gets thin, and the team is stretched across both the modernization and business-as-usual.

**Key statement (lines 262-264):** "Sometimes it's a struggle internally to tackle all of that because now the bench is getting a little bit light on people that are actually able to supplement that work and do it, but also keep up with the [modernization] work."

### What BayOne Offered

Colin offered to supplement Mani's team with additional staff who can handle ongoing reporting and BI work so that the core team can focus on the modernization (lines 264-266): "We can definitely help from [a staffing] perspective... both from this perspective, staffing, but also even just as a [consulting partner]."

### Mani's Flexibility Ask

Mani's response to the staffing discussion was to emphasize flexibility (lines 444-448). He does not want a rigid full-time-only staffing model. He wants options that include:
- Part-time resources ("people for a couple of hours")
- Full-time augmentation
- Flexible engagement models that can scale up or down

This is a critical design requirement for the proposal: the staffing options must not be "one size fits all."

## 8. Proposal Structure Requirements (Consolidated)

Based on everything Mani asked for, the proposal must contain:

1. **Three distinct engagement options** -- not three tiers of the same thing, but three genuinely different models (full ownership of a track, collaborative design with BayOne implementation, flexible staffing supplementation)

2. **Each option mapped to a different category** -- finance (as reference/validation), plus two other tracks such as supply chain or merchandising

3. **A confidence-building / investment component** -- at least one option (likely the pilot) should show BayOne absorbing some risk or cost, demonstrating willingness to invest in the relationship

4. **Specific pilot scope with assumptions and pricing** -- not vague; Mani asked for a "specific example with certain assumptions" and approximate cost

5. **Flexible staffing models** -- part-time, full-time, and hybrid resource options, not a single engagement model

6. **Case studies attached or referenced** -- Coherent Corp (architecture, SSAS/SSRS to Snowflake) and Cisco (Databricks, connectors)

7. **Evidence of lived experience** -- the proposal should reinforce that Colin has personally done this type of modernization, not just that BayOne has theoretical capability
