## What This Meeting Was

Initial discovery call between BayOne (Zahra, Neha) and Mani (VP-level, responsible for all marketing and enterprise-wide analytics at Sephora). BayOne was seeking to understand Sephora's 2026 roadmap and identify opportunities. Mani gave a thorough, unprompted walkthrough of his entire organizational restructure, project portfolio, and hiring plans.

## Key Takeaway

The **EDW Modernization** project is the dominant opportunity. It is a funded, multi-year initiative (2026-2028) to migrate thousands of reports from legacy IBM Cognos/SQL Server to Databricks, Tableau, and ThoughtSpot. Mani explicitly asked for AI help to accelerate this, and explicitly instructed that Colin should come with a prepared proposal, not a blank-slate discovery meeting.

## Critical Signals for the Engagement

1. **Mani wants a prepared proposal, not discovery.** His exact framing: if Colin comes with groundwork done, talking to the teams first, and bringing a proposal, "that would be a very productive discussion." This set the tone for the entire engagement approach.

2. **Two specific AI use cases for EDW:**
   - Analyze the legacy Cognos codebase at scale (queries are "deep inside the code," manual review would "take years")
   - Batch-process report migrations (do 3-6 at once instead of one-by-one)

3. **Databricks is already in the room** as a trusted partner, providing AI-oriented tools and recommendations. BayOne needs to differentiate, not duplicate.

4. **The org restructure creates multiple entry points.** Four hiring managers active across different areas, each with distinct needs and timelines.

5. **Rate ceiling has dropped.** No more $120/hr. Onsite is $105-115, nearshore is $75-105. This affects pricing for any engagement.
