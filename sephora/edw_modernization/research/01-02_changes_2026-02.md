## What Was Validated

1. **EDW Modernization is the core opportunity.** Mani confirmed in Set 02 that Colin's understanding was correct and his approach "looks good." The opportunity is real, funded, and in progress.

2. **AI acceleration is wanted.** Mani explicitly engaged with Colin's AI proposals and asked for a formal proposal with options and pricing. This moved from "interesting idea" (Set 01) to "bring me a proposal" (Set 02).

3. **Finance is the first track.** Confirmed again, and Mani added that it's nearly done ("another 20-odd days"), which shifts the pilot target.

4. **The team has deep institutional knowledge.** Mani emphasized that his people know the reports inside and out (3-6+ years). This validates the SME-in-the-loop approach Colin proposed.

## What Was Corrected

1. **"Re-engineering, not migration."** This was the most emphatic correction. Mani was insistent: this is NOT lift-and-shift. They are re-engineering from the ground up. The front end (Cognos) is being retained in many cases, but the underlying architecture is completely rebuilt. Colin had framed it too much as "migration" in his opening summary.

2. **Not everything moves to Tableau/ThoughtSpot.** Set 01 implied a broad move away from Cognos. Set 02 clarified: there is a desire to move to ThoughtSpot or Tableau, but that's not being pursued right now. Cognos remains as the front end to reduce change management friction.

3. **Finance may not be the right pilot target.** In Set 01, Finance seemed like the obvious first target. In Set 02, Mani noted Finance is almost done, so a pilot there might not add value. Supply chain or merchandising might be better targets for a BayOne pilot.

## What Was New in Set 02

1. **Colin is now in the room.** Set 01 was Zahra/Neha doing discovery. Set 02 is Colin presenting a prepared proposal with slides. The dynamic shifted from relationship-building to solution-selling.

2. **Colin's prior experience.** He worked at a 40,000-employee, $1.6B revenue company doing the same type of EDW modernization (SSAS/SSRS to Snowflake). This credibility point was not available in Set 01.

3. **Three specific AI approaches proposed.** (a) Pattern detection/clustering across reports, (b) code analysis and business logic surfacing, (c) automated mapping and validation. These are concrete, not abstract.

4. **Mani wants to see BayOne's "investment."** He asked what kind of investment BayOne can make. This signals he wants a confidence-building measure (possibly a free or low-cost pilot) before committing to a larger engagement.

5. **Proposal structure defined.** Three options, different categories per option, flexibility in staffing models, case studies included. This is an explicit RFP-like request.

6. **Cross-functional "table" revealed.** Databricks, Microsoft, and the data platform team all have seats. BayOne would need to earn a seat here.

7. **Semantic layer is pragmatically deferred.** Mani will not let semantic layer definition slow down the modernization. Progress over perfection.

8. **Accelerators being evaluated.** Databricks and partners have provided accelerators, each good for different things. The team is assessing fit per use case.
