## Executive Summary

Mani provided a comprehensive overview of his 2026 organizational restructuring and major initiatives. The conversation revealed:

1. **Major organizational shift:** Reporting is being decentralized from Mani's central team into domain engineering teams (Stores, E-commerce, Supply Chain)

2. **EDW Modernization:** A 3-year project to migrate from legacy SQL Server/Cognos to Databricks, with explicit interest in AI acceleration

3. **Marketing initiatives:** Three major areas under restructured leadership (Ram, Andrew Ho, Rizwan)

### 2. Organizational Restructuring - Reporting Democratization

Mani explained a major 2026 initiative: **decentralizing reporting** from his central team into domain engineering teams.

**Current state:** One centralized reporting team handles everything.

**Future state:** Reports become embedded within domain teams. Mani's team transitions to:
- Lean governance
- Framework and tool standardization
- SME advisory support
- Data validation and architectural oversight

**Three models being tested:**

| Model | Domain | Structure | Timeline |
|-------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Model 1** | Stores (David/Natalia) | Delivery owned by Stores; Grishi's team (Andrew Ho, Grishi) as SME support | ~1 year to stabilize |
| **Model 2** | E-commerce - Omni (Rajesh) | Grishi still hiring manager, but engineers execute in Rajesh's org | Full transition by June/July 2026 |
| **Model 3** | Supply Chain/Merchandising (Kalyan) | Remains centralized under Grishi | Transitions later once legacy tech modernized |

**Key quote:**
> "Reports will be part of those individual domain engineering use cases itself. Because that's the right thing to do, because they know their business, they know their project... report is just actually reporting what they have done. So it doesn't have to be done by another separate team."

---

### 3. EDW Modernization Project

Mani described the **major multi-year project** starting in 2026:

**Project name:** EDW Modernization
**Scope:** Re-engineering all reports from past 15-20 years
**Scale:** Thousands of reports in legacy technologies (IBM Cognos, SQL Server)
**Target:** Databricks, with Tableau and ThoughtSpot for visualization; some Cognos retained with modernized data layer
**Timeline:** 3-year project (2026-2028), attempting to accelerate to 2027 or early 2028

**Key quote:**
> "If we go report by report, it will take a long time for us. So we are saying, what would be an easier way, what would be the right way, like efficient way... can we do something like maybe if we do this, we can finish off three reports at one shot... six reports at one shot, so we can expedite the delivery."

**AI opportunities explicitly mentioned by Mani:**

1. **Batch migration acceleration** - migrate multiple reports at once instead of one-by-one
2. **Codebase analysis** - AI to scan legacy Cognos code and surface buried business logic
3. **Proactive risk identification** - AI to analyze codebase and flag areas to watch

**Key quote:**
> "Those things actually we are looking for AI to come and help to say, okay, look at the codebase, look at things, see actually, come up with like AI would itself proactively come and tell us, like these things are actually something to watch out for."

**Current state:** Already partnering with Databricks on tooling recommendations; still experimenting.

### 8. Hiring Landscape and Rate Expectations

**Key quote:**
