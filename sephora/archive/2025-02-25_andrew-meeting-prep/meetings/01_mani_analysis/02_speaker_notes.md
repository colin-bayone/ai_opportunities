## Mani Soundararajan (VP, Sephora)

**Role in meeting:** Primary information provider - shared org structure, roadmap, and AI interests.

### Topics Owned

1. Organizational restructuring and reporting democratization
2. EDW Modernization project details
3. Marketing org structure (Ram, Andrew Ho, Rizwan)
4. Rate expectations and hiring preferences
5. AI acceleration interests

### Key Statements

#### On Reporting Democratization
> "Reports will be part of those individual domain engineering use cases itself. Because that's the right thing to do, because they know their business, they know their project, they do what they're doing. Like report is just actually reporting what they have done. So it doesn't have to be done by another separate team."

> "My team will become a lean team, which is like a core platform report kind of a team."

> "My team is establishing like a framework and governance and all that, like what's the right tool to use?"

#### On the Three Operating Models
> "In the first one, David and Natalia will be the hiring managers. So they will hire the resources. But Grishi will be like in the interview panel."

> "In the second model, Grishi will still be the hiring manager, but they will be executing projects in Rajesh's area."

> "In Kalyan's area, for supply chain merchandising, we still have legacy technologies. Whereas in stores and .com, we are already in modern technology. So the first two, we are ready to do that."

#### On EDW Modernization
> "We're starting a big project called EDW Modernization. This project is to move from all legacy technologies into modern technologies."

> "We're going to be re-engineering all the reports that we have done over the past 15, 20 years. Over the past 15, 20 years, we have about seven, like thousands of reports, basically."

> "This one will be a three-year project, the first year is starting in 2026. We already started, by the way."

> "Right now the plan is already going till 2028. But we're trying to see how we can possibly do it in 2027 or maybe finish it off by early 2028."

#### On AI Acceleration Needs
> "If we go report by report, it will take a long time for us. So we are saying, what would be an easier way, what would be the right way, like efficient way to do this. Instead of going report by report, can we do something like maybe if we do this, we can finish off three reports at one shot. We can finish off like six reports at one shot, so we can expedite the delivery."

> "Databricks is already coming up with AI-oriented tools. For example, Cognos, let's say... something might have been implemented 15, 20 years back. So the queries are actually very deep inside the code. If we have to manually, if somebody has to go through that, it will take years for us to even finish this."

> "Those things we are looking for AI to come and help to say, okay, look at the codebase, look at things, see actually, come up with, AI would itself proactively come and tell us, like these things are something to watch out for. This is what you can take. So it will tell us where all actually, it will analyze the codebase and then say, okay, these are all the places for us to mind for."

> "We have also asked Databricks to come with recommendations. So this is something that you can take back to that person and they can come back with a proposal."

#### On Meeting Colin
> "If that person comes with a little bit of groundwork done, instead of coming and asking 'hey, what is this? What are you guys doing?' Sort of that, to keep that, that person can come with groundwork, talking to the teams and all that and then coming with a certain proposal, that would be great. So that would be a very productive discussion."

#### On Rate Expectations
> "If it is coming to like 100 to 105 and above, they are actually, I've told the team, they should be in the office. If the people are not in the office, then we cannot go with that rate anymore."

> "120 is something that we are not going anymore."

> "It will be definitely like from 105 to 115, depending on how experience they are."

#### On Location/Talent Preferences
> "I'm not worried about whether they are in the country or outside the country and all that. Like, there are people who are in the country, but they are already in the nearshore rate. So I'm totally fine with that."

> "Right now, what I'm observing is that some of the new talent hubs are emerging. People who come from Oklahoma or Kansas City or they are working from Utah. So these people are actually coming in nearshore rates."

> "Within personalization area, I myself am seeing approximately four or five engineers in nearshore rates like around 75 from Kansas City, Oklahoma, Utah area. Like talent is also very good, skillwise they are doing very good. They are committed, I'm not seeing any double dipping."

> "You definitely find better talent here and nearshore because India is just like sometimes feels like bodies than actual problem solvers. And there's such a churn."

> "If it is not in office, then the rate parameter, and there's no preference, you'd be okay with anywhere in the country, even in Canada. As long as they're in PST, yes, yes."

#### On Future Skills
> "We are coming up with completely like agentic coding and AI and all those things. So that will be a new required skill set for these."

> "We can prepare like two kinds of profiles... one would be like full stack, the other one would be with like AI kind of like data processing."

#### On His Direct Reports
> "Andrew, Ram, and Rizwan. Three people are direct reports."

> "Grishi reports to Andrew."

> "David does not report to me... David is taking care of Stores."

### Commitments Made
- Will send two skill profile types (full stack, full stack + AI) to BayOne
- Open to connecting Colin with teams after receiving proposal

## Cross-Speaker Dynamics

### Mani's Communication Style
- Detailed and thorough explanations
- Uses concrete examples (three models, specific rate numbers)
- Open about organizational changes
- Direct about expectations ("groundwork done", "proposal")
- Candid about talent preferences (nearshore > India)

### Zahra's Interview Technique
- Opens with value proposition (AI practice)
- Probes for pain points ("something that keeps you up at night")
- Confirms understanding with summaries
- Positions capabilities without overselling
- Closes with clear next steps

### Meeting Flow
1. Zahra frames opportunity (AI practice)
2. Mani provides detailed roadmap
3. Zahra probes for AI use cases
4. Mani articulates specific needs
5. Org structure clarification
6. Rate/hiring discussion
7. Clear next steps established
