# Critique: Neha's Follow-Up Email Draft

## Overall Assessment

**Verdict: NEEDS MAJOR REVISION**

The email reads like a project kickoff document, not a warm follow-up after a collaborative meeting. The tone is transactional and demanding when it should be appreciative and relationship-building.

---

## Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Transactional Structure (Major)

**Problem:** The headers "What we're building" and "What we need from your team" frame this as a vendor-client transaction, not a collaborative partnership.

**Evidence:**
- "What we're building:" - Sounds like we're announcing a project plan
- "What we need from your team:" - Sounds demanding, like a requirements doc

**Fix:** Remove these headers entirely. Weave the information into natural paragraphs that feel conversational.

---

### Issue 2: List-Heavy Format (Major)

**Problem:** The email is 80% bullet points. This reads like a project tracker, not a human email.

**Evidence:**
- 3 bullets under "What we're building"
- 4 numbered items under "What we need"

**Fix:** Convert to flowing paragraphs. An email from a person should read like... an email from a person.

---

### Issue 3: AI Tell Phrases (Medium)

| Pattern | Location | Fix |
|---------|----------|-----|
| "That level of detail is exactly what we needed" | Paragraph 1 | Generic. Reference something specific from the call. |
| "If scope allows" | Bullet 3 | Hedging phrase. Be direct. |
| "we are happy to set up" | Closing | Formal AI phrasing. Use "I'm happy to" or just "let me know." |
| "Looking forward to showing you" | Closing | Generic closer. Be more specific. |

---

### Issue 4: Missing Warmth (Major)

**Problem:** The meeting had a memorable moment (expectation mismatch → Andrew's rescue → relief). The email should acknowledge this was a *good* meeting despite the rocky start.

**Evidence:**
- No reference to the collaborative problem-solving
- No acknowledgment of specific people's contributions
- No personality

**Fix:** Add a genuine acknowledgment of how the meeting went. Reference specific moments or insights from specific people.

---

### Issue 5: Wrong Framing for the "Ask" (Major)

**Problem:** "What we need from your team" puts the burden on them. This was supposed to be collaborative - they *offered* to provide these things.

**Evidence from meeting transcript:**
- Gariashi: "We need to work with Vlad to get that. We can pick one report from finance."
- Maher: "We probably can have a simple DataStage job that we can share."
- These were *offers*, not requests.

**Fix:** Frame it as "Here's what you mentioned you could share" not "Here's what we need from you."

---

### Issue 6: No Subject Line Personality (Minor)

**Problem:** "Next Steps: Demo Preparation for Cognos/DataStage Agent POC" is corporate jargon.

**Fix:** Something warmer like "Following up - demo prep" or "Thank you + next steps"

---

---

## Recommended Rewrite Approach

1. **Open with genuine thanks** - Reference something specific from the call
2. **Acknowledge the collaboration** - They helped us figure out the right scope
3. **Recap naturally** - "Based on our discussion, we're going to focus on..."
4. **The artifacts** - "You mentioned you could share..." not "We need..."
5. **Warm close** - Specific to this relationship, not generic

---

## What the Email Should Sound Like

A person who was in that meeting, talking to people they've now met, keeping momentum going without being pushy or transactional.

Not: A vendor sending a requirements checklist to a client.
