## Red Flags to Watch For

### In Today's Meeting

| Red Flag | What It Means | Response |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Short answers | Low engagement | Ask more open questions |
| "We've tried that" | Skepticism | Ask what didn't work |
| Deference to Databricks | Competition strong | Emphasize complement |
| No follow-up discussion | Not interested | Probe for concerns |
| "Let us think about it" | Stalling | Ask what concerns remain |
| Comparison without context | Testing | Differentiate clearly |

### In Proposal Response

| Red Flag | What It Means | Response |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Long silence | Decision delayed | Follow up proactively |
| "Too expensive" | Budget mismatch | Offer scaled option |
| "Not the right time" | Priority shift | Ask when would be right |
| "Need to see more proof" | Trust gap | Offer smaller pilot |
| Escalation to Vlad | Beyond Mani's authority | Prepare executive pitch |

- Mani validated: "You understood it very well"

**Continue to Address:**
- Same preparation level for today
- Proposal should show deep understanding
- Never start from scratch

### For "AI Overpromise" Concern

**Strategy:**
- Use Colin's "deterministic + AI hybrid" framing
- Acknowledge validation still required
- Be realistic about acceleration (30% savings, not 10x)
- Show experience with what actually works

### For "Competition" Concern

**Strategy:**
- Never compete with Databricks
- Position as complementary
- Work with existing tools (Lutra, Flow)
- Add methodology value, not tool replacement

### For "Rate Pressure" Concern

**Strategy:**
- Respect stated thresholds
- Emphasize nearshore US talent (OK, KS, UT)
- Show value at acceptable rates
- Flexible models (not all full-time)
