# Sephora Agentic QE - Opportunity Brief

**Prepared For:** Internal
**Prepared By:** Colin Moore, Director of AI
**Date:** March 2026

---

## Opportunity Overview

Sephora's Quality Engineering organization is undergoing a significant transformation. The company has mandated AI adoption across the entire SDLC since August 2025 and is restructuring teams into Agentic Micro Pods (AMP), lean two-to-three person units focused on small, frequent enhancements. This shift is creating immediate demand for QE automation capabilities and talent that can operate in an AI-native environment.

BayOne has an existing relationship with multiple Sephora stakeholders and is well positioned to support both outcome-based project work and staffing across the QE function.

---

## What Sephora Needs

**QE Automation at Speed.** Sephora's QE function has historically been the bottleneck in the development lifecycle. The AMP model is accelerating development velocity, and testing must keep pace. The team is migrating from Selenium to Playwright, building QE agents across the testing lifecycle, and investing in a unified knowledge base that connects development and testing functions.

**Trust and Confidence in Agentic Systems.** Sephora's QE leadership is actively working to establish a framework for measuring agent reliability. Current trust levels in agent-generated output sit in the 40 to 50 percent range, and closing the gap to production-grade confidence is a top priority. BayOne's approach to agent evaluation, which mirrors how teams assess individual contributors, aligns directly with this need.

**Talent Across All Geographies.** The QE organization has open roles across onshore, nearshore, and offshore locations. The AMP model requires co-location for pod members, which is driving a predicted shift from offshore to nearshore staffing over the next six to twelve months. Strong candidates with AI and automation skills are the priority, regardless of geography. Sephora's QE leadership has expressed willingness to create roles for exceptional talent even outside existing headcount.

**Upskilling and Readiness.** Offshore teams are not upskilling at the same pace as onshore and nearshore counterparts. There is a stated need for training that enables teams to orchestrate and manage agentic workflows, not just build them.

---

## Where BayOne Fits

**Playwright and Test Automation.** BayOne's internal QA/QE practice is built on Playwright with a deterministic-first methodology. The approach begins with codebase mapping through state graphs, integrates with CI/CD pipelines for targeted test execution, and layers AI capabilities on top of a deterministic foundation. This philosophy aligns with Sephora's own direction and prioritizes consistency and reliability over speed.

**Agent Evaluation Framework.** BayOne has a working framework for agent trust and confidence scoring, built on tracked human approvals that progressively reduce the need for human-in-the-loop review. This directly addresses Sephora's stated challenge of computing a composite trust index for agent performance.

**Screening and Talent Quality.** BayOne's technical screening process, led by the AI practice, provides a level of candidate vetting that aligns with Sephora's interview philosophy. Both organizations prioritize problem-solving mindset and communication over resume credentials.

**Training and Incubation.** BayOne's model of training all new hires as AI engineers, combined with a proposed incubation approach where candidates work on client projects while being upskilled simultaneously, addresses both the immediate staffing need and the longer-term readiness gap.
