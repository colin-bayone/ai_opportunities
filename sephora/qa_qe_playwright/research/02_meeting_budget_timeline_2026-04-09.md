## Engagement Model

### Vaibhav's Preference: Hybrid Between Model 1 and Model 2

Colin offered three engagement models:
1. **Model 1:** BayOne owns 100% of the effort, working hand-in-hand with Sephora but doing all the work
2. **Model 2:** 70/30 split with knowledge transfer built in; the existing team gets upskilled while the project runs
3. **Model 3:** Pure staffing (BayOne provides people, Sephora manages them)

Colin recommended against Model 3:

> [00:12:31] Colin: "I never recommend that 'cause that always ends up being less than desired."

Vaibhav positioned between Model 1 and Model 2:

> [00:12:32] Vaibhav: "My thought process, and I discussed this with Deepika as well. Of course, I'll be leaning towards between 1 and 2 where you own it, but under Deepika's team shadow, so they know what's being done. They are being trained as things are being built, they can provide their input and some sort of a governance, you know, on that tool, how it integrates with other tools that we already have in place. So those kind of input they can provide while you and your team actually works on the solution."

And on long-term maintenance:

> [00:13:14] Vaibhav: "And seamlessly integrates with our ecosystem and of course in the long run the maintenance and updates are fed, then obviously Deepika or future resources may deal with it, right?"

**Summary of engagement model:**
- BayOne owns execution
- Deepika's COE team shadows for knowledge transfer
- Deepika's team provides governance input and integration guidance
- Long-term maintenance transitions to Sephora's internal team
- This is a "build, train, and hand off" model
