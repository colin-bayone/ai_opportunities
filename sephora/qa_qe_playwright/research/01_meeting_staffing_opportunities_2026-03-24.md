## 2. Role Title Evolution and Its Effect on Candidate Quality

Vaibhav described a direct cause-and-effect relationship between role naming and candidate quality:

"Initially, it was called QE, AI and GB [likely 'GenAI' or similar]. So as you put QE in the title, then you get QE resumes with AI buzzwords. Yeah, you can tell... 'Oh, yeah, I've used AI in my previous job.' Let's talk what I'm looking at."

The fix: "Then we changed it to automation engineer. We got some good people, actually."

This is a practical lesson: the "QE" label attracted traditional QE people who bolted on AI keywords. Removing "QE" and using "automation engineer" attracted candidates with a genuinely different skill profile.

**Specific candidates mentioned after the title change:** Santosh and Raghav. The BayOne side confirmed: "There were a couple of people, Santosh and Raghav."

## 3. Vaibhav's Hiring Philosophy

### "Give Me a Star, I'll Find the Role"

Vaibhav stated this philosophy explicitly and repeatedly:

- "I'm pretty open in terms of seeking the right skill and then finding a good role and rate as opposed to -- especially these days -- as opposed to filling a role at this rate."
- "Give me a good... if you're talking about just a resource, give me a good resource, I'll find the right role for that guy."
- "I told [Deepika] in fact in my last touch field that, yeah, you may have two openings, and you may have five resumes, you may have like four people, but just tell me if somebody is a star, you'll find a good [spot]."
- Later, reinforcing: "If there is a star performer, you'll find the place for them."

This philosophy is unusual and extremely favorable for BayOne. It means the emphasis should be on candidate quality over precise role matching. Vaibhav is willing to create or reallocate positions for exceptional talent.

### Communication Over Resume

When asked "What would stand out to you on a resume?", Vaibhav's answer was emphatic: "It's the communication that we'll have. Anybody can create a catchy [resume]. A lot of these resumes, we've just started conversing and said, yeah, thank you. You can tell."

He expanded: "I have the mindset I don't hire the resume. You can put anything on a resume. I've interviewed people from some way better schools than I went to and haven't picked them. And the reason why? You can tell."

### Whiteboard Interview Style

Vaibhav described his interview approach: "I always put them on the whiteboard. Simple problem. It's actually nothing related to Java or anything. I'm not testing that. This is a common problem. Tell me what you're thinking. That's it."

Key distinction: "I don't care whether you solve it or not. You [could] give it to ChatGPT. [It] can give you the solution. I just want your mental [model], attitude. [How] you are relating to your problem. That's all."

He explicitly values the thinking process over the answer: "Whether they are able to solve the problem, [the] process tells you a lot."

### Alignment with Colin's Screening Approach

Vaibhav recognized a philosophical match with Colin's screening: "To be able to screen that mindset, you need a like-minded person to be able to do that level of screening. That's why it's tough."

Colin described his screening: "They've got to survive an hour with me on a call." The BayOne side noted: "This is not a regular screen, this is not your HackerRank, this is just, I don't know what this is, but this screening is like your end all be all."

## 4. Geographic Strategy: Offshore, Nearshore, Onsite

### Current State: Majority Offshore

Vaibhav: "We have a majority of our workforce offshore."

### 6-12 Month Prediction: Shift Toward Nearshore

Vaibhav made a directional prediction: "I'm talking about six months to a year from now. There may be more people transitioning from offshore to nearshore."

The driving rationale has two parts:

1. **Speed gap in upskilling:** "Not upskilling at the same speed as the [onshore/nearshore]." This is a direct statement that offshore teams are falling behind in AI/agentic skill adoption.

2. **AMP model co-location requirement:** "If this AMP model is successful, the way it's structured, we will need people to work together as two or three member people. It will literally be locked in a room for the entire day. They have to be co-located."

Vaibhav's recommendation to BayOne: "Which means if you can find good talent in the nearshore, South America, Canada, et cetera, that may be an attractive [opportunity], not just for me, across the board."

### AMP Model Implications for Staffing

The Agentic Micro Pod (AMP) model is a "very lean team working on very, very small enhancements, small and frequent enhancements." This structure fundamentally changes staffing needs:

- **Co-location required:** "Two or three member people... literally locked in a room for the entire day."
- **Speed-driven:** "Churning code on a very regular basis and that demands equally stronger fast-paced testing as well."
- **Primarily onsite or nearshore:** Vaibhav explicitly stated the AMP model pushes toward these two geographies because of the co-location requirement.

### Onsite: Premium but Limited

Vaibhav: "On-site is always a premium. We have 10, 11 QE leads here. All of them are SMEs. I mean, I don't have a lot of room to hire on-site."

However, there is an exception pathway: "But if there is a star player, then I can argue. I need [another person]. I need more people to [do] this." This loops back to his "give me a star" philosophy.

### Offshore: Current Opportunity but Declining

The BayOne side asked: "How do you feel about offshore talent that can overlap?" and mentioned finding "really great talent in Pune."

Vaibhav: "Absolutely. I'm very open to it. That is where I'm currently focused in upskilling the talent because we have a majority of our workforce offshore."

But immediately he pivoted to the future: "I'm talking about six months to a year from now. There may be more people transitioning from offshore to nearshore... maybe looking at converting [the ratio], we offshore to one nearshore to one onsite, but bringing in presence more this side of the world."

**Key inference:** Offshore is a current opportunity window that may narrow. The trend is toward nearshore and onsite.

### Remote Onshore at Nearshore Rates

Colin introduced a third option: "There is even onshore... Bay Area, especially California in general, is way higher rate... Even for onshore roles if it's remote, there's people we've already found some there too that are going to be at like a nearshore rate or even better sometimes depending upon where they are in the country."

He specified: "Generally speaking they're in Central or Eastern time but the Pacific time overlaps way easier because it's really just working an extra hour or two in the day."

Vaibhav's reaction was receptive: "Coming in the office, that's what I needed for key meetings. I mean, unless you are part of the leadership, then there are some important things that you are attending. The rest of it can be done virtually."

His summary: "I'm open to all."

## 8. BayOne's Upskilling and Training Offer

Colin introduced two training-related offerings:

### 1. Mandatory AI Onboarding for New Hires
"One thing that will be coming for all the new hires is upskilling training that'll come from us. So even if their role has nothing to do with AI, they're going to come in with those skills from the start. That's from anyone that comes from BayOne from a staffing perspective."

### 2. Incubation Model (Contract-to-Hire Variant)
Colin proposed: "What if instead of like a contract-to-hire situation... what if it is a we incubate the person, they're working on your project for you, we incubate them while they're with us, we're teaching them what they need to know. They're still working. They're still getting everything done. But at the same time, they're learning."

The value proposition: "It gives us a chance to work with that person and really see how they are in the wild, I like to say, before we push off to them."

### 3. External Team Training
"Also going to think about doing actual real training, even not for BayOne, even if this is something where there's teams that want to be upskilled."

Vaibhav had separately identified upskilling as a major need: "We need talented people across the board, not just creating or working on the platform... but from pure user perspective, how to use this technology to deliver business commitments. That is another aspect of it. You are not just creating agents. You have to be solid enough to be able to use and orchestrate between these agents to deliver the right outcome."
