## Three Engagement Models (Corrected and Clarified)

Colin provided significant clarification on how the three models should be framed. The transcript captured them at a surface level, but the underlying philosophy is more nuanced.

### Model 1: Fully Managed Solution

BayOne takes a defined scope of work and delivers it end-to-end. The client provides requirements and feedback, but BayOne owns all execution. This is outcome-based, meaning the client pays for deliverables and milestones, not for hours or headcount.

### Model 2: Variable Split (Not Fixed at 70/30)

The "70/30" Colin mentioned on the call was one example of a possible split, not the definition of the model. The actual model is a variable split based on the client's appetite for involvement. Reasons a client might want to participate:

- **Cost reduction.** If their team takes on some of the execution, the project cost decreases accordingly.
- **Learning.** If the team wants to build capability during the engagement (exactly what Vaibhav described for Deepika's team).
- **Expediting.** If they have people who can contribute and want to move faster.

The split is flexible. It could be 90/10, 70/30, 60/40, or anything else depending on what makes sense. The costing adjusts to reflect the actual split of effort.

Like Model 1, this model is outcome-based. The engagement is structured around deliverables and milestones, not headcount. The difference is that some of the work producing those deliverables is done by the client's team.

### Model 3: Pure Staffing (Undesirable)

BayOne provides people, the client owns execution entirely. This is the only model that is headcount-based rather than outcome-based. Colin considers this undesirable because:

- BayOne has no ownership or accountability for the outcome
- The result is typically "less than desired" (Colin's words from the call)
- It removes BayOne's ability to ensure quality and methodology

This model exists as an option because some clients specifically want it, but Colin recommended against it on the call and Vaibhav agreed.

### The Critical Distinction: Outcome-Based vs. Headcount-Based

All models except pure staffing are outcome, milestone, and deliverable based. The client is not paying for bodies or hours; they are paying for defined work products delivered to defined standards on a defined timeline. This is a fundamental positioning choice that differentiates BayOne from a typical staffing firm and aligns with Vaibhav's stated preference for build over buy.
