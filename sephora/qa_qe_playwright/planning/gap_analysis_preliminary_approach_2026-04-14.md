### Severity Breakdown

**HIGH gaps (10):** These are items that should definitely be in the document before it is finalized.

- 6 are **outline compliance items** already identified in Round 1 feedback that have not yet been applied to the deliverable draft (title change, opening reframe, disclaimer, progressive buildout relocation, control panel removal, architecture section rewrite). These are known and tracked.
- 4 are **substantive content gaps** from the research that are not reflected anywhere:
  - A/B testing variant handling in nonprod (#1)
  - Production anomaly detection as a distinct use case from nonprod validation (#2)
  - Non-destructive guardrails and sandbox escalation (#3)
  - MCP server as a Sephora organizational requirement (#10)
  - Mid-2026 timeline as urgency context (#21)
  - Localization testing as a named gap with no home (#4)

**MEDIUM gaps (14):** These would strengthen the document. Most are Sephora-specific context items or Colin methodology details that differentiate BayOne's approach from a generic capabilities pitch.

**LOW gaps (10):** Nice-to-have items that add depth but are not essential for the document to succeed.

### Overall Completeness Assessment

The deliverable draft is structurally sound and covers the major themes well. The architecture content draft is a substantial improvement over the original Section 02 and, once integrated, will address many of the depth concerns.

The most significant category of gaps is **Sephora-specific operational context** that was discussed on the calls but is not reflected in the document. Items like A/B testing in nonprod, production anomaly detection as a distinct mode, the MCP requirement, localization testing, and non-destructive guardrails are all concrete details that would make the document feel responsive to the actual conversations rather than a generic capability overview. These are the gaps most likely to be noticed by Deepika during her scrutiny pass.

The second category is **known outline compliance items** from Round 1 feedback that have not yet been applied. These are tracked in the outline and are presumably queued for the next editing pass.

The third category is **Colin's methodology differentiators** that are either absent or underrepresented: the state graph concept, the recursive coverage model, the framework-as-bottleneck insight, the system autonomy distinction. These are less urgent but are the details that separate a BayOne proposal from what any other consulting firm would write.
