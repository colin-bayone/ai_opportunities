## 5. Strategic Notes: Layered Detection Approach (Filter/Mesh Analogy)

Colin walked the team through his technical approach using a physical analogy.

### The Filter Analogy
"Think about it like you have a coarse mesh, like a very coarse window screen as the top level. It's gonna block a lot, but it's also letting a lot through. And you do that because if you use too fine of a mesh up front, you end up doing what's called blinding the filter. The filter gets clogged because it's getting too much particulate." [00:10:02]

"The reason why you get the big filter as well is because they're cheap. So you want to use the cheapest way to stop things from getting through first before you go to the more expensive things." [00:10:33]

### Three Layers
1. **Layer 1 -- Rule-based:** "First level is going to be rule-based, yeah. And those are the easy outs essentially." [00:11:05] Anuj confirmed his understanding: "Any -- I'm just dumbing this down at my level -- hey, here's a command, anything with, you know, XYZ words, you know, pull it out. Whether it's out of context or in context, doesn't matter." [00:11:25]
2. **Layer 2 -- Semantic/NLP/ML-based:** "Something like semantic or NLP or, you know, some machine learning based approach." [00:12:18]
3. **Layer 3 -- Language model approach:** "The language model approach says yeah, this is definitely it within the context, because each of those takes a different tactic to identification." [00:12:18]

### Quarantine, Not Discard
Colin emphasized that flagged items are quarantined, not removed: "The trick here is that whenever we pull them out, they're not getting thrown out, they're getting effectively quarantined." [00:11:51] This directly addresses Lam's known false positive problem. Items quarantined at Layer 1 proceed to more expensive layers for review, not deletion.

### Convergence = High Confidence
"If layer one says this is likely a problem, layer 2 which might be something like semantic or NLP or you know some machine learning based approach also agrees with that. And layer 3, the language model approach says yeah, this is definitely it within the context... then you can say with an extremely high certainty that that is in fact something that should be identified for redaction." [00:12:18]

### Efficiency Argument
"The majority of the content is just going to pass through because it's not going to get flagged because it's clean. Those few things that are, quote, dirty are what you really want to focus on. And since that's the minority of the content, not the majority of the content, building a system in this way means that it's gonna be very fast and also much more cost effective than how they were trying to do it in the past." [00:12:58]
