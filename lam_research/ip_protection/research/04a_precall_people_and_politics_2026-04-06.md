## Lam Research Internal Political Landscape

### The Two Sides: CSBG vs. GIS

Pat provided the clearest picture yet of the internal organizational dynamics at Lam Research.

**CSBG (Customer Support Business Group)**
- Brad's business unit
- Has direct customer interaction and revenue responsibility
- Controls its own funding for projects
- The team BayOne is engaging with on the IP protection / NER-redaction opportunity
- Stakeholders in the room: Brad (EBI - Economic Buyer Influencer), Mikhail, Daniel

**GIS (Global Information Systems / IT-Engineering)**
- The IT and engineering function at Lam
- Has a new CIO who arrived in the last year
- The CIO came in after a failed digital transformation initiative under the prior CIO
- Is undergoing vendor rationalization with a select set of preferred partners
- BayOne is losing business on this side of the house

### The New CIO and Vendor Rationalization

Pat described the GIS situation directly:
- "The new CIO just came in last year and there was already a transition that happened because of a failure of a big technical initiative, which was the digital transformation."
- "He is coming with a few select partners that they worked on earlier."
- "That rationalization that is happening, we are obviously part of that change and we are taking a brunt on that."

Translation: BayOne had business with Lam's GIS/IT function. The old CIO left or was replaced after a failed digital transformation. The new CIO brought their own preferred vendor list. BayOne is not on that list and is actively losing GIS-side revenue.

### Brad Funding Directly, Bypassing GIS

This is the critical political dynamic. Pat stated explicitly:

> "Brad is actually funding all of that and he has called it out that 'I am funding for those guys.' So if they're not going to go with us, we'll directly get the funding from these guys."

Brad's CSBG unit is circumventing GIS entirely for AI and data initiatives. Instead of going through the IT organization (which would route through the new CIO and their preferred vendors), Brad is funding the work directly from his business unit's budget. This means:
- The engagement does not need GIS/CIO approval to proceed
- BayOne's proposal goes directly to the business buyer, not through an IT procurement process
- Brad is the economic decision-maker, not the CIO
- The scope and vendor selection is Brad's call

### The Shadow IT Problem

Colin identified the risk embedded in Brad's approach: "The CIO does not actually dislike day one or what we're doing. They dislike shadow IT. Which is hook, line and sinker what Brad's doing."

Colin's insight is that Brad's direct-funding model is textbook shadow IT -- a business unit spinning up technical capabilities outside the IT organization's governance. CIOs universally resist this, not because the projects are bad, but because they create ungoverned systems, security gaps, and duplicate infrastructure.

Colin's proposed resolution: "If we can get that out and say, look, we can still do this project, but we'll do it in a way that your IT department's not going to have their head explode when they see. Then that'll make both people happy and that'll be a good partner to them for a very long time."

This positions BayOne as the bridge -- delivering what Brad needs while building in enough IT governance that the CIO won't view it as rogue engineering. Colin called this out as a rare vendor capability: "Not a lot of vendors can do that."

## The Empowerment Strategy

Pat laid out a deliberate positioning approach for the meeting:

### The Pitch
Position BayOne as the technical capability that frees CSBG from dependence on GIS. Pat's framing:
- "Think of us as your technical empowerment."
- "You are currently depending on the GIS, which is the engineering side with the CIO and all. They're not getting along well. Hence they're actually trying to do this internally with this team."
- "We'll do it for you. You don't need to go to anyone else."
- "He should be confident that we can bring him value and results. He doesn't even need to go to the technical team and he can create either contracting, subcontracting or even direct."

### The Long-Term Frame
Pat was explicit that this is not about one project: "The goal is the long-term setup with them. The goal is not to just get this small rate. 2026, 2027, 2028, that kind of a partnership."

He also framed what Brad is evaluating: "Brad has multiple times told me there is enough work and opportunity. All he wants to see is who is really capable of taking that work up for him." This means the IP protection POC is an audition. Success here opens additional work across Brad's portfolio.

### Where Colin Added Nuance
Colin accepted the empowerment positioning but added the IT reconciliation layer. His version: give them power to operate independently AND provide a path that keeps IT comfortable. This is the differentiator -- other vendors might help Brad bypass IT, but only a partner who can bridge both sides has long-term staying power.

Colin also added the commercial framing for why low technical capability on Brad's side benefits BayOne: "If he's not very good, then that kind of leaves it open season for us. Once we have a grasp of what kind of value this brings to them, we have now an open season to say, here's how much this costs. And by the way, you can't even tell me I'm wrong because you don't even know enough to tell me."
