04 Proof of Concept Target

Understanding the Selected Application

Once a target application is identified, we need enough context to scope the proof of concept accurately.

Purpose and users What the application does, who uses it, and where it sits in the troubleshooting workflow
Data sources What feeds into it: documents, databases, APIs, user-entered text, transcripts, images, or other inputs
Sensitive content What customer-identifiable information looks like in this context: customer names, abbreviations, identifiers, and their known spelling variations
Volume and scale How much content flows through the application and how frequently
Technical environment Whether the application is on Azure, on-premises, or hybrid, and what services and infrastructure support it
Existing architecture How data enters the application, how it is stored, how it is served to users, and any existing pipelines or middleware
Representative Data Needs

To validate any approach, we need examples of the application's actual content: content that should be flagged (documents or entries containing customer-identifiable information) and content that is clean (entries that should pass through without flagging). If security concerns prevent sharing actual content, sanitized examples that preserve the structure and patterns of real data are also valuable.
