## Objectives (Priority Order)

### 1. Build Rapport with Daniel
First time meeting the technical gatekeeper. Everything downstream depends on him trusting us technically. Speak engineer to engineer. Do not oversell.

### 2. Extract the Entry Point Application
The blocker since the first meeting. "Name one application" is the single most important outcome. Frame it for a room with Brad: "To scope something meaningful for you, we need to anchor on one specific system. What would give you the most value if we solved it first?"

### 3. Understand What Was Actually Tried Technically
Daniel knows the details Mikhail could not provide. The Transformers/SpaCy/Azure AI models, the 20%-to-17% journey, the labeling exercise. Get the real story.

### 4. Gauge Competition
Are they talking to anyone else? Do not ask directly. Listen for: urgency level, how much they ask "what are others doing?", references to other conversations.

### 5. Gauge Their Perception of Difficulty
If they think this is nearly impossible, pricing leverage is high. If they think it is easy, positioning changes. Daniel's reaction to our questions will reveal this.

## Recommended Meeting Flow

### Opening (5-10 min): Bridge from Last Meeting

"Last time we met, you confirmed we understood the problem correctly. Today we would love to go deeper on the technical side so we can come back with something concrete and specific to your environment."

Short. Establishes continuity without re-presenting. Signals we are here to listen, not pitch.

---

### Block 1: Prior Work Autopsy (20-25 min)
*Target: Daniel. Brad and Mikhail benefit from hearing it.*

| Question | Why We Need It |
|----------|---------------|
| "Can you walk us through what was tried technically? We heard Transformers, SpaCy, and an Azure AI model. What was the setup?" | Understand if the approach was fundamentally wrong or just poorly executed |
| "Who did the implementation? Internal team, a partner, or both?" | Assess skill level applied. Do not name Accenture. |
| "What was the training data? How was the test set constructed?" | Determine if there was a proper benchmark |
| "When you hit 20% false positives, what did a typical false positive look like?" | Pattern recognition issue vs. model architecture issue |
| "The fine-tuning moved it from 20% to 17%. What specifically changed in that iteration?" | Confirms whether fine-tuning was meaningful or cosmetic |
| "Was there a formal testing methodology? How were false positives calculated?" | Validates whether the 20% number is even measured correctly |

**If Daniel defers or Brad redirects:** Pivot gracefully. "Totally understand. The reason I ask is that it helps us avoid proposing something that has already been tried and ruled out."

---

### Block 2: System Specifics and Entry Point (20-25 min)
*Target: Mikhail and Daniel together. This is where we extract the application.*

| Question | Why We Need It |
|----------|---------------|
| "You mentioned six search systems and multiple entry points. If we were to prove this works on one system first, which one would be the highest value to start with?" | THE question. Everything is gated by this. |
| "For that system, how does data get in? What does a typical document or entry look like?" | Scoping the ingestion pipeline |
| "What does the current restriction mechanism look like in that system today?" | Understanding the baseline we are improving on |
| "How many documents or records are we talking about in that system?" | Scale estimation for costing |
| "Is the data in that system primarily text, or are there images, drawings, attachments?" | Determines complexity of the detection approach |
| "Is this system on Azure, on-prem, or hybrid?" | Architecture planning |

**If they remain general:** "Even pointing us to the system that gave you the most pain when you tried the ML approach would be a great starting point."

---

### Block 3: Success Criteria (10-15 min)
*Target: Brad for business criteria, Daniel for technical criteria.*

| Question | Why We Need It |
|----------|---------------|
| "Last time, 97% accuracy in under 5 seconds came up. Is that the real target, or is there flexibility?" | Calibrate expectations |
| "For the detection use case, what does 'good enough' look like to start opening things up?" | Understand the minimum viable threshold |
| "What does your team consider a successful pilot? What would make you say yes, let us scale this?" | Define the finish line before we start |
| "Is there a timeline you are working toward? Anything this is blocking?" | Urgency gauge and pricing leverage |

---

### Block 4: Light Teaser (5-10 min, only if tone allows)
*Only if the room is warm and Brad has not tightened up.*

Keep it brief, one or two sentences max:

"Based on what we have seen in similar environments, we believe a hybrid approach combining deterministic matching for known patterns with AI-based classification for contextual sensitivity is the right direction. We would love to come back with specifics once we understand the first target system."

Do NOT go further. Do not mention unified control plane. Do not mention ingestion-first architecture. Do not present slides or diagrams. Save the detailed approach for the written deliverable or a dedicated follow-up.

If they ask for more detail: "Happy to go deeper on that, but I want to make sure we get the information we need from your side first so the approach is tailored to your actual environment, not generic."

---

### Closing (5 min)

- Summarize what we heard (demonstrates listening, reinforces credibility)
- Confirm next steps: "We will come back with a concrete, scoped proposal based on [whatever system they named]."
- Ask: "What else should we understand before we put that together?"
- Ask: "What is the best way to get the representative samples we will need?"
