## About Zeblok

Zeblok is a small company (~20 people) founded in 2019, headquartered at Stony Brook University's tech incubator. Their flagship product, Ai-MicroCloud, is an AI infrastructure platform designed to deploy complete AI/ML environments on-premises, at the edge, or in hybrid configurations.

**Mouli's background:** ~20 years on Wall Street, former Chief Architect at JP Morgan where he built their 10,000-CPU computing platform. Credible technical founder with deep infrastructure experience.

**Key claims:**
- "Mini Azure" that can be deployed anywhere, including fully air-gapped environments
- 75-80% GPU reduction through memory optimization (early benchmarks, not independently verified)
- First to implement post-quantum cryptography for hub-to-edge deployments
- Defense relationships including access to senior military leadership and a TS-cleared advisor

---

## Where the Value Is

### Strong Fit: Defense

Air-gapped, on-prem deployment is a genuine requirement for defense workloads. Zeblok's architecture aligns with ITAR, CMMC, and classified environment needs. Their claimed defense relationships are promising, though not yet validated through delivered contracts.

**Our advantage:** Colin's defense background (prior clearance, AGL/laser systems experience) and BayOne's US Persons talent engine position us well for this vertical.

### Moderate Fit: Manufacturing / High-Reliability Systems

Local model hosting makes sense when you need to fix a model version in place—no automatic updates, deterministic behavior. This applies to manufacturing, engineering systems, and similar environments where reliability trumps having the latest model.

### Limited Fit: Retail, Education, General Enterprise

For these verticals, on-prem infrastructure is not a compelling value proposition. Customers prefer hyperscalers. However, if Zeblok's agentic tools can operate independently of their full infrastructure stack, there may be opportunity here.

---

## Key Questions to Resolve

Before significant investment, we need clarity on:

1. **Lock-in:** Does using any part of Zeblok's platform require adopting the full Ai-MicroCloud stack? If yes, the addressable market is narrow. If tools can run standalone on existing customer infrastructure, the opportunity broadens considerably.

2. **Defense validation:** Relationships and connections are promising, but have they delivered on an actual defense contract? A named prime partnership or past performance reference would provide confidence.

3. **Competitive landscape:** Microsoft announced Azure Local with disconnected operations for Q3 2025, targeting the same air-gapped use case. Zeblok is not alone in this space.

## Recommendation

**Proceed with cautious optimism.**

This partnership offers genuine strategic value: it positions BayOne beyond staffing, opens a path into defense, and provides a technology differentiation story. The opportunity is real.

At the same time, the value is narrower than the pitch suggests. Air-gapped infrastructure matters for defense and specialized manufacturing, but most enterprise customers will stay on Azure/AWS. We should validate the lock-in question and defense credentials before committing significant resources.

**Suggested posture:** Colin evaluates the platform hands-on, we clarify the lock-in question in the follow-up call, and we pursue the GTC announcement as a low-commitment way to formalize the relationship while we validate the opportunity.
