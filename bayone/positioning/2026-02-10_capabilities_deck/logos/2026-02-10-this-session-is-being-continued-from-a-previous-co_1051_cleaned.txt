  For Big 4 style logo presentation, here are some approaches:

  1. Logo Wall / Grid
  Clean rows of logos, all same height, generous whitespace, no borders or
  cards. Just logos floating on the slide. McKinsey/BCG style.

  2. Industry Clusters
  Group logos by industry with a subtle label above each cluster. Logos
  smaller, tighter grouping. Shows breadth.

  3. Marquee Strip
  Single horizontal band of logos, evenly spaced. Very minimal. Often used
  in "Trusted By" sections.

  4. Tiered Display
  Larger logos for flagship clients (Cisco, Google, Salesforce), smaller
  logos for the rest. Creates visual hierarchy.

  5. Monochrome Treatment
  All logos converted to grayscale or single brand color (purple tint).
  Creates unity but loses brand recognition. More abstract/artistic.

  Given this is for a Cisco VP and we want to show client breadth across
  industries, I'd lean toward Option 1 (Logo Wall) or Option 2 (Industry 
  Clusters) вҖ” both are clean, professional, and let the logos speak for
  themselves.
