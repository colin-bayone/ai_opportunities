## #MakeTechPurple

### What It Is
BayOne's initiative launched in 2019 to close the gender gap in the tech workforce. The name comes from combining red (representing women in tech) with blue (representing the historically male-dominated industry influence), creating purple, which also symbolizes power, wisdom, and creativity.

### Key Facts
- BayOne increased its percentage of women hires from 26% to 46% in four years
- The initiative uses equitable hiring practices, the "Rooney Rule" (interviewing at least two women for every open position), and an AI-powered platform to source diverse candidates
- Programs include pro-bono technical training cohorts of 10-15 women in AI, Software Engineering, Data, and Cloud Engineering
- Neha is the Ambassador of the initiative
- Colin's AI team being 80% female is a tangible example of #MakeTechPurple in action
