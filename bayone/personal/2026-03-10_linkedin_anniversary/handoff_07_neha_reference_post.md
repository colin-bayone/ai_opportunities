## What to Take From Neha's Post

General DNA only:
- Personal narrative about a career transition
- Gratitude toward colleagues and leadership
- Forward-looking energy about what's next
- Individual shoutouts by name
- A personal/family shoutout at the end (her mom, Colin's wife Alli)
- Tie-in to #MakeTechPurple

## What NOT to Take From Neha's Post

- Her specific structure and flow
- Her tone (she uses em dashes extensively, inspirational pivot language, fragment sentences like "It's a mindset. It's how we grow." which are exactly the patterns Colin hates)
- Her level of corporate polish
- The "Through it all, one thing has remained constant" type of construction
- The LinkedIn default "I'm happy to share that I'm starting a new position" header

Colin's post needs to sound like Colin, not like Neha. Colin writes with complete sentences, no em dashes, no colons, no fragments, no rhetorical questions, no inspirational pivots. His voice is warmer and more direct, with dry humor and self-awareness.
