## Idea 13: Slide and Content Governance

**Ownership:** Slides and client-facing content are co-owned between two organizations:
- Rachel's organization (Director of Marketing) - brand, design, style
- Colin's organization (Director of AI) - technical accuracy, AI content

**Rules:**
- Slides must exist first in the curated library. If they don't exist, they must be requested at least one week in advance. No exceptions.
- Any slides that don't come from these two organizations should not be used for clients. Simple as that.
- People are free to customize at their own discretion, but neither organization will be in the business of customizing slides for individual salespeople
- Known decks will be maintained and kept current (the Singularity approach for on-demand generation from real data)
- No kitchen-sink decks. Materials are focused, scoped, and tailored to the specific meeting

**What the AI team offers:**
- Maintained, curated decks based on real capabilities and real project history
- On-demand generation of tailored slides via Singularity (grounded in actual data)
- Will NOT build custom one-off decks for every meeting
- Will NOT participate in kitchen-sink meetings where the plan is to throw everything at the wall

## Idea 14: Supply Qualification and Strategic Capacity Planning

### Supply Qualification (Part of Every Opportunity Assessment)
Even if the client is real and has money, the following must be answered:
- Can we actually deliver this?
- What else do we have going on right now that might prevent us from delivering?
- What's the timeline look like?
- Do we have the people with the right skills?
- Should we even take this on if we don't have the team to scale?

### Strategic Capacity Planning (Beyond Individual Opportunities)
Sales cannot keep piling on opportunities without a plan to grow the team. This must be addressed strategically, not reactively.

**The capacity decision framework (four categories):**
1. **Build internally:** What makes sense for BayOne to add as internal capability and expertise? These are core competencies that will be used repeatedly and should be owned.
2. **Do immediately (partner/external):** What needs to be done right now that we don't have internal capacity for? Partner organizations are appropriate here (consistently proposed by Surej and Priya).
3. **Plan for longer term:** What should we plan on building internally over time? Roadmap items that aren't urgent but will become core.
4. **Permanently external:** What should always be done via partners and never brought in-house? Things that are one-off, highly specialized, or not core to BayOne's value.

**Key principle:** You can't just keep adding vendors to support, or you're recreating the same bottleneck problem externally. There must be a line drawn between internal vs. external capability.

**Proactive, not reactive:** If we plan this out strategically as a roadmap, it makes sense. We already have enough pipeline visibility to start doing this. We don't need to know every future opportunity to plan capacity. We know enough right now.

## Idea 15: Sales Leadership Sign-Off Gate

**What:** A formal person on the sales side must sign off on an opportunity before:
- It reaches the technical team
- The technical team is asked to come in for a meeting

**This is not optional.** It must be part of the process and done formally. Individual salespeople cannot have a direct line to the technical team without their sales leadership reviewing and approving first.

**Purpose:** Filters unqualified, overstated, or premature opportunities before they consume technical resources. Puts accountability on sales leadership to verify what their team is bringing forward.

## Idea 16: Quality Gate for Client-Facing Materials (Dual Gate)

### Gate 1: Brand and Style (Rachel / Marketing)
- All client-facing materials must pass through Rachel's marketing team for brand guideline compliance
- Minimum 5 business days notice for review
- Covers visual design, formatting, branding, professional presentation standards

### Gate 2: Technical Accuracy (Colin / AI Team)
- All AI-related client-facing materials must pass through Colin's team for technical accuracy
- This includes PowerPoint, case studies, capability claims, and any other materials that reference AI
- AI tools can assist with this review to prevent bottlenecking

**Scope:** If it touches AI and it's going to a client, it must pass through both gates before it goes out the door.

## Idea 17: Role Differentiation for Meeting Involvement (Three Tiers)

### Tier 1: Sales Handles Alone
- Initial client conversations and relationship building
- Exploratory discussions before qualification
- Lead identification and early-stage nurturing
- Any meeting where the client hasn't shared a specific problem or interest area

### Tier 2: Pre-Sales Engineer / Technical Sales (Not Director of AI)
- Qualified opportunities that need technical explanation but not strategic direction
- Meetings where the client has a defined problem and needs to understand how BayOne could approach it
- Technical Q&A that doesn't require senior leadership or solution architecture

### Tier 3: Director of AI
- Strategic scoping and solution architecture
- High-value, complex opportunities that require senior technical leadership
- Discovery sessions where the client has a confirmed problem statement, budget, and decision maker
- Engagements where the scope and approach need to be designed

### Baseline Expectation
- Each tier has clear criteria. Meetings should be staffed at the appropriate level.
- Stop pulling leadership into meetings where they don't belong. There is no reason the CEO should be at a meeting with a manager-level client. It looks bad and wastes senior time.
- The framework should explicitly map meeting types to the right tier.

## Idea 18: Post-Engagement Feedback Loop (Continuous, Not Just at Completion)

**What:** After projects are delivered (or milestones reached), the experience feeds back into the sales enablement system.

**Mechanics:**
- Completed projects and significant milestones become new case studies in the portfolio
- The capabilities deck is expanded accordingly
- This is the responsibility of the Solutions team, not sales

**Timing:** This doesn't have to wait until the entire project is delivered. It can and should happen ongoing as milestones are reached. A project that's 60% complete with strong results so far can already feed into the portfolio.

**Why this matters:** This is what creates the flywheel from Idea 9 (Venn Diagram). Each delivered project makes the next related one easier to qualify, faster to deliver, and more credible to sell. The feedback loop is what turns individual projects into compounding organizational capability.
