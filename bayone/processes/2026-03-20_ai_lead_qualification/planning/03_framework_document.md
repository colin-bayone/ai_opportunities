# AI Solutions Opportunity Management Framework

## BayOne Solutions | March 2026

---

## 01 Purpose and Scope

This framework establishes the standards, processes, and accountability structures for how BayOne Solutions identifies, qualifies, prioritizes, and staffs AI solutions opportunities. It applies to all client-facing AI engagements from initial lead identification through project delivery and post-engagement review.

The framework addresses the full lifecycle of an AI solutions opportunity:

- How leads enter the pipeline and what information must accompany them
- How opportunities are qualified before technical resources are engaged
- How meetings are scheduled, scoped, and staffed
- How client-facing materials are created, reviewed, and approved
- How capacity is managed and scaled to meet demand
- How performance is measured to ensure continuous improvement

This document is intended for leadership, sales, solutions, and delivery teams. It is a living document and will be updated as the organization matures its AI practice.

---

## 02 Guiding Principles

The following principles underpin every element of this framework.

**Problem-first engagement.** Successful AI engagements start with understanding the client's problem, not presenting capabilities. The conversation should begin with what the client needs, not what BayOne offers. Every meeting should be structured so the client is sharing more than BayOne is presenting.

**Honesty about capability.** BayOne is building its AI practice and should own that transparently. The organization will not represent capabilities, case studies, or products that do not exist. All claims to clients must be grounded in verified, completed work.

**Qualification before involvement.** Technical resources are the closing mechanism, not the opening mechanism. Their involvement begins after a lead has been qualified, not before. Pulling technical resources into unqualified conversations wastes capacity that should be directed toward real opportunities.

**Discipline over speed.** A well-prepared meeting with the right people will always outperform a hastily assembled one. It is better to reschedule than to walk in unprepared. Process discipline protects both BayOne's reputation and the client's time.

**Fewer, better opportunities.** Pursuing a smaller number of well-qualified opportunities and delivering exceptional results will generate more long-term revenue than chasing every possible lead. Quality of pipeline matters more than volume of pipeline.

## 03 Systems and Infrastructure

### 03.1 Single Source of Truth: Salesforce

All opportunity information must be tracked in Salesforce. This is non-negotiable.

Salesforce is already deployed within BayOne and provides the capabilities needed for opportunity tracking, pipeline visibility, and reporting. The longer the organization operates outside of a formal CRM, the harder it becomes to establish the discipline required to scale the AI practice.

**What must be in Salesforce:**
- All active and prospective AI opportunities with current status
- Client contact information, role, and decision-making authority
- Notes from client interactions and meetings
- Qualification status and supporting documentation
- Internal team assignments and ownership
- Opportunity value estimates and priority classification

**What Salesforce enables:**
- Asynchronous visibility across sales, solutions, and leadership without requiring meetings or email chains
- Built-in reporting and dashboards for pipeline health, conversion rates, and capacity planning
- Accountability through auditable records of opportunity progression
- Elimination of duplicate tracking in emails, spreadsheets, and Teams chats

**Other tools feed into Salesforce, not the other way around.** Meeting planners, intake forms, and other supporting tools may serve as data entry points, but Salesforce remains the authoritative record. If it is not in Salesforce, it does not exist for the purposes of this framework.

### 03.2 Communication Protocol

Teams chats and email threads are appropriate for quick coordination and informal discussion. They are not appropriate for tracking opportunities, recording client interactions, or managing pipeline.

The current pattern of creating Teams channels with large distribution lists for visibility on individual opportunities must be replaced by Salesforce records that are visible to all relevant parties without requiring active notification.

Calls and meetings should be scheduled with purpose and should occur when there is sufficient information to make them productive. A call is not a substitute for doing the qualification work first. If the required information has not been gathered, there is no point in scheduling a call, even internally.

## 04 Lead Qualification Framework

No technical resource should be engaged on an opportunity until the following qualification criteria have been met. This is the responsibility of the sales team, with sign-off from sales leadership.

### 04.1 Minimum Information Requirements

Before an opportunity is escalated beyond the sales team, the following must be documented in Salesforce:

- **Client identity:** Company name, industry, and relevant context about the organization
- **Contact details:** Name, title, role, and LinkedIn profile (at minimum) of the primary client contact
- **Problem statement or area of interest:** What is the client looking to solve, explore, or improve? This must be specific. "Interested in AI" is not a problem statement.
- **Meeting history:** Summary of all conversations held with the client to date, including what was discussed and what was shared
- **Source of the opportunity:** How was this lead identified? Referral, existing relationship, inbound inquiry, outbound outreach, or other
- **Supporting documentation:** Any relevant materials (emails, meeting notes, client-provided documents) uploaded to the Salesforce record

### 04.2 Decision-Maker Qualification

The sales team must determine whether the client contact has decision-making authority sufficient to fund and approve work with BayOne.

Key questions:
- Can this person authorize a contract or project engagement?
- Do they control or influence the budget that would fund this work?
- Are they at a seniority level where they can make strategic platform or vendor decisions?

A mid-level manager expressing interest is not equivalent to a decision-maker with budget authority. If the primary contact lacks authority, the sales team must identify who does have that authority and whether they are engaged in the conversation.

### 04.3 Financial Qualification and Opportunity Sizing

Interest alone does not justify technical involvement. There must be a tangible revenue opportunity.

- Is there an actual project or initiative that could result in a paid engagement?
- What is the estimated value of this opportunity to BayOne (per quarter or per engagement)?
- Does the potential revenue justify the investment of time and resources?

Opportunities below a defined minimum value threshold should be handled by the sales team without escalation to technical resources unless there is a compelling strategic justification (see 04.4).

### 04.4 Priority Assessment

Priority is determined by a combination of financial value, strategic alignment, and delivery feasibility. It is not determined solely by the enthusiasm of the salesperson or the seniority of the client contact.

**Financial value:** Larger opportunities take priority over smaller ones, all else being equal.

**Strategic value with guardrails:** Some lower-value work creates legitimate inroads at a client that lead to larger engagements. This is valid only when:
- There is tangible, verifiable evidence of broader opportunity (not speculation)
- A decision-maker with authority to expand the relationship is part of the conversation
- The strategic path from the current opportunity to the larger one is articulable and credible

Strategic value is not a blank check. "This could lead to an MSA" or "there might be more work later" does not qualify as strategic justification unless backed by specific, documented evidence.

**Delivery feasibility:** Priority must account for current team capacity and competing obligations. A high-value opportunity that the team cannot deliver on is not a viable priority. (See Section 08 for capacity planning.)

### 04.5 Supply Qualification

Demand qualification (is this a real opportunity?) must be paired with supply qualification (can we deliver?).

Before committing to an opportunity, the following must be assessed:
- Does the team have the skills and experience to deliver this work?
- What is the current workload, and can this be absorbed without impacting existing commitments?
- What is the expected timeline, and is it realistic given current capacity?
- If additional resources are needed, where will they come from and how quickly can they be onboarded?

An opportunity that passes demand qualification but fails supply qualification should not be committed to. It should be flagged for the steering committee to assess whether capacity can be built or whether the opportunity should be deferred.

### 04.6 Capability Overlap Assessment

Opportunities that overlap significantly with work BayOne has already completed carry a lower qualification threshold because they require less effort and deliver higher confidence in execution.

**High overlap:** The opportunity closely resembles a completed project in terms of technology, industry context, or solution type. The team can move quickly and confidently. Lower qualification bar.

**Moderate overlap:** Some elements are familiar, but meaningful new work is required. Standard qualification bar.

**No overlap:** This is entirely new territory. Higher qualification bar. Requires strong financial or strategic justification to pursue.

**The overlap assessment must be based on a curated solutions portfolio of verified, completed work.** Staffing engagements, where BayOne placed an individual into a predefined role managed by the client, are fundamentally different from solutions engagements, where BayOne designed, built, and delivered a solution. These two engagement types must not be conflated when assessing capability overlap.

### 04.7 Sales Leadership Sign-Off

A designated sales leader must formally sign off on an opportunity before it is escalated to the technical team or before the technical team is asked to participate in any client meeting.

This sign-off confirms that:
- All minimum information requirements (04.1) have been met
- Decision-maker qualification (04.2) has been verified
- Financial qualification (04.3) has been assessed
- The opportunity record in Salesforce is complete and current

Individual salespeople do not have a direct escalation path to the technical team. All escalations route through the designated sales leader, who is accountable for the quality and completeness of what is brought forward.

## 05 Roles and Responsibilities

### 05.1 Sales Team

The sales team owns the first four stages of the opportunity lifecycle: identification, qualification, initial conversation, and handoff to the technical team.

**Responsibilities:**
- Identify and source leads through all available channels
- Qualify leads by completing all requirements in Section 04
- Conduct initial conversations with clients to understand their needs, without making promises or commitments about what BayOne will present, deliver, or demonstrate
- Document all client interactions and opportunity details in Salesforce
- Record outcomes of all meetings, including internal pre-meetings and post-meeting debriefs
- Maintain the Salesforce record as the opportunity progresses

**Boundaries:**
- Sales does not scope solutions, propose timelines, or recommend engagement structures (such as pilot durations or team compositions). That is the responsibility of the solutions team after qualification.
- Sales does not commit the technical team's time, deliverables, or participation without prior approval from the technical team.
- Sales does not represent capabilities, case studies, or products that have not been verified and approved by the solutions team.

If a client asks about technical capabilities, demos, or specific deliverables, the appropriate response is: "Let me check with our technical team and we will get back to you." No promises are made on the spot.

### 05.2 Sales Leadership

The designated sales leader serves as the quality gate between the sales team and the technical team.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and sign off on opportunities before they are escalated to the technical team
- Verify that qualification criteria have been met and that the Salesforce record is complete
- Filter premature, overstated, or unqualified opportunities before they consume technical resources
- Ensure that the sales team is following the framework and maintaining documentation standards

### 05.3 Pre-Sales / Technical Sales

For opportunities that have been qualified but do not require senior technical leadership, a pre-sales engineer or technical sales resource should be the first point of technical engagement.

**Appropriate for:**
- Qualified opportunities that need technical explanation but not strategic direction
- Meetings where the client has a defined problem and wants to understand approach options
- Technical questions and answers that do not require solution architecture

This tier exists to prevent every AI-adjacent conversation from escalating to the most senior technical resource. Not all technical meetings require the same level of involvement.

### 05.4 Director of AI / Solutions Team

The Director of AI and the solutions team engage on opportunities that have been fully qualified and require strategic scoping, solution architecture, or senior technical leadership.

**Appropriate for:**
- High-value, complex opportunities requiring solution design
- Discovery sessions with confirmed problem statements, budgets, and decision-makers
- Engagements where the approach and scope need to be architected

**The solutions team also owns:**
- Technical quality review of all AI-related client-facing materials (see Section 07)
- Maintenance of the curated solutions portfolio and capability documentation
- Post-engagement case study development and portfolio updates (see Section 10)

### 05.5 Meeting Staffing Guidelines

Meetings should be staffed at the appropriate level. The seniority of BayOne attendees should correspond to the seniority and nature of the client engagement.

| Meeting Type | BayOne Attendees |
|---|---|
| Initial exploration, no qualified problem | Sales team only |
| Qualified opportunity, technical Q&A needed | Sales + pre-sales/technical sales |
| Qualified opportunity, solution scoping | Sales + Director of AI / solutions lead |
| Executive-level strategic discussion | Sales leadership + Director of AI + executive sponsor as appropriate |

Leadership should not be pulled into meetings where their presence is not warranted by the seniority of the client stakeholders or the strategic importance of the engagement. Matching attendee levels to client levels is essential for professional credibility.

## 06 Meeting Standards

### 06.1 Scheduling Discipline

- Meetings with clients that require technical team participation must be scheduled with a minimum of **72 hours (weekday) advance notice** for the technical team to prepare.
- Meetings must be placed on the calendar at the time they are requested, not minutes before they occur.
- Internal availability must be confirmed before a meeting is proposed to a client.
- The opportunity must be qualified (per Section 04) before a meeting is scheduled.
- Scheduling a meeting does not override an existing commitment. If a new meeting conflicts with an existing one, the conflict must be resolved through a prioritization discussion, not by assuming the technical team will adjust.

### 06.2 Scope Lock

The scope of a meeting is defined at the time it is scheduled and recorded in Salesforce. This includes:
- The topic and objective of the meeting
- What BayOne will present or discuss
- What the client is expected to share
- Who from BayOne will attend and in what capacity

Once agreed upon internally, the meeting scope cannot be changed unilaterally. If the scope changes after internal alignment (for example, if new information from the client shifts the focus), the meeting must be reconvened or rescheduled with the revised scope agreed upon by all internal participants.

Sales cannot agree to a scope change with the client and then inform the technical team after the fact. That is a scope violation, and the technical team reserves the right to decline participation if the meeting no longer matches what was agreed upon.

### 06.3 Internal Pre-Meeting and Post-Meeting

**Pre-meeting:** Every client meeting involving technical resources must be preceded by an internal alignment meeting. This ensures all attendees understand the agenda, their role, and the desired outcome.

**Post-meeting:** A debrief should follow every client meeting to capture key takeaways, action items, and next steps. The sales team is responsible for documenting this in the Salesforce record. The solutions team can enable documentation through tools and templates, but the business ownership of recording client interactions belongs to sales.

## 07 Content and Materials Governance

### 07.1 Slide and Presentation Ownership

Client-facing slide decks and presentations are jointly owned and maintained by the Marketing organization and the AI Solutions organization.

- Marketing owns brand standards, visual design, and formatting compliance
- AI Solutions owns technical accuracy, capability claims, and content relevance

**Rules for client-facing slides:**
- All slides used in client meetings must come from the approved, curated library maintained by these two organizations
- If a required deck does not exist, it must be requested at least **one week in advance** of the meeting. No exceptions.
- Salespeople may customize approved decks at their own discretion for specific client contexts
- Neither Marketing nor AI Solutions will create custom one-off decks for individual meetings
- Decks that do not originate from the approved library should not be used in client settings

### 07.2 Quality Gates

All AI-related client-facing materials must pass through two review gates before distribution to clients:

**Gate 1: Brand and Style Review (Marketing)**
- Ensures compliance with BayOne brand guidelines, formatting standards, and professional presentation quality
- Minimum **5 business days** notice for review
- Covers all visual and editorial standards

**Gate 2: Technical Accuracy Review (AI Solutions)**
- Ensures all technical claims, capability statements, and case study references are accurate and verifiable
- Applies to slide decks, case studies, proposals, capability summaries, and any document that references AI solutions
- AI-assisted review tools may be used to streamline this process and prevent bottlenecking

Materials that have not passed both gates are not approved for client distribution.

### 07.3 Case Studies and Capability Claims

All case studies must be based on verified, completed work. The following are explicitly prohibited:
- Case studies generated by AI tools (such as ChatGPT or similar) without grounding in actual project work
- Case studies based on staffing engagements reframed as solutions engagements
- Capability claims for products, demos, or accelerators that do not exist
- Metrics, statistics, or performance claims that are not derived from actual project outcomes

If a client asks about capabilities that BayOne does not currently have, the honest response is to acknowledge where the organization currently stands and redirect the conversation to where BayOne can genuinely add value.

## 08 Capacity Planning and Strategic Scaling

### 08.1 Capacity as a Qualification Factor

Every opportunity assessment must include a supply qualification check (Section 04.5). Opportunities that pass demand qualification but exceed current delivery capacity should be flagged, not committed to. Overcommitting degrades delivery quality across all engagements and damages client relationships.

### 08.2 Strategic Scaling Decisions

As the AI practice grows, the organization must make deliberate decisions about how to build capacity. These decisions fall into four categories:

**Build internally:** Capabilities that are core to BayOne's AI practice and will be used repeatedly across engagements should be developed in-house. These represent long-term investments in organizational competency.

**Engage partners for immediate needs:** When a qualified opportunity requires skills or capacity the internal team does not currently have, partner organizations can fill the gap. This is appropriate for near-term delivery needs.

**Plan for longer-term internal development:** Some capabilities are not immediately needed but will become core over time. These should be placed on a hiring and development roadmap.

**Maintain as permanently external:** Some capabilities are highly specialized, one-off, or outside BayOne's strategic focus. These should always be sourced externally through partners.

Adding partner capacity without limit recreates the bottleneck problem externally. The organization must draw a clear line between what should be built internally and what should remain external.

### 08.3 Proactive Capacity Roadmap

Capacity planning should be proactive. The current pipeline provides sufficient visibility to begin strategic workforce planning. Waiting for every future opportunity to be confirmed before planning capacity results in chronic reactive hiring, which perpetuates the bottleneck.

The steering committee (Section 09) is responsible for maintaining the capacity roadmap and ensuring that hiring and partner decisions align with pipeline priorities.

## 09 Governance Structure

### 09.1 Steering Committee

A steering committee must be established to provide strategic oversight of the AI solutions pipeline, resource allocation, and priority decisions.

**Composition:** No more than five members, including:
- Committee lead (recommended: CTO, who holds organizational authority over both technical delivery and strategic direction)
- Director of AI / Solutions lead
- VP of Sales or designated sales leadership representative
- One additional leadership representative as needed
- One additional delivery or operations representative as needed

**Cadence:** Weekly or biweekly, at the discretion of the committee lead.

**Agenda:**
- Review current project queue and delivery status
- Review new opportunities that have entered the pipeline
- Prioritize opportunities and assign them to the appropriate delivery track
- Assess team capacity and resource allocation against current and projected demand
- Make go/no-go decisions on opportunities that require capacity trade-offs
- Review and update the capacity roadmap (Section 08.3)

### 09.2 Weekly Operational Stand-Up

A weekly stand-up meeting brings together representatives from sales, solutions, and delivery to coordinate near-term activity.

**Purpose:**
- Review what is coming down the pipeline for the current and upcoming week
- Address items that need immediate attention or reprioritization
- Identify conflicts, blockers, or resource gaps before they become emergencies
- Ensure all teams have visibility into what is happening across the organization

This meeting should be brief and operational. It is not a strategy session. Strategic decisions are made in the steering committee.

## 10 Sales Enablement and Training

### 10.1 AI Literacy Requirement

Salespeople who are expected to identify and qualify AI opportunities must complete a structured internal training program before they are authorized to represent AI solutions to clients.

This training should cover:
- Foundational AI concepts and terminology relevant to BayOne's service areas
- BayOne's current AI solutions portfolio, including what the organization can and cannot do
- The distinction between solutions engagements and staffing engagements
- How to identify AI-relevant opportunities vs. general technology requests
- The lead qualification process defined in this framework
- Common pitfalls and misconceptions that damage client trust

### 10.2 Evaluation and Verification

Training must include a formal evaluation to verify that participants have absorbed the material and can apply it in practice. The evaluation should be:
- Objective, with clear and consistent criteria applied to all participants
- Practical, focused on whether the salesperson can perform qualification effectively
- Fair, with no room for subjective judgment or the appearance of favoritism

The specific format of the evaluation will be determined in collaboration with the Learning and Development organization. The key requirement is that completion of training must be verifiable, not assumed.

### 10.3 Ongoing Enablement

AI knowledge does not remain static. The solutions team will provide regular updates to the sales organization:
- Periodic briefings on active projects, completed milestones, and new capabilities
- Updated talking points that salespeople can use in client conversations
- Refreshed capability documentation as the solutions portfolio evolves

These updates replace the current pattern where individual salespeople contact the solutions team ad hoc for background information. A regular cadence of proactive updates reduces the volume of reactive requests.

## 11 Performance Measurement

### 11.1 Outcome-Based KPIs

Sales performance related to AI solutions should be measured on outcomes, not activity volume.

**Metrics that matter:**
- Revenue generated from qualified, delivered AI engagements
- Conversion rate from lead to qualified opportunity (measures qualification quality)
- Conversion rate from qualified opportunity to delivered engagement
- Growth within existing accounts (expansion of scope, follow-on projects)
- Client satisfaction and engagement health

**Metrics that should not be the primary measure:**
- Number of meetings held
- Number of leads submitted (without regard to quality)
- Number of MSAs signed (without associated project revenue)

If performance metrics reward volume (meetings, MSAs, accounts claimed), the incentive structure will continue to drive the behaviors this framework is designed to correct. Metrics must align with the framework's emphasis on qualification quality, delivery success, and sustainable growth.

## 12 Post-Engagement Feedback Loop

### 12.1 Portfolio and Capability Updates

After each delivered project, or upon reaching significant milestones within an active project, the solutions team will:
- Develop a case study based on verified outcomes and deliverables
- Update the curated solutions portfolio to reflect the new capability
- Expand the capabilities deck used by the sales team
- Assess how the completed work creates overlap with potential future opportunities (per Section 04.6)

This is the responsibility of the solutions team and occurs as a standard part of the engagement lifecycle, not as an ad hoc request.

### 12.2 Continuous Improvement

The feedback loop does not wait for project completion. Milestones reached during active engagements can and should feed back into the portfolio. A project that is 60% complete with demonstrated results is already a credible reference point.

This continuous loop is what transforms individual projects into compounding organizational capability. Each completed engagement makes the next related one easier to qualify, faster to deliver, and more credible to sell.

## 13 Enforcement and Adoption

### 13.1 Leadership Endorsement

This framework requires formal endorsement from organizational leadership. The policies defined here, particularly around commitment authority (Section 05.1), content governance (Section 07), and qualification gates (Section 04), must be established as organizational standards, not individual preferences.

### 13.2 Process-Based Enforcement

The AI solutions team will support all requests that follow the framework. Specifically:
- Slides and presentation materials will be provided for meetings that have been qualified and scheduled per the framework
- Technical resources will participate in meetings that meet the qualification, scheduling, and scope requirements defined above
- Case studies and capability materials will be generated from the curated portfolio and made available through the approved process

Requests that have not followed the framework will be redirected to the appropriate step in the process. This is not punitive. The framework is designed to produce better outcomes for all parties. Following the process results in better-prepared meetings, more relevant materials, higher client satisfaction, and stronger close rates.

### 13.3 Adoption Timeline

Behavioral change takes time. The organization should expect a transition period of three to four months before the framework is fully embedded in daily operations. During this period:
- Training and enablement activities will prepare the team
- The framework will be introduced incrementally, starting with the highest-impact elements
- Feedback will be collected and incorporated to refine the process
- Consistent reinforcement from leadership is essential to establishing the new standard

The goal is to make following the framework easier than working around it. When the process is supported by the right tools, the right training, and the right incentives, adoption becomes natural.
