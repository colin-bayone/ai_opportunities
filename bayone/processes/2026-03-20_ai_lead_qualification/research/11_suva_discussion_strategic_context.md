# Strategic Discussion: Colin & Suva (VP of L&D) - Mar 2026

## Context
This was a working conversation between Colin and Suva, BayOne's VP of Learning and Development, about the systemic problems with the sales process and how to address them. Suva is an ally who understands the operational gaps and has been pushing for the same things Colin has.

---

## Key Takeaways

### 1. Suva Independently Validates Every Core Problem
Suva confirms the qualification problem from an organizational perspective, not just Colin's frustration. He uses the framing of "demand qualification AND supply qualification" which is more structured than how it's been discussed so far.

- **Demand qualification:** Does the client actually want this? Is it real?
- **Supply qualification:** Can we actually deliver this? Do we have the people, the capabilities, the bandwidth?

Both must be true before proceeding. This is a critical addition to the framework.

### 2. The Sales Workshop Will Not Fix This
A sales workshop is scheduled (facilitated by someone named Anand), and both Colin and Suva are skeptical. Key observations:

- The workshop will likely focus on the sales funnel (getting clients from "maybe" to "yes")
- The facilitator **explicitly rejected supply qualification as his problem.** His position: even large SIs sign up for anything and everything, and the delivery team figures it out afterward
- Colin and Suva's role at the workshop is less about learning and more about "reminding people about guardrails"
- Suva estimates the workshop will address maybe 20% of the real problems at best

### 3. Suva Volunteers to Lead Framework Creation
Because Colin is overloaded with delivery and Amit is busy on the delivery side, Suva offered to take the lead on creating the "rules of engagement" document. His vision:

- Specific questions that must be answered before escalation
- A staged gate system ("only if the answer is clear and crisp on all 10 is when you go to stage two")
- Clear rule: "I don't even show up on a client call until you have the answer for all these 10 questions in advance"
- Needs to be backed by specific examples (good and bad), not generic guidance

### 4. Colin's Chatbot Idea
Colin proposed putting the framework into a chatbot/intake tool for the sales team. Purpose:

- "Call BS without wasting our time on meetings"
- Prevent the behavior where sales gets you on a call and then everything becomes a "yes"
- Force written documentation before escalation
- Could be ready as early as the following week

Suva was cautiously supportive but questioned whether a chatbot is needed if the document itself is clear and under 10-15 pages. His view: a bot helps if there are many subjective qualification questions that can't be inferred from a generic document.

## New Patterns and Dynamics Revealed

### Pattern 12: The Phone Trap
Colin's observation: "The moment anyone on sales gets you on the phone, you're done. Doesn't matter what you say on that call. They will take everything as a yes."

Example given: Han got Colin on a call he shouldn't have been on, then messaged him afterward asking "where's my content?" Sales treated his presence on the call as agreement to own deliverables.

**Implication for framework:** The qualification gate must happen BEFORE any call is scheduled. Once the meeting is booked, the damage is already done.

### Pattern 13: Teams Channel Pressure Tactics
Colin described getting 10-15 new Teams channels per week with opportunity names. Sales adds leadership names (Surej, etc.) to the channel to create social pressure for Colin to participate.

"Almost all complete waste, and people want to get on a call and kind of force it by who else is on the channel."

**Implication for framework:** Teams channels are not qualification. The framework should specify that creating a channel and adding names does not constitute bringing a qualified opportunity.

### Pattern 14: Misclassifying Non-AI Work as AI
Example: Someone brought a "big opportunity with a client for AI dashboarding." Turned out to be building a SharePoint site for an internal team. No AI involved at all.

Suva's nuance: Sometimes a client's budget does come through an AI initiative, so there may be valid reasons to tag something as AI-adjacent. But the current behavior is "any kind of technical requirement is getting funneled as a potential AI opportunity."

**Implication for framework:** The framework needs a basic screen for "does this actually involve AI?" to prevent Colin's time being wasted on generic IT projects.

### Pattern 15: Logo Hunting Over Value
Colin: "Stop with this logo hunting thing. We're not the Boy Scouts. We've got enough good opportunities that we don't need to go for the small fish."

Sales has incentives to get MSAs signed and claim accounts even without real projects, because it gives them exclusive territorial rights to that client. This creates a pipeline full of "accounts" that have no actual revenue potential.

**Implication for framework:** The framework needs a minimum value threshold. Not every potential engagement justifies technical involvement.

### Pattern 16: Massive Overstatement of Opportunities
Suva confirmed: "People are kind of misrepresenting things at some level. They sound really good in a Teams chat. But whenever you dig in a little bit deeper, it's like 80% misses in the 20% remaining."

Colin confirmed: Of all the opportunities raised by the sales team, only about 12 survived his personal (informal) qualification. Everything else fell apart under scrutiny.

## Organizational Gaps Identified

### No Triage Sessions Exist
There is no regular session where qualified opportunities are reviewed, prioritized, and triaged. Sales just throws things at Colin ad hoc. Suva and Colin both agree this needs to happen periodically (they proposed setting it up during the in-person meeting in Pleasanton).

### Mixed Signals from Leadership on Hiring
Colin gets contradictory guidance:
- Surej and Priya: "Get people, get people"
- Yogesh: Fluctuates between "get the right people, pull them on" and "don't hire anyone"
- Rahul (RS): "Make sure we have work for them to do" (more conservative)

This creates paralysis on scaling the team even when there are qualified opportunities.

### No Standard Operating Procedures
Suva explicitly named this: "We need an SOP." The fundamentals are missing:
- People aren't writing things down
- No standard communication channels
- Nothing going into Salesforce
- No standard process for moving leads through stages

Colin's metaphor: "It feels like I'm trying to be a dentist and make people's teeth out just to get them to... At some point your job is not just to call people."

### Delivery and Pre-Sales Not Separated
Suva's observation: The same team (Amit's) is accountable for pre-sales, presentations, solutioning, AND delivery. This creates conflicts because people get pulled from delivery into pre-sales constantly. Priya was hired with the intention of eventually separating delivery, but this hasn't happened yet.

## Implications for the Framework

This conversation confirms and extends the problem statements:

| Existing Problem | What Suva Added |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| No qualification before technical involvement | Supply qualification is equally important (can we deliver?) |
| Sales can't distinguish sales vs solutions conversations | Workshop facilitator explicitly won't address this |
| AI literacy gap | Non-AI work being tagged as AI; sales doesn't know the difference |
| Capacity bottleneck | No triage sessions, no prioritization, leadership sends mixed signals on hiring |
| No accountability | Salespeople have incentive structures that reward quantity over quality (logo hunting, MSA signing, territory claims) |

**New framework requirements from this discussion:**
- Dual qualification: demand AND supply
- Minimum value threshold (not every logo is worth pursuing)
- AI relevance screen (does this actually involve AI?)
- Written documentation requirement (no more "call me and I'll tell you about it")
- Periodic triage cadence (not ad hoc)
- The qualification gate must fire BEFORE any meeting is scheduled, not after
