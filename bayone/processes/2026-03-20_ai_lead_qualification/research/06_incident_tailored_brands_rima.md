# Incident: Tailored Brands / Rima (Feb 26 - Mar 9, 2026)

This was the most extended and revealing incident. It spans multiple weeks and escalated to CEO/CTO involvement.

## Patterns Present
- Pattern 2 (Scope changes and moving goalposts)
- Pattern 3 (Promising things that don't exist)
- Pattern 5 (Committing Colin's time without asking)
- NEW: Fabricating the state of an opportunity
- NEW: Misrepresenting staffing engagements as solutions work
- NEW: Persistent re-engagement after being told no

## Phase 1: The Setup (Feb 26)
Colin had met with the sales team and CEO Surej about Tailored Brands. The agreed angle:
- Scoping known internal initiatives (code modernization, QA testing, analytics, dashboards, customer intelligence)
- Tailored Brands has a new Director of AI (Cao Yuan) who's non-technical but a domain SME
- There were already known work streams and teams

Rima then sent a message changing the ask entirely:
- "Go over our AI expertise around retail side"
- "Bring info about how Ecom is looking at AI"
- Expectation that Colin presents retail ecommerce AI expertise BayOne doesn't have

**Three different meetings were described to Colin:**
1. What was agreed with Surej (scoping known internal initiatives)
2. What Rima told him it was (scoping call where client shares what they want)
3. What the latest message described (Colin presents retail AI expertise)

Colin's response identified the "third thing" contradiction directly.

## Phase 2: Rima Commits Colin's Deliverables (Feb 26)
Without asking Colin, Rima asked the client what they'd like to hear from BayOne, then committed Colin to presenting on "how eCom is looking at AI." She also assumed Colin would share specifics from Cisco and other client engagements as sales material.

Colin's key boundary: "I can't own talking points or be expected to make slides that were offered up without my input."

## Phase 3: Client Validates Colin's Position (Feb 27)
The client director (Kalyan) responded: **"I am not ready for that yet, apologies if there was an expectation set that there would be anything around AI topic."**

This was the definitive proof that Rima had set expectations with the client that even the client didn't want. She was manufacturing urgency around a meeting the client wasn't ready for.

## Phase 4: The Meeting Goes Wrong (Mar 4)
Despite all the pushback, the meeting happened and went exactly as Colin warned:
- Client wasn't ready to share anything of substance beyond "we want to use more AI"
- No entry point for Colin
- Two minutes in, Rima said Colin would present on case studies, other work, and industry thoughts
- This was exactly what Colin said not to do
- Case studies were volunteered for work still in progress

## Phase 5: Rima Keeps Pulling Colin Back (Mar 8-9)
Even after the meeting proved Colin right, Rima continued trying to pull him back in:
- Suggested they could "help with AI some" even without pain points
- Argued that some customers don't have pain points yet and they should still pursue
- When pressed, she kept reframing to make it sound like Colin was essential to the conversation

Rima's response to the CTO asking a direct question about retail experience was to dodge and pivot to "looking forward to discussing further."

## Phase 6: CTO Validates (Mar 9)
Priya (CTO) independently reached the same conclusion as Colin:
- "It's everything on earth and hard to fine grain"
- "We should not just randomly submit to solve world hunger in a deck"
- "We might lose face"
- "We need to have a clear plan of what we are trying to address"

## Key Dynamics

### Rima's Pattern
- Presents opportunities as much further along than they actually are
- Told the CEO there were known work streams when there weren't
- Commits Colin's time and deliverables without asking
- When challenged, reframes the conversation to pull Colin back in
- Dodges direct questions when caught
- Engages in persistent re-engagement even after being told no
- Doesn't know her own client well enough to know their Director of AI wasn't ready to talk AI

### The Meeting Proved the Sales Philosophy
- Problem-first approach: ~100% success rate
- Capability-first pitch: ~0% success rate
- This meeting was the latter and it went exactly as predicted

## New Insights for Framework

1. **Opportunity verification.** When sales says "they have known work streams," verify that before prepping. The framework should require documented evidence of client interest, not just a salesperson's characterization.

2. **Scope lock before meetings.** Once a meeting scope is agreed upon (especially with CEO involvement), it should not change without consulting everyone involved.

3. **Persistent re-engagement is a pattern.** The framework needs to address what happens when a salesperson keeps trying to pull the technical resource back in after being told the opportunity isn't ready. There needs to be a clear escalation path, not an infinite loop of pushback.

4. **Commitment authority.** Sales should not be able to tell a client what Colin will present or bring without his explicit sign-off.
