# Incident: AVI SPL / Kaden (Mar 18, 2026)

## Patterns Present
- Pattern 1 (Come present our AI capabilities)
- Pattern 9 (No minimum information provided)
- Pattern 15 (Logo hunting over value)
- NEW: The "could lead to" justification

## What Happened
Kaden (Strategic Partnerships Manager) emailed Colin and Richa saying a contact at AVI SPL "has requested a meeting to deep dive into Solutions and AI discussion, partnership exploration." He asked Colin for availability and suggested it "could potentially lead to a Master Supplier agreement, and other opportunities."

That's the entire context. No company background, no contact's role, no problem statement, no indication of what AVI SPL is interested in, no qualification of any kind.

## Colin's Analysis (Email to Suva)
Colin forwarded this to Suva with candid commentary. Key points:

1. **Not a real lead, but also don't want to say no to business.** This is the tension the framework must resolve. The fear of turning away potential business is what keeps the door open for unqualified requests.

2. **"Saying yes to one means saying yes to all."** Any person at any company could theoretically lead to an MSA. That logic justifies every possible meeting. The framework needs to break this reasoning.

3. **Zero context provided.** Colin has no idea who this person is, what their role is, what the company does, or what they're interested in. "I do not feel it should be my job to do research for them."

4. **Generic capability pitch has no value right now.** BayOne doesn't have enough delivered projects to credibly pitch. "Conversations like these are frankly premature as to where we are at."

5. **Bird in hand vs bird in bush.** Colin would rather focus on the ~12 qualified opportunities than chase speculative ones with no substance.

6. **Timing matters.** After a few months of delivered projects, this kind of meeting becomes more viable. Right now it's putting the cart before the horse.

7. **Anuj should be gatekeeping.** Colin explicitly requested that the VP of Sales start filtering these before they reach him. "A good muscle to train internally."

8. **This is a sales engineer's job.** Colin noted this is exactly where a US-based technical sales person aligned with the AI portfolio would be the right person, not the Director of AI.

## The "Could Lead To" Pattern
This is worth calling out as a distinct tactic. The justification for involving Colin is speculative future value:

- "Could potentially lead to a Master Supplier agreement"
- "And other opportunities"

This reasoning can justify literally any meeting with any person at any company. It's unfalsifiable. The framework needs to counter this by requiring present-tense qualification, not hypothetical future value.

## Implications for Framework

1. **Minimum information standard.** At bare minimum, more than a name and a company. The framework must specify exactly what information is required in a handoff.

2. **"Could lead to" is not qualification.** Speculative future value (MSA, other opportunities) does not qualify a lead. There must be present-tense indicators of value.

3. **Gatekeeping role for VP of Sales.** Sales leadership should be filtering requests before they reach the technical lead. The framework should define this as an explicit responsibility.

4. **Maturity awareness.** Some types of meetings (generic solutions/capabilities pitches) only make sense after BayOne has a portfolio of delivered projects. The framework should account for where BayOne is in its AI maturity, not where it wishes it were.

5. **The sales engineer gap.** Many of these requests would be appropriate for a technical sales person. The framework should distinguish between what requires the Director of AI vs. what a sales engineer could handle.
