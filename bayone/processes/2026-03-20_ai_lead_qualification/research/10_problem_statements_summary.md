# Problem Statements for Framework Design

These are the consolidated problems that the lead qualification framework must address. Derived from the skill playbook and the full transcript analysis.

---

## Problem 1: No Qualification Before Technical Involvement
**Statement:** The sales team consistently brings opportunities to Colin before performing basic qualification. He is being pulled into cold calls, introductory meetings, and exploratory conversations that should be handled by sales first.

**Evidence:** All incidents in the transcript. Han provided only a job title. Pratik manufactured an opportunity from a LinkedIn post. Rima's Tailored Brands opportunity had no confirmed client interest.

**Framework requirement:** A clear checklist of what must be true before the technical resource is involved.

---

## Problem 2: Sales Cannot Distinguish Sales Conversations from Solutions Conversations
**Statement:** The sales team doesn't understand the difference between an introductory pitch (their job) and a solutions scoping conversation (Colin's job). They treat Colin as a pitch tool for any meeting that involves AI.

**Evidence:** Nearly every incident involves sales asking Colin to "present capabilities" or "share his perspective on AI" rather than scope a specific problem.

**Framework requirement:** A clear definition of meeting types and which ones require technical involvement.

---

## Problem 3: Commitments Made Without Technical Input
**Statement:** Salespeople and delivery leadership tell clients what Colin will present, deliver, or share without consulting him. He finds out after the fact and is expected to comply.

**Evidence:** Amit sent a deck to Walmart without Colin's knowledge. Rima told Tailored Brands that Colin would present on retail AI. Rima asked the client what they'd like to hear and committed Colin to answering.

**Framework requirement:** An explicit policy that no commitments about the technical team's deliverables can be made without the technical lead's sign-off.

---

## Problem 4: Fabricated Capabilities and Case Studies
**Statement:** Sales and delivery are representing capabilities, case studies, and products that don't exist or are based on work from Colin's prior company. When confronted, they keep doing it.

**Evidence:** Amit claimed BayOne has AI accelerators and chatbot demos. Case studies are being generated with ChatGPT. Staffing engagements are being reframed as solutions work.

**Framework requirement:** An honest capabilities inventory that sales is trained on, with clear guidelines on what can and cannot be claimed. Case studies must be based on completed, verifiable work.

---

## Problem 5: AI Literacy Gap on the Sales Team
**Statement:** The sales team doesn't understand AI well enough to qualify leads, set client expectations, or have initial conversations without needing Colin. Some salespeople are putting in the work to learn; others are offloading entirely.

**Evidence:** Sales recommending "4-week structured pilots" without understanding what that means. Unable to recognize AI-generated content. Can't distinguish between relevant and irrelevant AI work for a client.

**Framework requirement:** Sales enablement (workshops, capabilities deck, discovery question templates) that gives the team baseline AI literacy. Plus ongoing touchpoints (biweekly updates) to keep them current.

---

## Problem 6: Opportunity State Misrepresentation
**Statement:** Sales presents opportunities as further along than they are. "Known work streams" turn out to be nonexistent. "Confirmed interest" turns out to be assumed. Leadership makes decisions based on fabricated pipeline.

**Evidence:** Rima told the CEO there were known work streams at Tailored Brands. The client's Director of AI wasn't even ready to discuss AI.

**Framework requirement:** Verification mechanism. When sales says an opportunity is at a certain stage, there should be documented evidence (emails, meeting notes, client statements) to back it up.

---

## Problem 7: Capacity Bottleneck / Single Point of Failure
**Statement:** Colin is the single point of contact for delivery, management, recruiting, and sales support. Every unqualified lead competes directly with actual client delivery work.

**Evidence:** Colin's own assessment across the transcript. Four active projects, India team management, TalentAI, recruiting, and sales support all on one person.

**Framework requirement:** The framework itself partially solves this by reducing the number of unqualified leads that reach Colin. But it should also define escalation paths that don't require Colin for every AI question.

---

## Problem 8: No Accountability for Process Violations
**Statement:** When someone skips the process, the consequences fall on Colin (scramble to deliver or push back and look difficult), not on the person who created the problem. There is no structural enforcement of the sales process.

**Evidence:** Every incident. Amit faced no consequences for the Walmart deck. Rima kept pushing after multiple corrections. Sales faces no consequences because Colin absorbs the fallout.

**Framework requirement:** The framework must include accountability. What happens when someone skips the process? Who owns that? This is probably the hardest problem to solve structurally, but without it, the framework is just a suggestion.
