# Incident: HPE / Pratik (Mar 1, 2026)

## Patterns Present
- Pattern 4 (AI-generated opportunities with no human thought)
- Pattern 1 (Come present our AI capabilities)
- NEW: AI-on-AI loop with zero human qualification
- NEW: Sales pre-scoping solutions they don't understand

## What Happened
Pratik (salesperson) found a LinkedIn post from someone at HPE who had just completed a 16-hour GenAI course. The post said he wanted to experiment with GenAI for incident reviews, documentation, and developer workflows. It ended with "DM me if you've done something similar."

Pratik:
1. Took the LinkedIn post (which was itself AI-generated)
2. Ran it through ChatGPT
3. Generated a pitch, a "4-week structured pilot" framework, discovery questions, and a "Shadow Pilot" concept
4. Packaged this as "HPE: AI-SRE Shadow Pilot" and sent it to Anuj (VP of Sales)
5. Anuj forwarded it to Colin asking to set up discovery

**The entire "opportunity" was manufactured.** No one had talked to the HPE person. No one had verified interest, budget, authority, or even that HPE was looking for outside help.

## The AI-on-AI Loop
Colin nailed the absurdity: "Right now we have AI writing a post, us using AI to come up with a response to that post, and then me being asked to get on a call about it. At some point a human needs to be in the loop here."

## Additional Problems
- The LinkedIn post person was a Senior Engineering Manager, not a decision maker with budget authority
- A "4-week structured pilot" was proposed by a salesperson who has no business scoping solutions
- Pre-written discovery questions were generated without any context about what the person actually needs
- Sales framed this as a real engagement with a real stakeholder and a real ask

## Colin's Response
The strongest message in the transcript. Sent to both Anuj and Pratik. Key elements:
- Acknowledged the initiative of finding leads on LinkedIn
- Made it clear this is not a real opportunity yet
- Called out the AI-on-AI loop with humor
- Laid out the qualification process that should happen before his name comes up
- Drew the clear line: "I'm happy to be the closer, but I can't also be the opener"
- Escalated the pattern (not just this incident) to leadership via forward to Yogesh and Rahul

## Outcome
**Anuj's response was positive:** "Perfect and thank you for the feedback, Colin. This is extremely helpful and your realism is key here." He redirected Pratik to have a real conversation first.

This is the one incident where the pushback immediately changed behavior.

## Colin's Frustration (Venting, but Revealing)
"If I'm identifying leads, qualifying opportunities, joining discovery calls, and then also doing the solutioning and delivery, we need to think about what the sales process looks like without me in every step of it."

"They literally just used AI to create an opportunity out of dust."

"They keep saying 'we don't know about AI, so Colin needs to get on the sessions with us.' Where I stand is, not my problem. You figure it out. Familiarize yourself with AI enough so you can sell it."

## New Insights for Framework

1. **AI-generated pitches are not leads.** The framework must explicitly state that running a LinkedIn post through ChatGPT does not constitute lead qualification. A human must have a real conversation.

2. **Sales does not scope solutions.** A "4-week structured pilot" should never come from sales. Scoping is the technical lead's job after qualification.

3. **Minimum qualification bar.** Before Colin's name comes up:
   - Confirmed interest from the client (not assumed from a LinkedIn post)
   - A real problem statement or area of interest
   - A decision maker with budget authority
   - Some indication of deal size and revenue potential
   - Actual sales intelligence gathered through human conversation

4. **The closer vs. opener distinction.** Colin's involvement should begin after qualification, not before. This needs to be explicit in the framework.
