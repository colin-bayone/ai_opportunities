## Key Facts

**The opportunity:** Troy Ward, Director of IT Operations in Supply Chain at Walmart, wants health checks and early diagnostic capabilities for in-store machines (POS, self-checkout, payment systems, store networks, associate devices, IoT). His primary challenge is identifying and resolving issues before they impact store performance.

**How we got here:** Warm referral through someone Surej knows personally. Kanchan is the sales contact. Round 1 involved Amit sending an unauthorized deck that promised nonexistent capabilities (unresolved). Round 2 is a discovery call with Troy, properly framed by Surej as discovery despite Kanchan titling the meeting "Showcasing BayOne's AI Capabilities."

**Credibility anchor:** Colin's Multimodal SFT Field-Support Visual Assistant work at Coherent Corp. Same core pattern (AI-assisted diagnostics for equipment), different domain (industrial lasers vs. retail store infrastructure). Must be positioned as Colin's experience, not a BayOne product. Kanchan's verbal framing to Troy was correct on this point.

**Key risk:** Massive vendors with turnkey store tech solutions likely already serve Walmart. BayOne's differentiation is custom predictive models trained on Walmart's own data and failure patterns, which is not something bought off the shelf. Discovery must determine whether Troy is looking for custom work or a ready-made platform.

**Capacity constraint:** BayOne does not currently have bandwidth for immediate new work. Colin's team needs to grow. This is a leadership issue Colin has been raising for months.

## Internal Dynamics

- Colin and Surej are aligned on discovery approach
- Sales team (Kanchan) had significant communication gaps but is not malicious. Kanchan was the only one who responded and her case study positioning instincts were correct
- Amit (VP Delivery) caused Round 1 problems and is not involved in Round 2
- The recurring pattern is not a technical knowledge gap but a basic communication and preparation problem

## Open Questions Going Into the Call

1. Is Troy Ward connected to the Round 1 Walmart contact, or is this a separate thread?
2. What health data do Troy's machines currently produce (telemetry, logs, SNMP, error codes)?
3. Is there an existing monitoring platform, or is this starting from scratch?
4. Is this proactive (building toward something) or reactive (responding to active pain)?
5. What does the resolution path look like after a predicted outage?
6. Is Troy looking for custom-built solutions or evaluating off-the-shelf vendors?
7. What is the competitive landscape? Who else is he talking to?
8. What is the timeline, priority level, and funding mechanism?
