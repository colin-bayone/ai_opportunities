Your Environment

Your team oversees the technology infrastructure across store operations:

  • Point-of-sale systems and self-checkout machines
  • Payment processing systems
  • Store networks and connectivity
  • Associate devices such as handhelds and scanners
  • IoT and store infrastructure

The stated objective is to establish health checks and early diagnostic capabilities for in-store machines, identifying and resolving issues before they impact store performance.

Objectives for today:

  • Identify the primary pain points driving this initiative

01

Predictive Health Monitoring Framework

Effective health monitoring operates across four stages. Each stage depends on the one before it.

1
Observe

Collect health signals from machines and infrastructure. Telemetry, logs, error codes, performance metrics.

2
Detect

Identify patterns and anomalies that indicate a developing problem before it reaches failure.

3
Alert

Surface the right information to the right people at the right time, with context and confidence.

4
Resolve

Connect the prediction to a clear action. Dispatch, remote fix, parts replacement, automated recovery.

Key consideration: Stage 1 determines whether there is sufficient signal to predict anything. Stage 4 determines whether a prediction leads to a measurable outcome. Without a clear resolution path, a prediction generates alerts with no operational impact.

02

Current Environment

The current monitoring maturity level determines the appropriate starting point for any initiative.

If Data-Rich

  • Telemetry and monitoring already flowing
  • Existing platforms that fall short of predictive capability
  • Historical incident data available

The focus shifts to building an intelligence layer on top of existing infrastructure.

If Data-Lean

  • Primarily reactive, discovering issues at point of failure
  • Limited telemetry or fragmented logging
  • Manual ticket-based workflows

The first step is instrumentation and data capture. Predictive capability follows data maturity.

  1. What does monitoring and alerting look like today? Is there a centralized platform, a homegrown solution, or are issues primarily discovered after the fact?
  2. What health data are these machines producing? Telemetry, logs, error codes, heartbeat signals? Or is there limited visibility until a failure occurs?
  3. How does your ticketing and incident management work today? Is there a system of record?
  4. What tools or vendors are in place for monitoring or diagnostics today? Have any been evaluated and found insufficient?
  5. Have you explored any AI or ML approaches to this before? If so, what worked and what did not?
03

Problem Definition

Whether this initiative is proactive or reactive determines the approach, the timeline, and the expected outcomes.

  1. Is proactive health monitoring something your team is building toward, or is this a response to problems you are actively dealing with right now?
  2. When a machine goes down today, how does your team find out? Monitoring picks it up, a ticket comes in from the store, or someone physically notices?
  3. What is the typical time from initial detection to resolution? Where in that process does the most time get lost?
  4. Are there certain systems that are more problematic than others? POS, self-checkout, network, associate devices, or is it across the board?
  5. Once an issue is predicted or detected, what does the resolution path look like? Is a technician dispatched to the store, is there a remote fix capability, or does it require parts replacement? How does that chain operate today?
  6. What does downtime on these systems cost, both in direct revenue impact and operational disruption?
04

Success Criteria and Priorities

Defining the desired outcome before discussing any approach.

  1. Twelve months from now, with this initiative operational, what has changed for your team?
  2. Are there specific metrics you are trying to move? Mean time to resolution, uptime percentage, number of incidents, cost reduction?
  3. Is this primarily about reducing downtime, reducing cost, improving the associate or customer experience, or all of the above?
  4. Where does this initiative sit in terms of priority for your team right now?
  5. What would a good next step look like from your perspective?
