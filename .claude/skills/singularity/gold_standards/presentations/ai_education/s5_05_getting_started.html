Where and How to Get Started

You do not need a strategy, a budget, or an IT department. You need one task, one tool, and 30 days.

1

Pick one repetitive task

Choose something you do frequently that follows a consistent pattern. Client follow-up emails, meeting note formatting, renewal comparison spreadsheets, document intake summaries.

2

Pick the right tool

For most text-based professional work, a paid subscription to ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini is the right starting point. Pick one and commit to learning it well rather than bouncing between all three.

3

Set a clear goal

Define what success looks like before you start. "Save 30 minutes per client review" or "reduce first-draft turnaround from 2 hours to 20 minutes." Measurable goals prevent the experiment from feeling vague.

4

Evaluate honestly after 30 days

Did it actually save time? Was the output reliable enough to use? Did it fit naturally into your workflow or did it create friction? Then expand what worked and drop what did not.

The biggest mistake is trying to transform everything at once. Start with one task that you personally find tedious, prove the value there, and let the results make the case for expanding. Momentum builds from small wins, not grand plans.
