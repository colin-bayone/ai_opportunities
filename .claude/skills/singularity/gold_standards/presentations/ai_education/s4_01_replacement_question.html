Section 4 - Resilience

The Replacement Question

Rather than speculate about what might happen, here is what actually happened in fields that adopted AI early. The pattern is remarkably consistent across industries.

What the headlines predicted vs. what actually happened

Radiology

"AI will replace radiologists within 5 years" (predicted 2016)

Demand for radiologists has increased. AI handles initial screening and flagging, which means radiologists spend less time on routine scans and more time on complex cases. The profession did not shrink. The work shifted toward higher-value interpretation.

Legal Discovery

"AI will eliminate document review attorneys" (predicted 2018)

AI-assisted review became standard. Firms that adopted it won more cases by finding relevant documents faster. The attorneys who used AI outperformed those who did not. Junior roles evolved from page-turning to oversight and strategy.

Financial Analysis

"Robo-advisors will replace financial advisors" (predicted 2015)

Robo-advisors captured the low end of the market where clients were not getting advice anyway. Advisors who serve real relationships and provide holistic planning saw their value increase because clients started expecting more from the human side.

The pattern repeats across every industry. AI augments the practitioner. The practitioner who uses AI outperforms the one who does not. The work shifts toward higher judgment, deeper relationships, and more complex problem-solving.
