Your 10-Second Quality Check

The AI Audit

Four questions to ask yourself every time you look at AI output. These catch the vast majority of failure modes before they become a problem.

01

Who wrote the input?

Did you give it clear, specific instructions with the right context, or did you type a vague question and hope for the best? The quality of the output is directly tied to the quality of the prompt you provided.

02

What data did it have?

Was the model working from documents you uploaded, or was it pulling from its general training data? If you gave it the source material, the output is grounded. If you did not, the model is working from memory and that memory has gaps.

03

When was it trained?

Every model has a training cutoff date. If you are asking about current tax law, recent case precedent, or updated insurance regulations, the answer could be based on information that has since changed.

04

Is this a fact or a synthesis?

Is the model asserting something verifiable, like a specific statute number or a claim about a company, or is it combining ideas and drawing connections? Facts need checking. Synthesis needs your professional judgment.

These four questions take 10 seconds and catch 90% of the problems. Write them on a sticky note and keep them next to your screen until they become second nature.
