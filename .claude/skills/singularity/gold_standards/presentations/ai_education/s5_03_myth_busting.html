Myth-Busting

Common misconceptions about AI that circulate in professional circles, and what is actually going on

"AI is going to replace my profession in 5 years"

Every industry that has adopted AI so far has seen the same pattern. The practitioner who uses AI outperforms the one who does not. The profession evolves, the work shifts toward higher judgment, and demand for the human side actually increases.

"AI is too unreliable to use for anything serious"

AI is unreliable when used without constraints. When you control the input and verify the output, reliability improves dramatically. The same tool that invents fake citations also summarizes a 50-page contract flawlessly. The difference is how you use it.

"If I use AI, my work product is somehow lesser"

Nobody thinks less of you for using spell check, tax software, or legal research databases. AI is the next version of tools professionals already rely on. The value of your work comes from your judgment and expertise, not from typing every word yourself.

"AI understands what I am saying"

It does not, and that is fine. Language models predict the most likely next sequence of words based on patterns. The output can look like understanding, but knowing that it is pattern matching helps you use it more effectively and trust it appropriately.

"The free version is basically the same as the paid version"

The difference goes beyond features and speed. Free tiers often use your inputs to train the model and offer weaker privacy guarantees. For any professional handling client information, the paid tier is the minimum responsible starting point.

"I need to learn to code to use AI effectively"

The most effective AI users are not programmers. They are people who know how to ask clear questions and evaluate the answers. If you can write a good email brief to a colleague, you can write a good prompt. The skills transfer directly.
