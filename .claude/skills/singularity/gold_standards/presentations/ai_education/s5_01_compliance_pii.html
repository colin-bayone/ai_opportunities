Section 5 - Getting Real

Compliance and PII Privacy

The single most important practical question for any professional using AI with client data. Getting this wrong can undo years of trust in a single incident.

If you would not put it in an email to the wrong person, do not put it in a consumer AI tool. That is the simplest rule of thumb and it covers most situations.

What "enterprise" actually means

Enterprise versions of AI tools (ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude for Business, Gemini for Workspace) contractually guarantee that your data is not used to train the model and is not accessible to other users. The free or personal tiers of these same tools do not make that guarantee in the same way.

Training on your data

Some AI tools use the content you submit to improve their models. That means your client's financial information, legal documents, or policy details could theoretically influence future outputs for other users. Enterprise tiers and certain platforms opt out of this entirely. Always check before uploading sensitive material.

Free tier vs. paid tier

The difference between free and paid is not just about features and speed. It is about data handling. Paid tiers typically offer stronger privacy guarantees, data retention controls, and compliance certifications. For any professional handling client information, the paid tier is the minimum responsible starting point.

This is not about being paranoid. It is about being informed. The right tools with the right settings are perfectly safe for professional use. The wrong tools with client data in them are a liability waiting to happen.
