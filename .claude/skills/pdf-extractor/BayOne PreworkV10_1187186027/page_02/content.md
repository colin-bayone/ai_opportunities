⚛️ **Categories of Change for Your Customers**

Businesses go through change in six key areas. Think about how change is affecting your customers' world and provide several examples that you're able to identify for your customers.

1.  **Marketplace:** What are the industries where your customers are attempting to thrive (e.g., healthcare, telecommunications, manufacturing)? What changes to demographics, politics, and economics are occurring within those marketplaces?

    *Example: There is growing pressure on organizations to reduce their environmental footprint and adopt sustainable practices*

2.  **Customers:** What is happening with youfr customers' customers and your customers' target customers? Changes might be creating opportunities or making it more difficult for them to make their own sales.

    *Example: Customers perceive a lack of online data security, resulting in fewer signups for a social media company.*

3.  **Competition:** Who is your customers' competition, and how do they affect your customers' business today? What is going on in the competitive landscape that your customer is trying to thrive in?

    *Example: A new company from Singapore is threatening an American tech manufacturer's worldwide market share.*

4.  **Products:** Your customers' products are always changing. What new products are they about to release? Which are they discontinuing or no longer supporting? What are the popular opinions about their products?

    *Example: The release of two new software programs is increasing revenue, but complaints are surfacing about older programs that are no longer supported.*

5.  **Strategy and Tactics:** Are your customers deploying the right kinds of strategies and tactics to sell their products or services? Do you even know what those are? What are they communicating through their website or when they go (or don't go) to trade shows? How are they deploying salespeople? What are they doing to get their products into the marketplace?

    *Example: A manufacturer with an online store recently went through a $100,000 e-commerce website redesign.*

6.  **Organization:** How is the organization itself changing? Have there been changes in leadership? Layoffs? Reorganizations? Territory realignments? What effect does this have throughout the organization?
