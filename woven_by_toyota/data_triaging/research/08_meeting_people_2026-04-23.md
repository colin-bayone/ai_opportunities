## Interpersonal Observations — Travis Millet

The team has previously reported limited traction with Travis historically. The following observations from this call are recorded factually to inform how the team reads the relationship going forward.

### Meeting Management Style
- Ran the meeting flow. Set a hard 30-minute cap at the top of the call and held to it.
- Controlled topic transitions, steered the conversation, and closed the call on his own terms.
- Comfortable asserting authority in the conversation.

### Dismissive Register on Non-Technical Credentials
- When describing how his current team was staffed, listed degrees (anthropology, psychology, sports medicine) in a tone that implied low regard for those credentials. The listing was framed as a challenge of finding technical talent at the pay rate, but the delivery communicated mild disdain for the contract workers currently on the team.

### Tactical Commercial Posture
- Caught himself mid-disclosure when referencing the incumbent staffing vendor. Recovered tactically by redirecting to sharing the JD via chat rather than continuing the verbal description.
- Accepted a head-to-head staffing competition cleanly and without hesitation. That decision was made live on the call without consultation.
- Set a clean floor for the head-to-head (one backfill role, this one, see how it goes) without opening the 13x scale conversation commercially.

### Technical Framing Profile
- Described the in-house AI tool's accuracy with a pop-culture phrasing ("it works 40 percent of the time, it works every time") rather than as an engineering problem to be solved.
- Framed the problem difficulty in terms of what other vendors could or could not do, rather than in terms of architectural properties.
- Claimed that ground truth rules change daily. This framing does not hold up under technical scrutiny for a long-established AV program and is consistent with a stakeholder who describes AI at the level of business narrative rather than at the level of engineering substance.
- Architect-level title claimed on the AI tool, but the engineering discourse expected from an AI architect at a company of Woven's scale did not appear in his responses to technical questions.

### Implication for Team Engagement Pattern
- Reads as tactically sharp in a business sense, cunning in commercial posture, and shallow on the underlying engineering. The pattern is consistent with a stakeholder who communicates well in AI-forward language without deep architecture depth, and who arrived at an authority position because of the framing skill rather than the underlying technical depth.
- Given the limited prior traction the team has reported with this stakeholder, the path forward on this opportunity runs through commercial execution (winning the head-to-head, performing at scale if it materializes), not through technical persuasion. A solutions conversation with this stakeholder becomes possible only if the current architecture encounters a failure that his current approach cannot resolve.
