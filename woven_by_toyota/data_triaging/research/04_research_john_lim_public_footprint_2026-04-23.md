## 5. Role Function: What a Contingent Workforce Program Manager Actually Does

The role owns the end-to-end lifecycle of non-employee labor inside the company. In a company the size and complexity of Woven by Toyota, this typically includes:

- **Policy and program design.** Defines who can be engaged as a contractor, under what engagement model (staff augmentation, statement of work, managed service), at what rates, and through which suppliers.
- **Supplier panel management.** Curates the approved vendor list, onboards and offboards suppliers, and consolidates spend to preferred partners. This is the gatekeeper function for firms like BayOne.
- **VMS operation.** Administers the Vendor Management System (for example Beeline, Fieldglass, VectorVMS, Magnit) that routes all contractor requisitions, timesheets, and invoices. Toyota Motor North America has publicly used Magnit (formerly PRO Unlimited). If Woven inherits or mirrors that relationship, John Lim likely has to work within Magnit or bring his own preferred VMS.
- **Rate cards and markups.** Sets the pricing discipline that governs what suppliers can charge, including bill rates, supplier markups, and conversion fees.
- **Compliance.** Owns worker classification, co-employment risk, tenure policies, background checks, and IP protection terms for non-employees. The Woven job description explicitly calls out Legal and IP collaboration.
- **Reporting and analytics.** Surfaces spend, headcount, time-to-fill, and supplier performance data to leadership.

How this role interacts with a technical manager like Travis Millet:
- Travis defines the work and the skill profile needed. He typically does not select the supplier; he submits a requisition into the VMS and receives candidates through the approved supplier panel.
- John controls the commercial frame. He decides which suppliers are on the panel, at what rate cards, and under what engagement structure. He can accelerate or slow any supplier's access to Travis's requisitions.
- On a managed services or SOW engagement, John's office typically owns the commercial negotiation, with Legal and IP, while Travis provides the technical scope.

**Strategic read for the BayOne call.** John Lim is a gatekeeper, not a buyer of technical outcomes. A warm John substantially reduces friction to getting on the panel and to getting paid. A cool John can effectively veto a supplier relationship even if the technical manager wants to work with them. A prior relationship with BayOne is meaningful leverage if it exists; the current public record does not confirm it, so treat it as an asset to validate, not an asset to assume.
