## Hypotheses Validated

- **The opportunity is staffing, not solutions.** Set 05 and Set 07 identified this as the likely outcome. Set 08 confirmed it: the ask is a head-to-head backfill submission against the incumbent staffing vendor. No solutions deliverable was requested. That said, Pratik's "no product" framing on the call is not a closure of the solutions path; it is the BayOne Hard Rule 9 posture that differentiates BayOne from product vendors. BayOne remains a solutions provider; this specific ask does not currently invite a solutions engagement. See `08c` for the correct interpretation.

- **The work is dynamic root cause analysis, not static labeling.** Set 01 raised the labeling-versus-correlation-versus-root-cause question as the central technical framing. Set 08 confirmed root cause analysis, both in Travis's framing during the meeting and in the explicit "identified root causes" language of the HireArt JD.

- **There is an incumbent staffing vendor.** Set 06 and Set 07 flagged that a drafted JD would indicate advanced staffing framing. Set 08 confirmed an active incumbent (identified internally as HireArt via the platform and benefits language in the JD) and an open JD that BayOne will compete against.

- **Travis's authority on solutions scope is bounded.** Set 06 flagged this. Set 08 confirmed it: Travis operates as a buyer within a procurement-governed staffing framework, is not the interviewer for the role, and the engagement structure is fixed by the procurement process above him.

## Hypotheses Revised

- **Travis's organizational position.** Set 04 research assumed AutoTriage was owned exclusively by Suigen Koide's team and that Travis sat purely on the operations side. Set 08 revealed that Travis states he is "one of the architects" of the in-house AI triage tool. Revised understanding: Travis holds an architect-level role on or adjacent to the AI triage tool, not purely an operations role. Does not change the staffing-versus-solutions outcome; does change the organizational picture.

- **The AI acceleration angle did not open a solutions door.** Set 05 and Set 07 assumed that Pratik's AI framing to Travis via Naoki might open a solutions door during the discovery call. Set 08 showed that the framing on the call, with the "no product" statement, positioned BayOne as a solutions partner in general and as a staffing provider specifically for this opportunity. That is the correct positioning. Whether solutions work materializes at Woven through this stakeholder depends on future events (scaling failures, architectural stress), not on anything that could have been said differently on this call.
