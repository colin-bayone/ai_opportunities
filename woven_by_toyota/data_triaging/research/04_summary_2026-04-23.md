## Key Takeaways That Reshape the Call

1. **Woven is more technically mature than the internal framing assumed.** They have in-house AutoTriage (GenAI plus HITL, 80 percent accuracy, 34,000 engineer-hours saved, $2.2M reported savings). They run Union.ai for orchestration on petabytes of data. They are publicly named as an Encord annotation customer. They have a dedicated Data Annotation and Labeling Engineering team. The pitch that lands is "we install the loop around your existing governance and models," not "we replace your tooling" or "we set up labeling."

2. **Travis reports to Steve Lauziere.** Director of Engineering or Senior Manager, prior roles at Argo AI and Ford AV. This is Travis's manager and the person whose strategic priorities Travis is executing.

3. **The day before this call, April 22, Toyota and Woven announced a major AI stack** including the Woven City AI Vision Engine, Integrated ANZEN System, Infra Hub, and Data Fabric. Travis may reference this announcement on the call. Colin should be aware it happened.

4. **The SOW is almost certainly not platform construction.** Given Woven's existing stack, a 3-4 person SOW for data triaging is far more likely to be:
   - Human triage capacity augmentation (Core Triager roles, SLA-driven)
   - Ontology-change-driven labeling surge
   - Quality assurance and inter-rater reliability instrumentation across workforces
   - Integration glue between Union.ai and Encord and internal tooling
   - Specific edge case mining or scenario discovery work tied to a model release
   - Residual labeling work that AutoTriage cannot absorb (the 20 percent)

5. **The most important diagnostic questions to ask Travis** (from the three-modes research):
   - What is the deliverable at the end of this work? (frames labeled, correlations explained, root causes identified)
   - What unit do you count? (per-frame vs per-ODD-cell vs per-event)
   - What is the SLA or timeline pressure driving the SOW?
   - Is this augmenting an existing workforce or establishing a new capability?
   - What breaks if the work is not done?

6. **Likely team sizing context.** A peer Triage Manager job description at Woven (via Lever) describes approximately seven Core Triagers reporting to a Senior Manager. The 3-4 person SOW fits as a capacity increment, not a platform team.

7. **Correction flag: John Lim's prior employer.** Research strongly suggests Applied Intuition, not Applied Materials. Applied Intuition is the AV simulation platform and lists Toyota as a customer. The confusion is easy, and the context fits Woven's adjacency much better than Applied Materials would. Colin should mentally flag this but not correct Jesse on the call.

8. **Vendor landscape context.** Scale AI is under regulatory and confidentiality pressure post the Meta stake (June 2025, $14.3B, 49 percent). Major AV customers including Google, OpenAI, and xAI reduced engagement. Aquarium Learning was acquired out of the category by Notion (October 2024). Segments.ai was acquired by Uber (October 2025). Encord closed a $60M Series C in February 2026. This landscape context matters for how Travis may frame competitive alternatives.

9. **Colin's Set 01 hypothesis (labeling vs correlation vs root cause) is supported by the technical research.** The three modes are materially different engagements. The diagnostic is not data volume but deliverable and unit counted. Colin should probe deliverable first.

10. **Where BayOne credibly fits.** Integration layer between best-of-breed tools, custom active-learning and edge-case-mining logic tied to Woven's specific models, labeling QA and throughput instrumentation across workforces (BayOne plus Encord plus internal), format conversion backends, closed-loop synthetic data workflows, on-premises or air-gapped deployments, and ML engineering capacity augmentation. Not tool replacement.
