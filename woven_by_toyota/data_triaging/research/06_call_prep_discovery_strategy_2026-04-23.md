## Call Format (As Framed by Colin in Set 02 Conditions)

This is a discovery call. Travis frames the problem. BayOne listens. BayOne asks clarifying questions. BayOne does not pitch capabilities, does not present materials, does not propose solutions in this call.

The ordering in the call should be:

1. Light introductions. Jesse and Pratik handle relationship framing. Colin is introduced briefly as the technical lead.
2. Travis walks through his problem in his own words. This is the main substance. Travis owns the first meaningful block of time, not BayOne.
3. Colin asks technical clarifying questions based on what Travis has said.
4. Jesse or Pratik asks any commercial or process clarifying questions.
5. Closing. Brief acknowledgment. Specific next step (format of follow-up, timeline, any information request).

## Colin's Primary Listening Goal

**The binary:** is this acceleration or is this capacity?

- **Acceleration:** Travis wants to make the work faster through AI and automation. Custom engineering work possible. Colin stays engaged.
- **Capacity:** Travis wants more humans doing the work as-is. Jesse and Pratik handle. Colin disengages clean after the call.

Nothing else on this call matters as much as answering this binary with confidence.

## Question Bank

These are questions Colin can ask once Travis has framed the problem. Colin does not ask them all. He uses them as probes once Travis has said something substantive to probe into.

### Workforce and Work Shape

- Walk me through what a typical week looks like for the people on this team. What are they actually spending time on?
- What are the hardest parts of the current work?
- Where does your team spend time that feels like rework or wasted effort?
- How long does it take to get a new person productive on this work?
- How do you measure quality on the output?

### Work Volume and Scale

- What is driving the request for three to four people now? Is volume increasing, or is this backfilling something you lost?
- How has the scope changed over the past six to twelve months? What is changing next?
- Is the work tied to a specific release, a specific model deployment, a specific dataset, or general ongoing operations?

### Interface with the Rest of Woven

- How does your team hand off to the ML platform or training teams?
- When automated systems get something wrong, how does correction flow back?
- Where does your team's output feed into?
- What is the most common friction point between your team and other teams you work with?

### The Nature of the Ask

- What is the deliverable at the end of a typical week for this team?
- Is there a job spec or role description already drafted for the three to four people?
- Will these people sit with your existing team, or form a separate unit?
- What would it look like if this work went well a year from now?

### Acceleration Probes (If Signals Appear)

- If you could change one thing about how this work happens today, what would it be?
- Are there parts of the work where you feel like a tool or an automated system should help more than it currently does?
- Is any of the work today bottlenecked by something other than headcount?

## Signals to Listen For

### Signals the Conversation Is Capacity (Disengage After)

- Travis talks in throughput terms: frames per hour, cases per week, hours billed
- Travis has a drafted job spec or JD and wants to discuss the roles
- Travis frames the three to four people as interchangeable contractors
- Procurement terms and VMS are emphasized early
- John Lim visibly drives the conversation toward rate cards or supplier panel terms
- Travis says or implies "we know what the work is, we just need hands"
- Travis has no latitude to scope changes; he is executing a decision made above him
- No mention of AI, automation, or tooling surfaces in 15-20 minutes of Travis talking

### Signals the Conversation Is Acceleration (Stay Engaged)

- Travis volunteers pain around rework, inconsistency, inter-labeler drift, slow onboarding, or quality variance
- Travis mentions feedback loops to AutoTriage or to ML platform teams as slow, painful, or broken
- Travis expresses frustration with existing tooling or with a specific capability gap
- Travis uses words like "we keep," "we always," or "we can never" followed by a description of friction
- Travis talks about how a tool, a feature, or an instrumentation layer would help
- Travis has latitude to propose or influence scope changes
- Travis references specific decision points where better data, better signals, or better tools would change outcomes
- Travis asks what BayOne has done on adjacent problems

### Ambiguous Signals (Probe Further)

- Travis talks about the work abstractly without committing to shape
- Travis defers specifics to someone not on the call
- Travis describes the work but not the pain
- Travis's framing is purely process-driven with no indication of operational frustration

## Escalation Paths Based on Signal

### If the signal is clearly Capacity

Colin stays polite, asks one or two final questions to close the loop, and lets Jesse and Pratik handle the closing. After the call, Colin disengages. No follow-up materials from Colin. Jesse and Pratik pursue the staffing engagement through their channel. Colin communicates disengagement after the call, not during.

### If the signal is clearly Acceleration

Colin can probe more deeply into the specific pain Travis surfaces. Still does not pitch capabilities or propose solutions in this call. Colin can say something like "there are a few patterns we have seen in similar situations, happy to share thinking after I have had a chance to digest what you have described." Close the call with a concrete next step: Colin returns after April 28 with a brief technical framing document, or Colin proposes a shorter follow-up deeper-dive call on the specific pain point.

### If the signal is Ambiguous

Colin probes further with the diagnostic questions. If ambiguity persists through the call, Colin defers the decision to post-call review rather than committing in either direction during the call. The close is "we would like some time to review what we have heard and come back with a framing." This buys Colin the ability to decide after April 28.

## Opening Behavior

- Colin's microphone on, camera on if Travis's is on, camera off if Travis's is off.
- Colin speaks briefly when introduced. A sentence or two about his role at BayOne, not a capabilities pitch.
- After introductions, Colin is silent until Travis finishes framing.
- Colin takes notes but does not appear to be reading from prepared material.

## Closing Behavior

- Colin does not commit to any specific deliverable timeline in the call. If pressed, Colin references his post-April 28 availability and says any follow-up will be shaped around the specific conversation Travis has framed.
- Colin does not float pricing, engagement models, team composition, or contract shapes during the call.
- Colin does not promise a proposal, SOW response, or capabilities document. Any follow-up commitment is shaped around what Travis asked for, not what BayOne wants to sell.

## Condition Verification (Before the Call Starts)

Before Travis joins the call, Colin verifies with Jesse:

- **Condition 3:** Has Travis been told this is a discovery call where he frames the problem? Yes/No?
- **Condition 4:** Has Travis expressed openness to the AI and acceleration angle? Yes/No? What did he actually say?

If both conditions are not clearly met, Colin raises it with Jesse before Travis joins. The disposition depends on specifics.

## Disposition After the Call

- If the call went as expected (discovery happened, signals read, decision made), Colin captures a post-call debrief and determines next steps. This becomes Set 07 in the research chain.
- If the call did not go as expected (pitch, misframed, no discovery), Colin flags the breakdown internally and addresses it with Jesse and Pratik through the appropriate channel. None of that surfaces in client-facing materials.

## Time Budget

30 to 60 minutes is the assumed call length. The first 15 to 20 minutes should be Travis framing. Colin's questions come in the second half. Closing is brief. If Travis is short on time, Colin prioritizes the workforce and work-shape questions first and defers the rest.
