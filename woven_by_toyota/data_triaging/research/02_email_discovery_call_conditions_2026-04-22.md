### Message 2 - Colin Moore, 2026-04-22, 6:35 AM

Colin responded at 6:35 AM with a detailed message explaining why he had been unresponsive and laying out four conditions for his attendance. The framing was apologetic but firm.

Colin's context paragraph disclosed that he was in "hermit mode" for the week, working on an urgent client deliverable that had been missed and was significantly behind schedule. He had turned notifications off. He referred to it as a nightmare week and indicated he was trying to cram two months of complex work into five days, with a Monday deliver-and-demo deadline that would cost BayOne a $500,000 contract if missed.

Against that context, Colin stated he could join the meeting if four caveats were understood and true. The caveats are substantive and should be preserved exactly in spirit:

1. **Time constraint.** Colin's week is fully booked. He can carve out an exception for this single meeting because Jesse is in-person and he does not want to put Jesse in a bad spot. However, he will not be able to do anything after the meeting (materials, proposals, follow-up emails, follow-up meetings with the client team or internal team) until Tuesday, April 28. Anything before that date is not possible.

2. **No materials expected.** Colin will not be expected to present slides or any materials during the call. He does not have time, bandwidth, or background information to prepare anything. He is joining only to verbally ask questions and gather intelligence.

3. **Explicit discovery framing.** Travis must go into the meeting understanding that he is framing the problem for BayOne. If Travis is not aware that this is a discovery call where he will walk through his problem statements so BayOne can ask questions, the meeting should be postponed. If it has been positioned as a meet-and-greet, a quick catch-up, or a general meeting without the explicit discovery understanding, there is no purpose for Colin to attend. Colin does not want to arrive expecting discovery and be expected to deliver a sales pitch or capabilities presentation.

4. **Confirmed AI openness.** Before the meeting, Colin wants to confirm that Travis has expressed relative openness to the AI angle or acceleration opportunities. BayOne should not be cold-pitching AI at Travis or solving a problem that Travis has not identified as a problem. Colin wants internal confirmation that Travis is aware of and open to the purpose and intent of the meeting.

Colin softened the tone with an explicit acknowledgment that the message might read as severe, reassured Jesse and Pratik that he was genuinely excited for the opportunity, and noted that the only reason he could consider taking the meeting that week was the high confidence and faith he has in both of them. He stated that other requests this week had been declined because he did not have the same confidence in their validity or urgency.

Colin closed with a commitment: if the four items are met, he will make time for the meeting.

## Why This Is a Separate Set (Not 01a)

The email thread could superficially be treated as supplementary material (letter suffix, like 01a) to the April 13 internal sync. It is not. Letter suffixes are for material tied to the same event (a debrief of the same meeting, a follow-up email about the same meeting). This email thread is forward-prep for a different, upcoming event (the Travis Millet discovery call). It establishes new conditions, introduces Naoki, and captures Colin's specific boundary conditions that did not exist during the April 13 sync. Treating it as Set 02 preserves the chronological honesty of the blockchain record.
