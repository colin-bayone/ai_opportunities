## The Central Contradiction

The most substantive issue is the direct contradiction between Colin's written Condition 3 in Set 02 and the meta-strategy in Jesse's Set 03 email.

- **Colin's Condition 3 (Set 02):** Travis must go into the meeting understanding that he is framing the problem. The call is discovery. Travis walks BayOne through his problem statements so BayOne can ask questions.
- **Jesse's Meta-Strategy (Set 03):** "The first 15 minutes should be almost entirely questions."

These two positions are not compatible. In Colin's framing, Travis owns the opening of the call because he is the one with the problem, the context, and the role clarity. In Jesse's framing, BayOne fills the opening fifteen minutes with questions, which is closer to a structured discovery interview than to a problem-framing discovery call.

Colin's position is that if he is going into a call with someone he is meeting for the first time, armed with almost no detail beyond "they collect large amounts of data and then label it," he has no basis for fifteen minutes of structured questions. The person who has the basis for fifteen minutes of substantive opening content is Travis. Travis should frame the problem. BayOne should listen, then ask.
