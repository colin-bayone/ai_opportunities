## Jesse's Proposed Call Strategy

Jesse structures the strategy as five anticipated statements from Travis with recommended responses, followed by a meta-strategy for the overall call arc.

### Point 1: "We need three people to do X, Y, Z. Here is the job spec."

- **Jesse's rationale for expecting this:** Travis is a technical operations manager whose default mode is headcount-based thinking. He likely has a drafted SOW or mental model of the roles.
- **Jesse's recommended response:** Do not push back on roles immediately. Ask a clarifying question about what a good week looks like for these three people and what they are actually doing day to day. The goal is to surface whether the work is truly headcount-driven or whether it can be accelerated with AI.

### Point 2: "We are not really thinking about AI for this."

- **Jesse's rationale for expecting this:** Pat's account suggested they were describing a traditional operations problem. Travis's aerospace background is not machine learning.
- **Jesse's recommended response:** Agree first ("not here to push AI where it does not belong"), then plant a seed about hybrid approaches (people handling nuanced judgment, AI handling volume and pattern recognition), framed as worth keeping the door open. Do not oversell. Let Travis arrive at the AI value himself.

### Point 3: "What do you know about autonomous vehicle data pipelines?"

- **Jesse's rationale for expecting this:** Travis is technically deep and has been in this world since Toyota Research Institute. He will probe whether BayOne understands the domain or is a staffing shop.
- **Jesse's recommended response:** This is where Colin's presence pays off. The answer should demonstrate understanding of the data life cycle (vehicles collect telemetry, edge cases get flagged, those cases are reviewed and categorized, then fed into either training pipelines or operations). Jesse cites that Woven data science teams work on modeling road event distributions, developing evaluation frameworks for AD and ADAS deployment readiness, and assessing ML training data strategy. Framing the conversation around these outcomes should signal credibility.

### Point 5: "We need people who can start quickly and ramp fast."

Jesse's email skips point 4 (the numbered sequence goes 1, 2, 3, 5 in the source). Point 5 as written:

- **Jesse's rationale for expecting this:** The SOW framing and procurement path suggest a budget window or timeline pressure. Travis may have been waiting.
- **Jesse's recommended response:** Have a concrete ramp time answer. Introduce the 70/30 model subtly, framed as "we can embed with your team so knowledge transfer happens in real time, not at the end of a project."

### Meta-Strategy Proposed by Jesse

Jesse proposes the following call arc:

- **Open with curiosity, not a pitch.** Jesse writes: "The first 15 minutes should be almost entirely questions." Her reasoning is that Travis is an engineer who will respect someone who wants to understand the problem before selling anything.
- **Use Naoki and John Lim as the trust anchor.** Reference the existing relationship early to legitimize BayOne's presence in the room.
- **Leave the AI thread open, not front and center.** Drop it once, then let Travis pull the thread if he is interested. Do not make it the centerpiece.
- **End with a concrete next step.** Close the call by asking for a specific follow-up, even if it is a one-pager share or a short follow-on call with Colin to go deeper on technical architecture.
