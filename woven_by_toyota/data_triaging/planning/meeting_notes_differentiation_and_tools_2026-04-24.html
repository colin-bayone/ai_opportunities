Tool References

TechScore

TechScore came up during the call as the internal screening tool that scores candidates on a numeric scale (described loosely as a credit-score model, with thresholds the customer can set). It was offered as a differentiator against generalist staffing pools. The team going forward will benefit from being able to speak to TechScore at a level of depth that includes:

  • The scoring model (what categories of skill it scores against, how the score is calibrated, what a typical threshold looks like for a given role profile).
  • The trade-off between threshold-strict screening (filters out borderline candidates who might still fit) and threshold-permissive screening (broader pool, lower precision).
  • When to recommend skipping TechScore for a particular submission (for example, candidates being evaluated more on soft skills or domain experience than on technical breadth).
  • What TechScore does not screen for (so the team can position its limits as a strength rather than overclaim coverage).

An hour of internal walk-through with the TechScore team or product owner would close the depth gap and make this differentiator land harder in any future client conversation where it comes up.

TalentAI

TalentAI is the newer internal screening tool now in active use alongside or in place of TechScore in some BayOne workflows. It was not referenced on the Woven call. For future client conversations where the differentiation question is asked, TalentAI is the tool to lead with given its current operational status. The team would benefit from a similar lightweight walk-through to be conversant on:

  • What TalentAI does that TechScore does not, or does better.
  • How TalentAI fits into the screening pipeline and where it complements TechScore.
  • Specific capabilities (semantic skill matching, AI-assisted evaluation, candidate explanation) that translate into procurement-relevant differentiation.
  • Reference customers or example placements where TalentAI improved outcome quality.

Other Topics That Surfaced

The 23-Vendor Landscape

Travis stated he has been in conversation with approximately 23 vendors. He categorized them as roughly half human-labor pools (typically offshore) and half AI-only auto-labeling or auto-annotation product companies. Both categories have been unable to meet the dynamic nature of Woven's bug catalog. BayOne's positioning as locally based and as a methodology-led partner that does not pitch a generic product places BayOne outside both of these vendor categories. Reinforcing that positioning in the next conversation, with depth on what the methodology actually entails, will help.

Daily Operations Cadence

Travis described daily 30 to 60 minute team alignment meetings to adjust on what is correct versus incorrect in the bug taxonomy. Candidates submitted for the role should expect this cadence. It is not a once-a-week or project-style engagement; it is a daily operations rhythm. This is worth setting expectations on with candidates during the screen.

The Japan Counterpart

Travis referenced a Japan counterpart team. The Japanese fluency preferred qualification flows from this. Whether the Japan counterpart routes its own staffing through the same channels as the US function is unclear and is an open item for future conversations.
