## Interpersonal Observations — Travis Millet

The team has previously reported limited traction with Travis historically. The following observations from this call are offered as a factual record to inform how the team reads the relationship going forward.

### Interpersonal Style
- Ran the meeting flow. Set a hard 30-minute cap at the top of the call and held to it.
- Comfortable asserting authority in the conversation. Steered topic transitions.
- Described contract worker credentials in a dismissive register when listing team member degrees (anthropology, psychology, sports medicine). The listing was framed as a challenge of finding technical talent at the pay rate, but the tone implied low regard for non-engineering degrees.
- Caught himself mid-disclosure when referencing the incumbent staffing vendor. Recovered tactically by redirecting to sharing the JD via chat rather than continuing the verbal description.
- Accepted a head-to-head staffing competition cleanly and without hesitation. That decision was made live on the call without consultation.

### Business vs Technical Profile
- Strong tactical business sense. Managed the meeting as a commercial buyer, not as a technical evaluator.
- Technical framing presented with confidence but shallow engineering depth. Observations supporting this are captured separately in `08d_meeting_travis_technical_presentation_2026-04-23.md`.
- Used casual pop-culture phrasing when describing the 40 percent accuracy of the in-house AI triage tool ("it works 40 percent of the time, it works every time"). The phrasing signaled that accuracy concerns were not being taken seriously as an engineering problem.

### Implication for the Team
- The relationship pattern (limited prior traction, dismissive of non-technical credentials, tactical and cunning commercial posture, shallow technical depth presented with confidence) suggests that the path to any deeper engagement with this stakeholder runs through demonstrated commercial execution, not through technical persuasion. A solutions conversation with this stakeholder would require him to first experience a problem that his current architecture cannot solve.
