## One-Paragraph Overview

The Travis Millet discovery call on April 23, 2026 at 3:30 PM PST confirmed that the opportunity is a pure staffing engagement. Travis described a scale problem (6,000 to 7,000 tickets per month growing to approximately 100,000 by mid-July 2026, a 13x increase) and an in-house AI triage tool (AutoTriage) operating at roughly 40 percent accuracy against 150 to 440 dynamic bug categories. He framed the work as dynamic root cause analysis, not static classification, and contrasted Woven's approach with Waymo and Cruise's static classification model. The immediate ask is a single backfill role on the 12-person triage team, with Travis accepting a head-to-head submission from BayOne against the incumbent staffing vendor. The incumbent was inadvertently disclosed as HireArt via a verbal slip and confirmed via the JD shared in chat. The 13x scale work is still undecided and is a separate future conversation. Pratik positioned BayOne on the call as a staffing and services partner, explicitly stating BayOne does not have a competing product. The technical framing offered by the BayOne technical lead (hybrid architecture, deterministic-first layering, progressive human-to-AI offload, track-record-based confidence) was engaged with on the call but did not shift the commercial framing. The technical presentation from Travis showed surface-layer AI fluency and architect-level framing but limited engineering depth, and his stated claim that ground truth rules change daily does not hold up under scrutiny.
