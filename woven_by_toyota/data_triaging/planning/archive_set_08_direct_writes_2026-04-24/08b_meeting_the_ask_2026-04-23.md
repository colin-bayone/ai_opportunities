## Sourcing Constraints

The sourcing constraints materially limit the candidate pool.

### Geographic

- Palo Alto (Bay Area) or Ann Arbor only. Travis was explicit that physical presence in one of those two locations is preferred even though the current remote policy is permissive. The reason cited was that if the remote policy is retracted in the future, Woven wants people who can come into the office.
- The Japan counterpart function introduces a potential third geography, not elaborated on the call.

### Language

- Japanese fluency appears as a preferred qualification in the JD. This is not a BayOne-defined constraint; it reflects Woven's parent company (Toyota) being a Japanese organization and the Japan counterpart function Travis referenced.
- Japanese fluency combined with the other preferred qualifications (Python, SQL, AI agent API experience, AV or robotics experience) produces a candidate profile that is difficult to source at the stated pay rate.

### Pay Rate vs Preferred Qualifications

- The stated rate of $53 to $62 per hour is standard Bay Area contract QA rate.
- The preferred qualifications (Python scripting, SQL, AI agent API experience, AV or robotics domain, Japanese fluency) describe an AI-literate engineer, not a standard QA contractor.
- The mismatch is structural. Either Woven expects to not find candidates who hit all preferred qualifications, or the pay band is constrained by the incumbent staffing vendor's rate card.

### Implication for Sourcing

Woven is effectively looking for unicorns at a commodity rate. This limits the pool, increases time-to-fill, and increases the likelihood of compromises on preferred qualifications. BayOne's path to winning the head-to-head is a candidate submission that disproportionately hits the preferred qualifications without exceeding the rate ceiling.
