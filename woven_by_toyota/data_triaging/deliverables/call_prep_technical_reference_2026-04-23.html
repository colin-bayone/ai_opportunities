Acceleration vs Capacity

Shape What the Client Wants BayOne Fit
Acceleration Make the existing work faster through AI, tooling, and instrumentation. Volume is going up; workforce cannot scale linearly. Solutions engagement. Custom engineering work.
Capacity More humans doing the work as it is done today. The shape of the work does not change. Staffing engagement. Time and materials.

Signal

Leans Capacity Leans Acceleration
Throughput language: frames per hour, SLAs, headcount math Quality inconsistency, rework, slow onboarding
Drafted job spec Weak feedback loops between teams
"We know the work, we just need hands" Specific tooling gaps

Clarifying Questions About the Work

Question Why We Ask
What is the deliverable at the end of a typical case this team handles? Distinguishes labeling, correlation, and root cause analysis shapes
What do you count? Frames labeled, cases closed, hours billed? Most reliable signal for capacity vs acceleration framing
What would make your team twice as productive if you could change one thing? Surfaces the biggest perceived lever
Where does your team spend time that feels like rework? Acceleration signal if present
How do you measure quality on the output today? Quality measurement gap is a common acceleration opportunity
How long does it take to get a new person productive on this work? Ramp time is a common acceleration opportunity

Clarifying Questions About the Interface with the Rest of Woven

Question Why We Ask
How does work flow between your team and the ML platform or training teams? Feedback loop breakdowns are common acceleration opportunities
When automated systems get something wrong, how does the correction flow back? AutoTriage is likely adjacent; probes the handoff
Where does your team's output feed into downstream? Clarifies the downstream consumer and what success looks like to them

Clarifying Questions About the Ask

Question Why We Ask
What specifically will the three to four people do? Distinguishes capacity from structured work
Is this replacing capacity you lost, handling new volume, or doing something different? Framing signal
Is there a job spec drafted? A drafted spec indicates advanced staffing framing
What would success look like twelve months from now? Forward framing reveals whether Travis thinks in terms of change or continuity

Acceleration Probes (If Signals Appear)

Question Why We Ask
If you could change one thing about how this work happens today, what would it be? Invites Travis to name the biggest friction
Are there parts of the work where a tool or automated system should help more than it currently does? Direct probe for acceleration interest
Is any of the work bottlenecked by something other than headcount? If yes, solutions territory; if no, capacity territory

Candidate Acceleration Solution Shapes

Shape
Active Learning Harness over Existing Annotation

Decision layer that chooses which samples to route to humans based on model uncertainty, disagreement across model versions, and strategic data gaps. Lives on top of Encord or internal tooling rather than replacing it.

Fits when: Travis describes deciding what to label next as hard or inefficient, or mentions humans labeling things the model already knows.
Shape
Edit-Distance Telemetry on Pre-Labels

Instrumentation that measures how often humans accept AutoTriage or pre-labeled outputs as-is versus minor edit versus full redo. Ongoing signal about both model quality and reviewer calibration.

Fits when: Travis mentions rework, inconsistency across reviewers, or slow feedback loops to the automated system.
Shape
Multi-Workforce Quality Assurance

Golden sets, adjudication routing, and inter-rater reliability measurement across mixed workforces (internal, Encord-provided, contractors).

Fits when: Travis mentions vendor quality variance, or difficulty comparing outputs across groups.
Shape
Feedback Loops Between Workforce and ML Platform

Structured capture of corrections when AutoTriage produces wrong outputs. Error pattern analysis that benefits both the operational team and the ML platform team.

Fits when: Travis describes the correction path to the ML platform team as slow, informal, or broken.
Shape
Ontology Migration Tooling

Tools that compute minimal re-labeling footprint when a taxonomy changes (new class, retired class, merged classes) and route residuals to human reviewers.

Fits when: Travis mentions schema or taxonomy churn, or the cost of re-labeling historical data.
Shape
Faster Onboarding Instrumentation

Adaptive onboarding datasets, consistency measurement across new hires, training curriculum tied to measured gaps.

Fits when: Travis mentions ramp time as a bottleneck, or output quality variance from new hires.
