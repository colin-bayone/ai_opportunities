The Three Branches of Artificial Intelligence

AI can generally be thought of in three distinct areas, each built for different kinds of problems and already showing up in different parts of your daily life.

Machine Learning

Systems that learn patterns from historical data to make predictions and decisions. This is the oldest area, and it has been quietly embedded in everyday tools for over a decade.

Visa and Mastercard fraud detection flags unusual transactions in real time before you even notice them
Netflix, Spotify, and Amazon recommendations learn your preferences over time to suggest what to watch, listen to, or buy next
Google's Gmail spam filtering keeps the vast majority of junk email out of your inbox without any manual rules
Weather forecasting from services like The Weather Channel and AccuWeather uses ML models processing satellite, radar, and sensor data continuously
FICO scores and insurance underwriting use pattern recognition across millions of data points to assess risk and set pricing
Manufacturing predictive maintenance at companies like Siemens and GE monitors equipment sensor data to predict failures before they cause downtime

Computer Vision

Systems that interpret and act on visual information. These models process what cameras see and make decisions based on images, video, and physical environments.

Apple Face ID and Samsung facial recognition use real-time 3D mapping to unlock your phone securely
Tesla Autopilot and Google's Waymo rely entirely on visual processing to navigate roads and avoid obstacles
GE Healthcare and Siemens medical imaging detects tumors, reads X-rays, and identifies abnormalities faster than manual review
Chase and Bank of America mobile deposit reads and processes handwritten checks from a photo through your banking app
Factory quality inspection at companies like BMW and Toyota uses cameras on assembly lines to catch defects that human inspectors would miss at production speed
Ring and Nest security cameras distinguish between people, vehicles, animals, and packages to send you relevant alerts

Generative AI and Language Models

The newest area and the one generating the most attention right now. These systems produce text, images, code, and other content based on the instructions you give them.

OpenAI's ChatGPT, Anthropic's Claude, and Google's Gemini are the major platforms driving business and consumer adoption
Document summarization and drafting can condense a 50-page report into key takeaways or draft a client letter from rough notes
Image generation through tools like DALL-E and Midjourney creates visuals from text descriptions for marketing, presentations, and design
Meeting transcription and summarization from tools like Otter.ai and Microsoft Copilot captures conversations and produces structured notes automatically
Email and communication drafting takes a few bullet points and produces polished client-ready messages tailored to your tone and audience
Research and analysis can synthesize information across multiple documents, compare options, and surface patterns you might not catch manually
