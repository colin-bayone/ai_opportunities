How Language Models Learn

Ground Truth and Training Data

Ground truth is the verified, accepted correct answer that a model is trained against. In fraud detection, ground truth is whether a transaction was actually fraudulent. In medical imaging, ground truth is a confirmed biopsy result. In language models, ground truth is the massive body of text the model learned from, and that distinction changes everything about how you should think about AI output.

What the model "knows" is what it was trained on

A language model's knowledge is the statistical patterns across billions of text examples. It learned what words tend to follow other words in what contexts. It does not know things the way a person knows things. It has learned what language looks like when people write about those things.

The training data's strengths and weaknesses carry forward

If the training data contains biases, outdated information, or errors, the model carries those forward into its responses. It has no independent way to verify whether something in its training data was accurate. It reflects what it was given.

Connections between ideas come from patterns, not understanding

When a model draws a conclusion or links two concepts together, it is doing so because that pattern existed in the training data. Sometimes that connection is genuinely insightful. Sometimes it is a statistical artifact with no real-world validity. The model cannot tell the difference.
