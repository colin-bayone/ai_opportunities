  Here's a summary of the key design points:

  The dual-track tracking model works like you described: per-meeting action
  items docs in research/ (blockchain, immutable record of "at meeting 03, these
   items were identified and these prior items were reported as done") plus
  living docs in tracking/ (cumulative operational view of where everything
  stands right now). The research chain is the audit trail; tracking is the
  dashboard.

  The cross-reference file (cross_reference.md) is the lookup artifact you asked
   for. It maps team meeting sets to parent chain sets by both set number and by
   topic thread, so you can trace "the GitHub MFA blocker thread appears in Team
   sets 02, 03 and Main sets 09, 10."

  The nested singularity pattern is documented as a general, scalable concept.
  Any sub-singularity follows the same template: source/, research/, documents/,
   planning/, and optionally tracking/, plus a cross_reference.md. The rules
  are: one level of nesting only, parent org chart is the master people
  reference, convergence flows upward (sub-singularity findings enter the parent
   chain as new sets when relevant), and each sub-singularity has its own
  independent numbering chain.

  For the Cisco team-without-Colin meetings, those would be a sibling
  sub-singularity (team_cisco_syncs/) at the same level as team/, with its own
  source/research/documents chain.

  The documents/ folder is included in the template as you requested, for HTML
  reports, formatted status docs, etc.

  The existing sets 08, 09, 10 in the main chain stay put (blockchain rule).
  Future team meetings route to team/. The cross-reference file documents the
  historical overlap.
