  FedRAMP Explained

  What it is: The https://www.fedramp.gov/ - a government-wide program 
  providing standardized security assessment, authorization, and continuous
  monitoring for cloud products/services.

  Who needs it: Any cloud service provider selling to U.S. federal agencies.
   Mandatory for IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS that store, process, or transmit
  government data.

  Impact Levels:
  | Level    | Data Type                                 | Controls Required
   |
  |----------|-------------------------------------------|------------------
  -|
  | Low      | Public-facing information                 | ~125 controls
   |
  | Moderate | Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) | ~325 controls
   |
  | High     | Law enforcement, financial, health data   | 400+ controls
   |

  The Process:
  1. Find a federal agency sponsor OR achieve "FedRAMP Ready" status via
  3PAO assessment
  2. Complete security documentation and implementation
  3. Third-party assessment organization (3PAO) validates
  4. Continuous monitoring after authorization

  Cost/Timeline: https://secureframe.com/hub/fedramp/authorization-process

  2025 Update - FedRAMP 20x: GSA is modernizing to a
  https://www.fedramp.gov/20x/ - Phase 2 pilot is underway with finalized 
  standards.

  ITAR Explained

  What it is: https://www.pmddtc.state.gov/ddtc_public?id=ddtc_public_portal
  _itar_landing - U.S. Department of State regulations controlling export of
   defense and military technologies. Administered by the Directorate of
  Defense Trade Controls (DDTC).

  What it covers:
  - Items on the U.S. Munitions List (USML)
  - Manufacturing, sales, distribution, access, use, export, and temporary
  import of defense-related:
    - Physical goods
    - Software
    - Services
    - Technical data (blueprints, manuals, specifications, even email
  discussions)

  Critical Constraint - U.S. Persons Only:
  "ITAR mandates that access to physical materials or technical data related
   to defense and military technologies is restricted to US citizens only."

  This means:
  - No foreign nationals can access ITAR data without State Department
  authorization
  - U.S. companies with overseas operations cannot share ITAR technical data
   with local employees abroad
  - Every person in the supply chain (subcontractors, vendors, suppliers)
  must be ITAR compliant

  Digital/Cloud Implications:
  - https://cloud.google.com/security/compliance/itar
  - End-to-end encryption carve-out exists (22 C.F.R. 120.54(a)(5)) for
  properly configured systems
  - CAD files, engineering specs, research data, maintenance docs,
  collaboration tools - all potentially controlled
