Everything that has been suggested, agreed to, or partially implemented as a solution across all sources. Organized by who proposed it and what problem(s) it addresses.

## From Colin (Across All Sources)

### The 5-Step Process
**Source:** Skill playbook
**Addresses:** Problems 1, 2, 14
- Sales identifies lead > Sales qualifies > Sales has initial conversation (no promises) > Sales brings context to Colin > Colin engages
- Colin's involvement starts at Step 5, after Steps 1-4 are complete
- Already articulated clearly but not formally adopted or enforced

### Quick Pointers via Teams (Not Meetings)
**Source:** Skill playbook, HPE transcript
**Addresses:** Problems 1, 7, 15
- If sales needs help framing AI in a conversation, Colin will give quick talking points
- "That's a five-minute Teams message, not a meeting"
- Draws a line between helping sales get smarter (yes) and doing sales for them (no)

### Qualification Checklist (Informal)
**Source:** HPE/Pratik email, AVI SPL email, message to Surej
**Addresses:** Problems 1, 6, 9, 13
- Confirmed interest from the client (not assumed)
- A real problem statement or area of interest
- A decision maker with budget authority
- Potential deal size / revenue that justifies time investment
- Sales intelligence gathered through human conversation
- Exists informally in Colin's pushback messages; not yet formalized

### Problem-First Sales Philosophy
**Source:** Skill playbook, Tailored Brands messages
**Addresses:** Problems 2, 17
- "What can we do for you" not "here are our capabilities"
- Client talks more than BayOne
- ~100% success rate when open-ended and problem-first
- ~0% success rate on capability pitches
- Proven but not codified for the sales team

### Paper Trail / Escalation to Leadership
**Source:** HPE transcript, Tailored Brands
**Addresses:** Problems 8, 14
- Colin documents exchanges and forwards patterns to Yogesh and Rahul
- Creates accountability record for repeat offenders
- Reactive, not preventive

### Anuj as Gatekeeper
**Source:** AVI SPL email
**Addresses:** Problems 14, 1
- Colin explicitly requested that Anuj start filtering requests from his team before they reach Colin
- "A good muscle to train internally"
- Not yet implemented

### Minimum Value Threshold
**Source:** AVI SPL email, Suva discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 12, 13
- Not every opportunity justifies AI resource involvement
- "Bird in hand vs bird in bush" prioritization
- Focus on ~12 qualified opportunities vs. chasing speculative ones
- Colin wants a per-quarter revenue threshold; not yet defined

### Chatbot / Intake Tool
**Source:** Suva discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 1, 10, 14
- Put the qualification framework into a chatbot available to the sales team
- Purpose: "Call BS without wasting our time on meetings"
- Prevents the Phone Trap (Pattern 12) by requiring written input before any call
- Colin mentioned his team could potentially build this for the following week
- Suva was cautiously supportive; questioned if the document alone would suffice

### SEAL Team 6 vs. Baseline Everyone
**Source:** Anand discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 5, 15
- Option A: Train all salespeople to a baseline AI literacy
- Option B: Create a specialist AI sales subset as pre-sales qualifiers
- Colin's instinct leans toward a specialist subset but is open to starting with baseline
- Anand recommends: start with baseline, differentiate in 1-2 months based on adoption
- Not yet decided

## From Anand (External Consultant)

### Meeting Planner Tool
**Source:** Anand discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 1, 10, 14
- Simple form (~15 min to fill) before any meeting
- Documents meeting purpose, desired outcomes, context, stakeholder info
- Creates a natural gate: "Fill up the meeting planner and send it to me"
- Anand is introducing this as part of the workshop
- Colin's intended use: replace "20-minute verbal stories" with "5-minute written brief"
- Anand's observation: "People will follow the process only when others stop listening to stories"

### Common Language / Terminology
**Source:** Anand discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 5, 6
- Standard terms for discussing opportunities, stages, stakeholder roles
- Currently no shared vocabulary; different people mean different things
- Will be introduced at the workshop

### Role-Based Presentation Approach
**Source:** Anand discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 17, 18
- Different presentations for different stakeholder levels (CEO vs. director vs. finance)
- Replaces the current one-size-fits-all kitchen-sink approach
- Will be introduced at the workshop

### Weekly AI Talking Points
**Source:** Anand discussion
**Addresses:** Problems 5, 15
- 5 talking points sent to the sales team weekly
- Enables them to use the "shiny thing" (AI) themselves instead of needing Colin in the room
- Proposed as a collaboration between Colin and Anand
- Not yet started
