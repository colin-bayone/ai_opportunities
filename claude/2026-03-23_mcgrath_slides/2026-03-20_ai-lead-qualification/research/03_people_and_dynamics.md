## Dynamics Worth Noting

### The Amit Problem
Amit (Delivery) is specifically called out as a repeat offender for Pattern 3 (promising things that don't exist). He has been told directly that BayOne doesn't have pre-built AI accelerators, chatbot demos, or agent-based products, and has continued to represent otherwise to clients. This is a delivery leader, not a salesperson, which makes it a cross-functional boundary issue.

### Sales Team Spectrum
Not all salespeople are the same. The playbook explicitly distinguishes:
- **Salespeople who are learning:** Some are putting in the work to understand AI well enough to qualify leads. These people deserve warmth and support.
- **Salespeople who are offloading:** Others are skipping qualification entirely and dumping unvetted opportunities on Colin. The firmness is directed here.
- **Salespeople following orders:** Some are just doing what their boss told them. They're not the source of the problem. Responses should be warm to them and firm about the pattern or the person creating it.

### Escalation Pattern
Colin documents these exchanges as a paper trail and forwards patterns to leadership (Yogesh, Rahul) when needed. This suggests the problems are known at the leadership level but not yet resolved structurally.

### Power Dynamic
When Colin pushes back, he risks looking "difficult." When he complies, he absorbs the consequences of someone else's bad process. The current dynamic puts all the accountability on the technical resource and none on the person who created the problem. The framework needs to shift this.

---

## Implications for the Framework

1. **Amit and delivery leadership need to be in scope.** This isn't just a sales team problem. Delivery is making promises too.
2. **The framework needs to work for different skill levels.** Some salespeople need guidance, others need guardrails.
3. **Leadership buy-in is essential.** Colin has been escalating patterns individually. A framework gives leadership a structural solution instead of case-by-case firefighting.
4. **Accountability must shift.** The framework should make it clear whose job qualification is, and what happens when it's skipped.
