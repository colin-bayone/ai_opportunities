# Role Boundaries and the Process That Works

## Colin's Role: Where It Starts and Stops

### What Colin Does (Solutions / Closing)
- Assesses qualified opportunities to determine fit for BayOne
- Leads discovery and scoping calls when there is a confirmed problem or interest area
- Provides quick pointers and talking points to help sales frame AI (a 5-minute Teams message, not a meeting)
- Builds real approaches and proposals when there's something concrete
- Joins calls where a client has a known problem statement

### What Colin Does Not Do (Sales / Opening)
- Identify or source leads
- Qualify whether an opportunity is real, has budget, or has a decision maker
- Join introductory or exploratory calls with no vetting or agenda
- Create generic AI capability decks or presentations
- Present broad AI capabilities to generate interest
- Do research, build slides, or prep for meetings sales committed to without him
- Own deliverables or commitments promised to a client without his input

### The Key Distinction
Colin is the **closer, not the opener**. His involvement should begin after qualification is complete, not before. When he's pulled in early, it's almost always because someone skipped the qualification step.

## The Process That Actually Produces Results

This is the pipeline that has been working. Colin's involvement should only begin after steps 1-4 are complete.

### Step 1: Sales identifies a potential lead
Source doesn't matter (LinkedIn, referrals, existing relationships). This is the sales team's core function.

### Step 2: Sales qualifies the lead
Before anyone else gets involved, sales must answer:
- Is there actual interest from the client (confirmed, not assumed)?
- Is there a real problem or pain point?
- Can this person actually give us business? Decision maker with budget authority?
- What is the potential deal size? Revenue that justifies time investment?
- What sales intelligence has been gathered?

### Step 3: Sales has an initial conversation
A short call or exchange to understand what the client is looking for. **No promises, no commitments** about what BayOne will present or deliver. Just listening.

### Step 4: Sales brings context to Colin
Notes, details, the problem statement, the client's areas of interest. Colin assesses fit and starts adding value.

### Step 5: Colin engages
Discovery, scoping, solution design, proposals.

## Where the Process Breaks Down

The five patterns documented in `00_recurring_problem_patterns.md` all represent failures at Steps 2-4:

| Pattern | Process Failure |
|---------|----------------|
| "Come present capabilities" | Skips Steps 2-4 entirely |
| Scope changes / goalposts | Step 3 happens but Step 4 is skipped or distorted |
| Promising nonexistent things | Steps 3-4 are done by someone else who fabricates |
| AI-generated opportunities | Steps 1-2 are automated with no human judgment |
| Committing Colin's time | Step 4 is replaced by telling Colin what he's doing |

**Common thread: sales is not completing qualification before pulling in the technical resource.**
