# Incident: Unknown Client / Han (Feb 26, 2026)

## Patterns Present
- Pattern 1 (Come present our AI capabilities)
- Pattern 5 (Committing Colin's time without asking)
- NEW: No basic information provided (no company name, no context)

## What Happened
Han (senior salesperson) sent Colin a message asking him to join a meeting with "a VP of Global Marketing Ops and AI Driven Analytics" on Monday at 9am PT. Han asked Colin to "share your perspective on AI and data analytics."

**All Colin had to work with was a job title and a time slot.** No company name, no problem statement, no context about what the client was looking for.

## Key Dynamic
- Han is senior to Colin career-wise (not in company hierarchy) so Colin wanted to be respectful
- This is the "calendar invite with vibes" problem: a time, a title, and "share your perspective on AI"
- Sales is treating Colin as a default plus-one on any call that mentions AI
- The quiet part: "How can I do my job if you're not going to do your job?"

## Colin's Approach
Asked clarifying questions that effectively force the salesperson to do qualification:
- What company/client is this for?
- Have they shared any opportunities or problems/pain points?
- Framed his requirement as "what works" rather than "what I refuse to do"

## New Insight for Framework
**Minimum information requirements.** The framework needs a minimum bar of information that must be provided before Colin's name comes up. At minimum: company name, contact person, confirmed interest, problem statement or area of exploration. A job title and a time slot is not enough.

## Outcome
Han came back with more context after Colin's questions. Colin's response essentially trained Han on what a qualified lead looks like, one conversation at a time.
