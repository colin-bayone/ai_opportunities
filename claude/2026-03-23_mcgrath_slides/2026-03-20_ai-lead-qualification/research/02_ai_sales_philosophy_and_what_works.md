# AI Sales Philosophy and What Actually Works

## The Data Point That Matters Most

Colin has tracked outcomes across his engagements:

- **~100% success rate** when the approach is open-ended, problem-first, and the client talks more than BayOne
- **~0% success rate** when BayOne leads with capabilities, talks more than the client, or tries to assume the client's problem

This isn't theoretical. This is tracked across real engagements.

---

## What Works: Problem-First Selling

- Lead with "what can we do for you" not "here are our capabilities"
- The conversation starts with the client's actual problem
- Client talks more than BayOne
- BayOne listens, asks questions, scopes solutions based on what it hears
- Trust builds naturally because the conversation is about the client, not about BayOne

## What Fails: Capability-First Selling

- BayOne presents a broad AI capabilities deck
- BayOne talks more than the client
- The pitch is generic and not tailored to a specific problem
- Client is passive listener, not engaged participant
- No problem statement anchoring the conversation

---

## Honesty as Strategy

BayOne is early in building out its AI practice. Colin's position:

- **Own it honestly** rather than pretend otherwise
- Never position BayOne as having capabilities it doesn't have
- Never fake demos, products, or accelerators that don't exist
- Real client work (Cisco, Sephora) is in CICD, DevOps, code modernization, enterprise platforms
- These are legitimate references but they don't map to every industry or use case
- Colin's personal experience from prior roles is not the same as BayOne's current capabilities. People confuse this regularly.

---

## Scoping Belongs to the Technical Lead

- A "4-week structured pilot" or any scoping recommendation should never come from sales without Colin's input
- Sales does not scope solutions
- When sales pre-scopes, it either under-promises (making the opportunity smaller than it could be) or over-promises (creating commitments that can't be met)

---

## Implications for the Lead Qualification Framework

The framework must encode these principles:

1. **No capability pitches.** The framework should ensure sales has gathered a problem statement before involving technical resources.
2. **Client should be talking.** If the meeting plan has BayOne presenting more than listening, it's not ready for Colin.
3. **Honesty about what BayOne has.** The framework should give sales clear language about what BayOne can and can't claim.
4. **No pre-scoping.** Sales should never include timelines, pilot structures, or solution approaches in their pitch. That's Colin's job after qualification.
5. **Prior experience vs. current capabilities.** Sales needs to understand the distinction and the framework should make it explicit.
