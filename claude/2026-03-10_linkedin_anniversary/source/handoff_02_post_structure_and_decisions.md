# Document 2: LinkedIn Post - Structure, Content Decisions, and Approved Beats

## What This Post Is

A one-year work anniversary post for LinkedIn celebrating Colin's first year at BayOne Solutions as Director of AI. The post date is March 10, 2026. Colin started in March 2025.

## Overall Vibe and Energy

Colin described the desired energy as: reflective, grateful, and fired up. He wants people to read this and know that he is genuinely happy to be at BayOne and extremely optimistic about the future. The post should also serve as a soft, organic sales pitch for BayOne's AI capabilities without being a direct or shameless sales pitch. The energy should convey "you don't even know what's coming" WITHOUT literally using that phrase (Colin explicitly said not to say it directly; an earlier draft ignored this instruction and used it as a standalone closing line, which was wrong).

## Reference Post: Neha's LinkedIn Post

Colin provided Neha's LinkedIn post about her promotion to VP of Customer Success & Growth at BayOne as a reference for general LinkedIn post DNA (personal narrative, gratitude, forward-looking energy, shoutouts). However, Colin explicitly said DO NOT copy her structure or content. Just get a general idea of the format of a professional LinkedIn milestone post.

## Approved Narrative Arc

The agreed-upon structure (Colin approved this outline):

1. **The Coherent to BayOne transition** - Left a 40,000-person global manufacturer. Great people, fascinating work, but things changed over time and it was the signal to move on. Landed at BayOne as Director of AI.
2. **BayOne's culture and people making the hard transition easier** - The kindness, thoughtfulness, and support from BayOne people made a tough transition feel natural.
3. **Building the AI practice** - Framed as what he built and where things are now, NOT as "there was nothing here before." AI was in its infancy; Colin brought structure, strategy, and solutions.
4. **The founding team** - Named individually (Vaishali, Pallavi, Shalini, Tanuja, Saurav, Durgesh). All still on the team. Roughly 80% female. Tie to #MakeTechPurple. This happened through merit-based hiring, not quotas.
5. **TalentAI and the ELETS award** - TalentAI is an internal recruitment intelligence platform, NOT a public product. Do not position it as a product for sale. Mention it casually. The ELETS India AI Impact Summit award for AI in Recruitment, Screening and Evaluation should be woven in alongside it, not as a focal point or a lead.
6. **Impact Leader Award** - BayOne internal award. Quick, humble mention.
7. **A couple specific capability areas named naturally** - Developer productivity, enterprise automation, agentic AI, quality engineering, etc. These should be mentioned as solutions BayOne offers, not as products. Worked in naturally, not as a pitch or a list.
8. **AI Strategy and Innovation Office** - This is the formalized structure Colin is leading that brings everything under one roof. This is the "what's coming" moment and the forward-looking excitement. Colin wants to drum up excitement and start being more public on LinkedIn with BayOne's AI business. The framing should convey anticipation without overpromising.
9. **Soft CTA** - If you've got problems, we've got solutions. Always a message away. Colin loves to talk, usually a little too much. This is the organic sales pitch moment.
10. **Hiring plug** - The team is growing. Looking for bright, passionate people who are ready to roll up their sleeves and immerse themselves in AI.
11. **Leadership and colleague shoutouts** - Rahul, Yogesh, Surej, Zahra, Rahul Bobbili (note: Colin spelled it "Babuli" at one point but "Bobbili" matches Neha's post), Vinayak, Venkat, Ambar, Suva, Amit, Anuj, and Neha. Colin noted that he'll swap names out with LinkedIn profile links when posting, so the names don't need to be perfectly formatted.
12. **Alli shoutout** - Colin's wife. Punchy, funny, real. The detail: late nights that turned into early mornings, usually in the same day, and Alli never getting sick of Colin yelling at his computer (as she calls it) whenever Claude or Gemini isn't cooperating. This should be warm and Colin-like. Colin said he'd think more about this one.
13. **#MakeTechPurple hashtag** - BayOne's initiative launched in 2019 to close the gender gap in tech. Purple = red (women in tech) + blue (historically male-dominated industry). BayOne increased women hires from 26% to 46% in four years. Colin's AI team being 80% female is a tangible embodiment of this mission. Neha is the Ambassador of the initiative.

## What This Post Is NOT

- Not a resume
- Not a pitch deck
- Not a capability presentation
- Not a sales pitch (even though it has soft sales energy)
- Not a product announcement (TalentAI is internal, not a product)
- Not condescending about where BayOne was before Colin arrived
- Not trashing Coherent Corp
