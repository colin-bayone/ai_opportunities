## Engagement Model Patterns

### Trust-Building Through Demonstrated Capability

**From rahul2.txt:**
> "We started off with weekly appearances... that's what brought in their confidence that they can trust the team and the people."

**Core Mechanism:**
- Weekly visibility into work (transparency)
- Clear metrics and deliverables
- Regular updates establishing predictability
- Stakeholder engagement from day one

### Onshore/Offshore Model - The Balanced Hybrid

**From email-1-16-2026.txt:**
> "Begin with 1–1.5 FTE onshore initially, followed by 4-5 offshore resources as the work scales"

**Bridge Role Function (rahul1.txt):**
> "If they are partially a dev, but more so like an interface layer, that is going to be probably the best bang for the buck."

**Critical Insight:**
- Onshore lead is NOT purely a developer - acts as interface/bridge
- Prevents "pigeonholing" (being trapped in technical tasks)
- Manages relationships across multiple internal teams
- Provides mentorship and direction for offshore team

### Phased Scaling Pattern
- Phase 1: Light discovery (Colin only, minimal support)
- Phase 2: Add onshore bridge person (1-1.5 FTE)
- Phase 3: Scale offshore team (4-5 resources) as work ramps

## Skill Development Philosophy

### Right Mindset Over Domain Expertise

**From email-1-16-2026.txt:**
> "This is not a traditional staff-augmentation project — success depends on: The right mindset (startup-style, iterative, problem-solving)"

**Key Requirements:**
- Problem-solving mindset > domain expertise
- Startup mentality (experimental, iterative, learning-oriented)
- Willingness to work within existing systems
- Collaborative rather than directive approach

### Critical Skills for Agentic Work

**From rahul1.txt:**
> "Someone with a real agentic AI experience in the CI-CD space, but not someone that's just doing prompt engineering, someone that's actually doing kind of a system-level automation."

**Key Distinction:**
- Agentic AI: **System-level automation**, not prompt engineering
- Airflow expertise: API integration, workflow orchestration
- Both are specialized skills requiring hands-on experience

## Knowledge Transfer Patterns

**From email-1-16-2026.txt:**
> "Working Rhythm - check-ins, reporting, knowledge transfer"

**What BayOne Does:**
- Standardizes processes with clients
- Transfers knowledge through embedded team presence
- Creates repeatable frameworks (not one-off solutions)
- Maintains weekly visibility into learning/progress

## Team Structure Evolution (Case Study)

**From rahul2.txt - Test Automation Case:**

**Organizational Pattern:**
