Anand Meeting: Strategy and Talking Points

April 15, 2026 — Colin, Neha, Zahra
Background

How We Got Here

A compressed history of the Cisco CI/CD engagement from inception through today, for Neha and Zahra's context.

The Engagement (Nov 2025 - Jan 2026)

Cisco's Cloud Networking Group (Nexus/NX-OS platform, 750+ developers) needs AI-driven improvements to their CI/CD pipeline. Anand Singh (Senior Director) is the executive sponsor. Srinivas Pitta (Director of Engineering, AI Lead) is the technical visionary who owns the DeepSight Atlas platform that all solutions will be built on.

In the initial meetings, six capability areas were identified and agreed upon as the engagement scope, spread across four quarters:

  • A: Developer Box visibility and instrumentation
  • B: AI-driven failure diagnosis for gate failures
  • C: Unified cross-pipeline interface (single pane of glass)
  • D: Coverage tracking enhancement
  • E: Self-healing and auto-resume (highest complexity, last phase)
  • F: Branch health and CD visibility for release leads

A and F were scoped as Q1 deliverables. The engagement was priced at $100-150K/quarter.

The Critical Assumption

All six deliverables were scoped with the assumption that Srinivas's team had existing infrastructure in place for BayOne to build on. Specifically, in the February 17 discovery meeting, Srinivas stated:

  • A CI/CD application already existed on DeepSight, being built by an engineer named Rui on Arun's team
  • Rui was "2-3 weeks from launching" the app in its current form
  • BayOne would "take that stack, whatever we have done, and build on top of it"
  • The DeepSight platform had SDK, AI infrastructure, and UI framework all provided
  • MCP infrastructure would be available for data integration

This was the foundation the engagement was built on. BayOne was extending existing work, not starting from scratch.

The Delays (Jan - Mar 2026)

SOW procurement took two months. Hardware provisioning took another month after that. Colin communicated every delay in writing through WebEx and direct messages. Anand was aware throughout, as evidenced by his own increasingly urgent check-ins:

  • Mar 3: "How is it going? Is everyone on board?"
  • Mar 12: "Do you have any visibility? Anywhere I can help? This is losing steam."
  • Mar 23: "What are next steps? Is all access/permission taken care?"
  • Mar 27: "Can we come up with a tentative plan for next couple of weeks?"
  • Mar 31: "Any update on this?"

Colin received his Cisco hardware the last week of March. That is when actual work began.

The Scope Shift (Apr 2-3, 2026)

In the first real working meeting with Srinivas's extended team (including Anupma Sehgal from DevEx and Justin Joseph from build infrastructure), the scope changed. Instead of picking up Rui's CI/CD app and building the A-F deliverables on top of it, Srinivas assigned three different first tasks:

  1. WebEx space scraper (mine the CI workflow chat for user pain points)
  2. Meeting recording transcriber (WebEx automation)
  3. Build log analysis (understand log structure with Justin)

Rui was not mentioned in this meeting at all. The handoff that the entire engagement was scoped around simply did not happen. Rui's app finally deployed yesterday (April 14), nearly two months past the stated "2-3 weeks from Feb 17" timeline. BayOne has never spoken to Rui directly. He may not know BayOne exists.

Where We Are Today (April 15)

The team (Colin, Srikar, Namita, Saurav, plus Vaishali and Tanuja onboarding) is active. Discovery is complete. The first of the three Srinivas-directed deliverables ships this week, the other two ship next week. The A-F items have not been started because the prerequisites were never in place. The contract renewal date is April 27.

Rule One

Two Separate Problems. Do Not Conflate Them.

Problem A: The Price Was Wrong

The original $100K/quarter was set before discovery, before any work estimate, and without consulting the technical team. This is a BayOne internal problem. Anand does not care about our margin issues.

This is not what we talk about today.

Problem B: Cisco Changed the Scope and Caused Delays

Procurement: 2 months. Hardware: 1 month. Access: ongoing. Srinivas redirected deliverables. DeepSight CI/CD app still not deployed. All documented in writing.

This is our strongest card. Lead with it.

If these get combined into one ask, Anand will see through it.

The scope story must come first. The money follows from the scope reality, not the other way around.

The Ask

What We Want Anand to Acknowledge

Before any discussion of money, Anand needs to agree to the following reality. This is the foundation for everything else.

  1. The prerequisites BayOne was told would be in place were not in place. The DeepSight CI/CD app, MCPs, database access, and Airflow integration points that the six deliverables were scoped against do not exist in the state described.
  2. Srinivas redirected the deliverables. The original A-F items were replaced with three precursor tasks on the first day BayOne had access. This was Cisco's decision, not BayOne's.
  3. Access delays are Cisco-caused and ongoing. Three months of the quarter were consumed by procurement, hardware, and provisioning, all on Cisco's side.
  4. The quarterly timeline needs to reset. Work effectively started the last week of March. Measuring against a January start date is measuring against time BayOne could not use.
Framing matters.

This is not "we failed to deliver." This is "the engagement timeline was consumed by Cisco-side blockers, and the scope was changed by Cisco leadership. We need to align on what next quarter looks like given where we actually are."

Positioning

Even with three months lost to Cisco-side delays, BayOne has:

  • Completed full discovery with all Cisco stakeholders identified
  • Built a working WebEx scraper POC (Saurav's Volley bot, demo-ready)
  • Conducted build log analysis sessions with Justin (understanding the infrastructure)
  • First deliverable shipping end of this week (April 18)
  • Remaining two deliverables shipping end of next week (April 25)
  • Established twice-weekly recurring meetings with Srinivas's team
  • Full team onboarded and equipped (5 people across US and India)

The team is not idle. The constraint is access velocity, not effort or capability.

Beyond Money

What to Ask For That Is Not a Dollar Amount

An access SLA.

Something concrete: "Within X days of a request, provisioning will be completed." Without this, adding more money and more people accomplishes nothing. The bottleneck is access velocity, not team capacity. Colin has said he could do the entire project with a single person if access were not the constraint.

This is the ask that protects us going forward. If Cisco agrees to an access SLA and then fails to meet it, every future delay is documented against their own commitment. If they meet it, the work accelerates and everyone wins.

Do Not

Things to Avoid in This Conversation

Do Not Why
Lead with a dollar amount Anand needs to acknowledge the scope and delay reality first. Money follows from the facts.
Expose internal costing confusion Cisco does not need to know about load factors, margin targets, or internal rate debates.
Let the conversation become "BayOne has not delivered" The narrative is: Cisco consumed the timeline with delays and changed the deliverables. BayOne delivered despite this.
Frame scope changes as complaints Frame them as facts that require alignment. "We want to make sure we are building what you actually need, not what was written before anyone had access."
Mention headcount or per-person rates Headcount was already exposed. Do not make it worse by voluntarily discussing individual rates.
