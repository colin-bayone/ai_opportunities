## Rama (Test Manager, Nexus Dashboard)

### Openness About Pain

Rama was candid about the struggle, not defensive:

> "It's a nightmare. If they're failed, if the high percentage failed, then they go cranky, right? Oh, I need to look and find analysis for everything."

> "Regression analysis is one of the costly functions. Where our op-ex is being a little bit overspent."

*Interpretation: Rama isn't hiding problems - he's actively looking for help and willing to admit the current state isn't working.*

### Willingness to Show Real Data

Rama pulled up actual dashboards during the meeting:

> "So yesterday I wanted to, the initial I was saying, let me share you, this probably may not be much interested, but..."

Then showed:
- 21,000+ total scripts
- 25,000 sanity runs
- Multiple failure columns with engineer assignments
- Real comments and DDTS (bug tracking) entries

*Interpretation: This isn't a theoretical discussion. Rama trusts Colin enough to show the messy reality.*

### Genuine Interest in Solutions

When Colin proposed the graph topology approach:

> "I got what you mentioned. But yeah, I think that's a good part to fix this."

Acknowledged the trade-off honestly:

> "But it requires, yes, the executable commit may be, guys, you need to make structural changes on your core test, all these things."

*Interpretation: Rama is engaged, not just nodding along. He's thinking through implications.*

### Enthusiasm for Demo

When Colin offered to demo UI testing solutions:

> "Sure. We will look forward. Looking forward to it as well."

*Interpretation: Not a polite dismissal - genuine interest in seeing more.*

### Pressure from Above

Rama mentioned leadership pushing on UI automation:

> "Milesh is asking me... they said, Rama, you need to move the needle on the UI automation."

*Interpretation: Rama has organizational pressure and would welcome outside help to meet expectations.*

## Colin's Engagement

### Technical Depth Immediately

Colin didn't stay surface-level. He probed:

> "Usually one of the bottlenecks that we could solve, but it doesn't sound like it's a problem, is even just the selection of the test suites."

When Rama clarified SRT already exists, Colin pivoted to the real problem.

*Interpretation: Colin is listening, not pitching. This builds credibility.*

### Connecting to Existing Work

Colin immediately saw the crossover:

> "This is actually good, because this is part of what we're doing already for Arun's team."

*Interpretation: Colin is thinking about leverage and synergy, not just adding scope.*

### Concrete Solution Offering

Colin didn't just listen - he proposed a specific technical approach:

> "One thing that we do to start out that work is we build essentially a graph topology of the space."

And offered a demo:

> "For that second and third use case, that's a fun one we can actually demo to you."

*Interpretation: Colin is being helpful, not salesy. He's offering value before any formal engagement.*

### Acknowledging Complexity

Colin validated Rama's concern about failure correlation:

> "I think that is the complex one... how you correlate..."

And Rama's response:

> "Yes, you are absolutely right, yes."

*Interpretation: Mutual technical respect established.*
