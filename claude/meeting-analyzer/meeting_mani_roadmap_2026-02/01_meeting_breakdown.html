Executive Summary

Mani provided a comprehensive overview of his 2026 organizational restructuring and major initiatives, revealing significant opportunities for BayOne.

The meeting revealed five key themes: a major organizational shift with reporting being decentralized from Mani's central team into domain engineering teams; the EDW Modernization as a 3-year project to migrate from legacy SQL Server and Cognos to Databricks with explicit interest in AI acceleration; marketing initiatives restructured under three leaders; an active hiring landscape across multiple teams with clear rate expectations; and genuine AI opportunity with Mani explicitly interested in AI help for EDW migration acceleration.

03

Discussion Topics

Organizational Restructuring - Reporting Democratization

Mani explained a major 2026 initiative: decentralizing reporting from his central team into domain engineering teams. Under the current state, one centralized reporting team handles everything. In the future state, reports become embedded within domain teams while Mani's team transitions to lean governance, framework standardization, SME advisory support, and data validation.

Model Domain Structure Timeline
Model 1 Stores (David/Natalia) Delivery owned by Stores; Mani provides SME oversight ~1 year to stabilize
Model 2 E-commerce - Omni (Rajesh) Grishi still hiring manager, engineers execute in Rajesh's org Full transition by June/July 2026
Model 3 Supply Chain/Merchandising (Kalyan) Remains centralized under Grishi Transitions later once legacy tech modernized

"Reports will be part of those individual domain engineering use cases itself. Because that's the right thing to do, because they know their business, they know their project... report is just actually reporting what they have done. So it doesn't have to be done by another separate team."

EDW Modernization Project

Mani described the major multi-year project starting in 2026. The scope involves re-engineering all reports from the past 15-20 years, representing thousands of reports in legacy technologies including IBM Cognos and SQL Server. The target platform is Databricks with Tableau and ThoughtSpot for visualization, with some Cognos retained using a modernized data layer. The timeline is a 3-year project from 2026-2028, with attempts to accelerate to 2027 or early 2028.

"If we go report by report, it will take a long time for us. So we are saying, what would be an easier way, what would be the right way, like efficient way to do this. Instead of going report by report, can we do something like maybe if we do this, we can finish off three reports at one shot... six reports at one shot, so we can expedite the delivery."

AI Opportunities Explicitly Mentioned

Mani identified three specific areas where AI could help: batch migration acceleration to migrate multiple reports at once instead of one-by-one; codebase analysis using AI to scan legacy Cognos code and surface buried business logic; and proactive risk identification using AI to analyze the codebase and flag areas to watch.

"Those things we are looking for AI to come and help to say, okay, look at the codebase, look at things, see actually, come up with, AI would itself proactively come and tell us, like these things are something to watch out for."

Marketing Organization Structure

Mani outlined his restructured marketing organization with three major areas. The first area is Top Funnel for Acquisition and Retention under Ram, covering advertisements including digital billboards, TV ads, digital marketing, and social media, with two major projects planned for 2026 and active hiring underway. The second area under Andrew Ho covers Influencer, Media, and Marketing AI, with two to three projects for 2026 and influencer marketing as a strategic priority. The third area under Rizwan Khan covers CRM and Personalization, with marketing personalization as a major 2026 project and hiring opening late Q1.

Expectations for Colin

Zahra pitched Colin and BayOne's AI capabilities. Mani responded positively but with clear expectations.

"If that person comes with a little bit of groundwork done, instead of coming and asking 'hey, what is this? What are you guys doing?' Sort of that, to keep that, that person can come with groundwork, talking to the teams and all that and then coming with a certain proposal, that would be great. So that would be a very productive discussion."

Rate Expectations and Hiring

Mani provided clear guidance on rates. For remote workers not in office, the acceptable range is $105 and below at nearshore rates. For in-office workers, the range is $105-115 depending on experience. The previously acceptable $120 rate is no longer available.

"If it is coming to like 100 to 105 and above, they are actually, I've told the team, they should be in the office. If the people are not in the office, then we cannot go with that rate anymore."

On location preferences, Mani is agnostic to location if the rate fits, open to anywhere in the US including Canada with PST preferred. He noted emerging talent hubs in Oklahoma, Kansas City, and Utah, and expressed less enthusiasm for India-only talent.

"You definitely find better talent here and nearshore because India is just like sometimes feels like bodies than actual problem solvers. And there's such a churn."
