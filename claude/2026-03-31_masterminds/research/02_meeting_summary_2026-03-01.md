## Meeting Overview

The first Masterminds kickoff meeting brought all five members together at Confluence FP's Wexford office. Zac Saunders facilitated with a prepared outline. The meeting covered introductions, group purpose, professional backgrounds, AI in professional services, cross-discipline collaboration, and group logistics.

## Key Takeaways

1. **The group is development-first, referrals-second.** This was stated explicitly by Zac, reinforced by Phil, and accepted by all. This is not BNI. The primary value is learning from each other and becoming better practitioners.

2. **Five complementary disciplines with natural client overlap.** Accounting, AI/technology, estate law, insurance, and financial planning all touch the same client types: business owners, affluent families, and professionals. Derek's business succession workshop idea demonstrated this clearly.

3. **AI is the cross-cutting topic.** Every member engaged with the AI discussion. Colin occupies a unique role as the group's technology guide. Specific opportunities: PII advisory, AI legislation awareness, tool evaluation. Each member has different AI experience and comfort levels.

4. **Strong interpersonal chemistry.** Pre-existing relationships (Zac-Ryan from Pitt, Zac-Derek from years of working together, Derek doing insurance reviews for Confluence) provide a foundation. New connections (Colin-Derek geographic proximity, shared family stage of life across most members) are forming.

5. **Structure is light and intentional.** Monthly meetings, 75-80% attendance, member-owned sessions progressing to outside speakers. No heavy governance. The group is small enough to stay informal.
