# 01-02 Bridge: Email to Kickoff Meeting

**Source:** Sets 01 and 02
**Date:** 2026-03-01
**Context:** What changed between the invitation email (Set 01, Feb 20) and the actual kickoff meeting (Set 02, March 1)

---

## What the Email Established (Set 01)

- Five members across five disciplines
- Dual purpose: mutual growth + trusted resource network
- Proposed dates: March 4 or March 11 at 11:30 AM in Wexford
- 75-minute target duration
- Zac as organizer, Phil as co-initiator
- "IMPACT" as a value word

## What the Meeting Confirmed

- **Group composition confirmed.** All five invited members attended. No additions, no absences.
- **Dual purpose validated.** Zac reiterated the development-first, referrals-second hierarchy. Phil reinforced it. No member pushed back. Derek and Ryan both articulated how they see the value.
- **Location confirmed.** Confluence FP office in Wexford. Food provided.
- **Zac as facilitator confirmed.** He ran the meeting with a prepared outline. Phil deferred to him on facilitation.

## What the Meeting Added (Not in the Email)

- **Concrete structure proposals:** Monthly meetings, 75-80% attendance expectation, possible July/December pauses, member-owned meetings progressing to outside speakers by month 6-7.
- **Mission statement draft:** Three pillars (trusted professionals/growth-minded, experts as trusted resources, introductions/referrals) plus an implicit fourth (protecting clients from bad advice).
- **Group size cap:** Under 9-10 members. Open to additions but no urgency.
- **Rich professional context:** Each member's detailed practice profile, client sweet spots, competitive positioning, and firm capabilities. The email only listed names and disciplines.
- **AI as a cross-cutting theme:** Extended discussion on AI tools, PII concerns, legislation, and each member's current AI posture. Colin's role as the group's AI guide emerged naturally.
- **Cross-discipline scenarios:** Derek proposed a business succession planning workshop. Wealth transfer (boomer retirement) identified as a macro opportunity. Tax code changes discussed in detail.
- **Existing referral pathways:** Derek already does insurance reviews for Confluence. Ryan coordinates with Zac on estate planning for mutual clients. These pre-existing relationships are the foundation the group builds on.
- **Family and personal context:** Detailed personal backgrounds shared, building trust and rapport.
- **Geographic clustering:** All members are in greater Pittsburgh north suburbs, making in-person meetings practical.

## What Was Left Open

- **Exact meeting day/time:** Everyone acknowledged unpredictable schedules. No recurring slot locked in.
- **New member candidates:** Discussed in principle (other disciplines to consider) but no names proposed. Tabled for future.
- **July/December pauses:** Floated but not decided.
- **Formal mission statement:** Working draft accepted, not finalized.
- **Colin's PII advisory:** Offered to help members understand which AI tools are safe for client data. Not yet scheduled.
- **First themed meeting:** The idea of each member owning a meeting about their discipline was discussed but no schedule or order was set.

## Hypotheses from Set 01

| Hypothesis | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| The group would be collaborative, not competitive | **Validated.** Tone was warm, supportive, and genuinely interested across all members. |
| Cross-referral would be the primary purpose | **Refined.** Explicitly positioned as secondary to professional development. Development first, referrals as natural byproduct. |
| All five disciplines would have natural overlap | **Validated.** Business succession scenario demonstrated clear touchpoints across all five disciplines. |
| AI/technology would be the odd one out | **Partially invalidated.** Every member engaged with the AI discussion. Colin's role was validated as the cross-cutting differentiator, not an outlier. |
