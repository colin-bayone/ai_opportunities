- Family complication resolution: "Whatever other family complications there may be in trying to resolve those and not have them pop up when somebody dies"

**2. Estate Administration (post-death):**
- "Guiding the executors, family members through the process of dealing with someone's estate once they've passed"

### Technical Knowledge Demonstrated

Ryan demonstrated deep working knowledge of current estate planning issues during the meeting discussion:

- **Federal estate tax exclusion and sunset provisions:** "We thought that the federal estate tax exclusion was going to sunset and be cut in half. And we're looking at $7 million exemptions per individual. That's different planning than if it's 15."
- **Unified credit calculations:** "Your unified credit with your spouse is 30 versus 14"
- **Step-up in basis vs. estate tax avoidance trade-off:** "It becomes a discussion of do we want a step up in basis or do we want to avoid federal estate tax? And you can't have both. What's better for me now?"
- **Complex trust work:** Referenced clients who have done "Hupert's" (likely speech-to-text for "ILITs" or another trust structure) and want to "roll out of some of these things because they're just not there. They're not going to get there. Their retirement age and net worth isn't going to skyrocket at this point."
- **Legislative impact awareness:** "It's crazy how one act changes the landscape and the outlook for so many different people" (referring to proposed "One Big Beautiful Bill" tax legislation)

### AI Usage and Perspective

- Firm has Lexis Nexis subscription with AI research capabilities: "Any law firm that's worth its salt has a Lexis Nexus subscription... They now have an AI function where you can have it do research for you, you can have it write briefs for you, you can have it write pleadings for you."
- Aware of disciplinary risks from AI misuse: "Lawyers everywhere are getting in huge trouble by judges and stuff like that because they're submitting things without checking sites. So they're submitting pleadings with bad law."
- Nuanced view on AI utility: Good with closed-circuit tasks ("You give it sort of like a closed-circuit type thing where it's not scouring the internet for a zillion different things, it's great"), good at summarizing uploaded documents ("You upload a document and say hey, ten takeaways -- it's unbelievable"), and good at generating drafts from parameters
- Experimenting with Claude for Outlook workflow automation
- Not fearful of AI: "I'm not terrified of it. I think that people were terrified of the printing press. They were terrified of the computer, the internet, and we can just learn how to work beside it to make ourselves better."
- Acknowledges AI cannot replace human judgment in estate planning: documents are not one-size-fits-all, every state is different, AI lacks empathy for human emotional considerations
- Believes families will always want a trusted attorney for estate administration and document drafting

### Competitive Positioning

- Trusted advisor positioning -- emphasizes that online document services are often "half the time wrong"
- State-specific expertise matters: estate law varies by state, which limits AI's ability to provide correct guidance
- Human judgment and relationship irreplaceable for executor guidance and family dynamics
