## 1. Mission Statement

### 1.1 Zach's Proposed Language

Zach introduced the mission statement as a set of working phrases, explicitly noting these were not final: "This can be tweaked, amended, adjusted. These are just really some phrases."

He presented three pillars:

1. **"A group of trusted professionals, growth minded, want to improve personally and professionally."**
2. **"Experts in their respective discipline who can serve as a trusted resource and can learn from one another."**
3. **"Provide colleagues with introductions, referrals, that's just it."**

He then opened it for feedback: "And they want to chop that up, change it, anything to add, give to the attorney and let him dice and slice it."

### 1.2 Ryan's Amendment: Ethical Obligation to Protect Clients

Ryan immediately connected the mission statement to a theme Zach had raised earlier in the meeting. During his introductory remarks, Zach had said: "I think half of what we do, candidly, is protecting people from all the other BS that's out there. And we see that all the time, really protecting clients from some of the other stuff that's just like you shake them out of your eyes. Why are they doing that?"

Ryan picked up on this and proposed it as a mission statement addition: "I liked where you were going before, you know, I think, you know, exactly. We've spoken about this before and for the protecting your clients and your customers from all the other bullshit that's out there. And I think that's sort of like, that's an ethical obligation."

Ryan then qualified his own suggestion, acknowledging the existing language might already cover it: "But I think trusted covers that, but yeah."

Zach affirmed the addition by pairing two words together: **"Ethical, trusted."**

### 1.3 Phil's Addition

Phil pushed to explicitly include the protective dimension: "I think we should add to cover the bullshit part. Protecting clients from the other bullshit. Put that on a t-shirt."

Colin agreed on the general principle: "Yeah, there's a lot of noise out there."

### 1.4 Examples of "Noise" and Why Protection Matters

The group briefly illustrated what "protecting clients" means in practice:

- Zach noted the noise "can come from somebody's neighbor that says you need to do XYZ and then it's like you have to tell them why XYZ is just not a good idea."
- Phil shared a real-time example: "On the way here out of my group chat was blown up because the one guy was like, guys it's the time to buy crypto. Like literally was giving like financial advice and he's a financial professional in a Snapchat group like saying this is gonna skyrocket over the next 24 hours."
- Zach drew the line: "There's a difference between speculation and investing. Yeah. I understand there's investors and there's speculators."

### 1.5 Final Status of the Mission Statement

Zach asked: "Anything else on mission statement?"

Phil responded: "I think it's great."

The group collectively: "Agreed."

**Decision: The mission statement language was accepted as a working draft with the three original pillars plus the "ethical, trusted" and "protecting clients" amendments. No one objected. It was not formally finalized as a written document at this meeting.**

## 2. Group Structure and Composition

### 2.1 Genesis of the Group

Zach described the origin: "Phil and I a while ago got together and said, hey, what would be a nice idea to continue something on a monthly basis where there could be a level of collaboration, a group of professionals that could learn from one another, be a trusted resource in different disciplines."

He listed the initial disciplines: "whether it's legal in estate work, whether it's insurance, or it's artificial intelligence, whether it's accounting, whether it's financial planning, and maybe that's just the initial core."

### 2.2 Explicit Distinction from BNI

Zach was deliberate in differentiating this from BNI (Business Network International) groups: "not your traditional BNI group, Derek and I talked about that, I know they could, even Ryan and I talked about that."

The key distinctions Zach drew:
- **No referral mandates:** "there's no mandate like, oh, every meeting you have to come with a name, no, that's not necessarily the genesis of all this."
- **Growth-first, referrals-secondary:** "More or less, hey, one is, hey folks that are growth minded, that want to continuously be getting better, how can we learn from one another, how can we trust resources." Referrals were positioned as: "really secondary of all that is potentially is okay, is there an opportunity from a growth standpoint that you know, we can introduce or refer folks to each other if there's a good fit."
- **Organic referrals based on trust:** "I've gotten to know Derek over the years. I know exactly how they handle clients and business. And if I have a certain business client that would be a good fit, and hey, I'm going to bring his name up when it's appropriate."

Phil reinforced this: "to try to keep it a small group of trusted professionals that we can just kind of rely on. If there's a referral on the back end, great. If not, not really a big deal. That's not really the point of this. It is more of the development and to actually get better at what the heck we're doing."

Five members present at the kickoff meeting:

| Member | Discipline | Firm |
|--------|-----------|------|

### 2.4 Group Size Cap

Zach stated a target cap: "I don't want this to be a group of 30 people. If we still want to keep it somewhat tight, less than 9 or 10."

### 2.5 Potential New Members

Zach explicitly did not ask for names at this meeting: "who are some other folks who we may want to consider, don't need answers today. That's something for us to think about in the future."

He had earlier noted: "Maybe there are some other folks that we might want to consider in the near future."

**Decision: The group acknowledged future expansion is on the table. Target cap is around 9-10 members. No names were discussed at this meeting. The topic was left as an open item for members to consider.**

### 2.6 Potential New Disciplines

No specific disciplines were named as targets for expansion. The current five disciplines (financial planning, accounting, law/estate planning, insurance, AI/technology) were described as "the initial core." Zach left the door open: "maybe that's just the initial core. Maybe there are some other folks that we might want to consider."

### 2.7 Derek's "Pride of Membership" Statement

Derek made a notable statement about the deliberateness of the group composition: "I think the level of sophistication in this room requires that too, because it's not just like anybody. Like, this was a very sought- thought over, considered group."

He continued: "Anybody could be, you could call any brokerage and they'd send anybody. So you have to take a little bit of pride of being in this room together and being aligned with professionals like you."

And further: "You guys is just gonna expedite any growth that I have and it's the same thing with me. We now can be connected in that sense. So I think having a level of pride in that, being in that conversation, I think is meaningful."

His closing line: "Anybody else could have filled these seats, but we're in here together, so I think honored that a little bit."

## 3. Group Member Expectations

### 3.1 Zach's Framing

Zach introduced expectations as "guiding principles and expectations in terms of being part of the group" and later returned to it under the heading "Group member expectations."

He framed it primarily around attendance: "This is open-ended, but really, more so just in terms of consistency of attendance, I think, is kind of the genesis of this."

### 3.2 Derek's Statement on Consistency and Participation

Derek articulated two principles:

1. **"Attendance obviously is a big one because we want to see everybody. And I think the consistency is what builds the trust."**
2. **"Participation also builds the trust... we want to know about what everybody's doing, right? And that helps with the collaboration aspect of everything."**

### 3.3 Phil's Statement on Commitment

Phil reinforced: "kind of like what Zach and I previously discussed, probably like once a month, not making this the end all be all."

He acknowledged life happens: "If you can't make it to one because your family's on vacation or something. Okay, yeah, try to be there at the next one."

But he drew a clear line: "but, you know, some sense of commitment to the group so that way it's not, okay, we only have seven guys and two of them don't come. Okay, why are you part of the group then? Yeah, if you're not going to be here and contribute and that kind of thing."

He summarized: "of a majority of the time throughout the year."

### 3.4 Zach's Quantified Suggestion

Zach proposed a specific threshold: "Maybe it's just, hey, the expectation is you attend at least 80% of the meetings in the year, you know, as an example, or 75% of the meetings you're able to attend."

**Decision: The 75-80% attendance expectation was stated but not formally voted on. The general principle of consistent attendance as a group expectation was agreed upon by all.**

### 3.5 Schedule Variability

Zach asked: "I think one thing, I don't know, how's everyone's schedule? Is it fairly fixed, fairly predictable?"

The group's collective response: **"No."**

This was the final substantive exchange in the transcript on this topic. The implication is that scheduling will need to accommodate varied and unpredictable professional schedules, but this was not discussed further.

## 5. Meeting Format and Structure

### 5.1 Member-Owned Meetings

Phil described the planned format for the early phase: "What we, preliminarily, and I've talked to each of you on this, is you know, it may mean, in maybe the first couple meetings, it might mean, hey Derek, you're the owner of that meeting. Share some insights about what you do, how you do it from a firm perspective, from a practitioner's perspective."

He gave a rotation example: "Maybe the next meeting is focused more on the legal, estate planning, AI."

### 5.2 Outside Speakers (Future Phase)

Phil placed a timeline on this: "maybe it gets to a point where once we get comfortable with that, then it gets to a point maybe in, hey, month six or month seven. Maybe we bring in an outside speaker that has some expertise or background on a certain thing."

### 5.3 Workshop-Style Scenarios

Phil described a third format option: "could be us focusing on a topic, i.e., hey, here's a topic, you know, helping business owners that are transitioning to business or exiting and selling. How does it impact insurance? How does it impact the estate? How does it impact accounting? How does it impact technology or AI? Or what are some things we can learn from each other in terms of adding value?"

Derek explicitly endorsed the workshop concept: "I honestly think Zach touched on something before that I think would be an excellent exercise for us in almost like a workshop type function where we can say you know small business, what do they say, the next five to ten years is going to be like the largest transfer of wealth in the history of the United States because the Boomers are small businesses and they're retiring and they have no plan on what's going to happen."

He elaborated on a specific scenario: "Is kid in the business or kid doesn't want the business and do we have a key employee and maybe working through that sort of as a group might be a good exercise and things like that. But I think that can give us some nice perspective on how we can all serve, say, a common client."

Zach responded: "I like that."

### 5.4 Planned Format Progression (Summary)

| Phase | Timeframe | Format |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Phase 1 | First several meetings | Member-owned: each person leads a session about their discipline |
| Phase 2 | ~Month 6-7 | Outside speakers with relevant expertise |
| Phase 3 | Ongoing | Workshop-style scenarios (e.g., common client situations analyzed from all disciplines) |

**Decision: The three-phase progression was discussed and generally agreed upon, but no formal schedule was set. The member-owned meeting rotation was the clearest near-term commitment.**

Zach repeatedly positioned himself as a facilitator, not a decision-maker: "candidly, I don't have all the answers nor this is really about the group collectively having input and deciding as a group, hey, what we think this should look like."

But I also think it's interesting in having the AI piece and having something with a technology background."
