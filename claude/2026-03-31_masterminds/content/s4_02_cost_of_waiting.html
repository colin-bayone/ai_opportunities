The Cost of Waiting vs. Doing It Wrong

For a group that advises clients on risk every day, this is how to think about the AI adoption decision honestly

The cost of waiting

Efficiency advantages compound

Competitors who adopt first gain small advantages that stack up over time. A 20-minute daily time savings adds up to over 80 hours a year per person.

Client expectations are shifting

People are beginning to expect faster turnaround, more personalized communication, and proactive insights from their professional advisors.

The learning curve exists either way

Whether you start now or start in two years, you will have to learn how to use these tools. Starting now means the curve is gentler and the stakes are lower.

The cost of doing it wrong

PII exposure from the wrong tool

Using a consumer AI tool with client information can create data privacy risks that no amount of productivity improvement is worth.

Bad output damages client trust

Sending something to a client that AI generated incorrectly and you did not catch undermines the relationship you spent years building.

Wasted investment on poor-fit tools

Buying software that does not actually match your workflow wastes money and creates frustration that makes the team resistant to trying again later.

Neither extreme is the answer. The smart move is deliberate, informed experimentation. Start small, verify the output, and expand from what actually works in your specific practice.
