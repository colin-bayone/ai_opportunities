What Works and What Does Not (Yet)

An honest assessment of where AI is reliable today and where it still needs a human in the loop

Reliable Today

Summarizing content you provide

Give it a document and ask for a breakdown. This is where AI performs best because you control the input.

Drafting from structured inputs

Bullet points into polished emails, rough notes into client-ready letters, templates filled with specific details.

Reorganizing and reformatting

Taking existing content and restructuring it for a different audience, format, or purpose.

Brainstorming and ideation

Generating options, exploring angles, and producing first drafts that you refine with your expertise.

First-pass review and proofreading

Catching inconsistencies, flagging potential issues, and cleaning up drafts before final human review.

Still Rough

Domain-specific accuracy

Insurance policy language, tax code nuance, legal citation, and regulatory specifics still produce errors that require expert verification.

Math and numerical reasoning

Language models work with words, not numbers. Financial calculations, actuarial math, and tax computations need dedicated tools.

Empathy, judgment, and trust

Guiding a family through an estate administration, advising on a difficult claim, counseling a client through a financial decision. AI cannot do this.

Open-ended research without guardrails

Asking AI to research a topic with no constraints or source documents produces confident-sounding output that may or may not be accurate.
