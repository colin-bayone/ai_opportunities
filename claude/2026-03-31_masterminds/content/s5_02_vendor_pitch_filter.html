Due diligence for AI

The Vendor Pitch Filter

Everyone in this room is going to get pitched an AI solution at some point. Probably already has. These three questions are the same kind of due diligence you already apply to any vendor or partner, just adapted for AI.

These are not technical questions. They are the same scrutiny you would apply to any vendor asking for access to your clients, your data, or your workflow. If the vendor cannot answer them clearly, that tells you everything you need to know.

Three questions to ask any AI vendor
1

What data does it need access to?

If the answer is vague or "everything," that is a red flag. A well-designed AI tool should be specific about what information it requires, why it needs it, and what it does with data it does not need.

2

Where is that data stored and who can see it?

Cloud or on-premise. Domestic or international servers. Whether your data is used to train the model. Who at the vendor has access. These are questions with specific answers, and any vendor worth working with will have them ready.

3

What happens when it is wrong?

Every AI system will produce errors. The question is whether the system is designed for a human to catch mistakes before they reach a client. If the vendor talks only about accuracy and never about error handling, be cautious.
