# Masterminds Presentation Brief

**Presenter:** Colin Moore
**Topic:** Artificial intelligence, productivity, growth, and evolution
**Date:** 2026-04-01 (tomorrow)
**Duration:** 60 minutes total (45 minutes content, 15 minutes Q&A)
**Format:** Slides (HTML from markdown content)

---

## Audience

Five-person professional mastermind group. Non-technical practitioners across:

- **Accounting/tax preparation** (solo CPA, small business focus, $0-$5M clients)
- **Estate planning/legal** (attorney, trusts, estate administration, tax implications)
- **Risk management/insurance** (commercial and private client brokerage, 100-year firm)
- **Financial planning/investments** (wealth manager, $1M-$25M investable assets)
- **AI and technology** (presenter)

None are developers. All are business owners or senior practitioners. They want practical value, not theory. They want to feel smarter after this, not overwhelmed.

## Audience AI Baselines (from Meeting 1)

- Financial planner: On firm AI committee, evaluating Zox (meeting summarization), cautious about PII, sees productivity upside
- Attorney: Uses Lexis Nexis AI and Claude, aware of hallucination risks in legal briefs, pragmatic ("closed circuit" framing)
- Insurance broker: Tried AI for policy reviews, hit accuracy issues, wife uses AI successfully for portal coding at a law firm
- CPA: Limited discussion of personal AI use, pragmatic orientation
- All expressed interest in understanding AI better without the hype

## Writing Ground Rules

1. No referencing people's names
2. No em dashes
3. No "it's not just X, it's Y" framings
4. No inspirational pivots
5. No AI tells
6. No colons in text or headings

## Process

1. Iron out curriculum in markdown
2. Create content as markdown docs
3. Convert to HTML slides using existing slide templates

## Key Constraints

- Make it relevant to their specific industries
- Substantive learning without going too technical
- No one should feel like they are being trained to become a developer
- Practical, applicable takeaways they can use Monday morning
