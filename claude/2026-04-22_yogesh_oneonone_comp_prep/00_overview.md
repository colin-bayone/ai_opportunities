## The five things to remember when you walk in

1. **Yogesh is not the adversary.** He's a potential ally who probably can't approve the decision alone. Make him your advocate, not your opponent.

2. **State your position once, cleanly. Then stop talking.** Silence is your tool. Let him respond.

3. **Specifics beat feelings.** "I'm operating as three roles" beats "I do so much." Have the number, have the dates, have the scope in your head.

4. **Don't threaten to leave.** The leverage is implicit. Saying it out loud converts this from a fairness conversation into a retention negotiation, which is weaker ground.

5. **Don't leave the room without a date.** When is the next conversation? Who's accountable? That's the minimum you walk out with.
