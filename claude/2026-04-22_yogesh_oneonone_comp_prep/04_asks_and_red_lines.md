# Asks & Red Lines

**You need to walk in with specific numbers and terms.** Vague asks ("I want more") get vague answers ("we'll see what we can do"). Before the meeting, decide on the numbers below, write them on a post-it, and have them in your head.

This document is a framework — not a demand the numbers must be exactly these. Tune them to what feels defensible to you personally, then commit to the tuned version before you walk in.

## Ask #1 — Compensation (the primary ask)

### Current state
- **Base:** $185,000
- **Bonus (this year):** $15,000 gross / ~$10,000 net
- **Equity:** None — BayOne is privately held and does not offer equity or phantom equity
- **Location adjustment:** Remote from PA, no location premium

### The anchor
You're operating as Director of AI + Technical Sales Lead + Lead Engineer. Any one of those, at a services firm with your credentials, is a $200K+ base role. Doing all three should be compensated at a premium — not a 3x premium, but materially above a single-role Director band.

### Suggested target range

**Tier A (aggressive, defensible):** $240,000 base. Roughly 30% increase. This would bring you within range of what the market pays for a Director of AI operating at your scope at a consulting firm. This positions you to credibly hold the line that *any* AI engineer you hire below you makes less than you do.

**Tier B (moderate):** $220,000 base. ~19% increase. A meaningful adjustment that closes most of the market gap but leaves room for further movement on promotion to VP.

**Tier C (floor — below this you should not agree):** $210,000 base. ~13.5% increase. This is the lowest number that both (a) materially addresses the comp gap, and (b) signals that BayOne is serious about retaining you.

**What to ask for in the room:** Open with Tier A. Expect negotiation. Have Tier B as your landing zone. Tier C is your walk-away number — below that, the response is *"I appreciate the gesture, but that's not a meaningful adjustment for what I'm asking to be recognized. Let's keep talking."*

### Additional comp levers to put on the table

**Important: BayOne does not offer equity or phantom equity. That removes a major lever and makes base movement correspondingly more important — base and bonus are the only tools available.**

- **Guaranteed bonus floor.** This year's $15K → net $10K. A guaranteed $30-40K annual bonus with a stretch upside to $60K+ is another way to close the gap. With no equity, this is one of very few creative levers that exists. But: it is **in addition to**, not **instead of**, base movement.
- **Retention bonus.** If this conversation is genuinely about a medium-term path (e.g., 18-24 months to VP), a retention bonus payable on milestones is a fair structure.
- **Title change.** *Director → Senior Director → VP of AI.* A title change with a base bump toward Tier B is sometimes easier to get approved than a pure base jump, but it's still a base conversation.

**Warning — what to refuse:** If Yogesh's counter is "big bonus this year, we'll figure out base later," that is a trap. A bonus spent this year leaves you at $185K base next year, with the same argument to make all over again. Base is the thing. Everything else is garnish.

### How to present the number

Don't say "I want" — say "I think this is fair":

> *"Based on what I'm doing, what the market is paying for it, and what BayOne is getting back in pipeline and practice-building — I think a base of $240,000 is fair and defensible. I'd want to hear your view."*

## Red lines — what you will NOT accept

Write these down so that in the moment, under pressure, you don't drift. If one of these lines gets crossed in the meeting, you push back; if it gets crossed in the outcome, you escalate.

1. **No "we'll revisit at year-end" without a specific date inside the next 30 days.** Year-end is eight months away. You are not waiting eight more months.

2. **No compensation adjustment under $210,000 base without additional meaningful terms** (guaranteed bonus floor, accelerated promotion path, retention bonus with milestones). Straight base below $210K is not a meaningful response — and with no equity available, "additional terms" has a narrower menu than it would at other companies.

3. **No deferral to Sripriya without a two-week deadline.** If "Sripriya needs to be involved" is the answer, then she needs to be in a meeting with you within two weeks. If she can't be, the reporting line needs to change. And remember — Yogesh can act on this himself; "Sripriya needs to weigh in" is not a real block given his authority.

4. **No "verbal commitment only."** Anything agreed in this meeting — comp review date, AI Manager hire timeline, path-to-VP conversation — needs to be in writing. You'll handle that by sending the follow-up email within 24 hours.

5. **No quiet continuation of status quo.** If the outcome of this meeting is that nothing concrete changes, the next conversation — whether with Yogesh, Suresh, or Sripriya — needs to happen within two weeks, not two months.

## What to do if the meeting goes badly

If Yogesh is dismissive, defensive, or tells you essentially "this isn't going to happen":

1. **Do not escalate in the room.** Stay calm. Acknowledge his answer. *"I hear you. I don't think this is resolved, and I'm going to need to take this further. I'd rather do that with your support than around you. What would you recommend?"*

2. **Send a clear follow-up email** within 24 hours summarizing what was said — without editorializing — and stating what you plan to do next.

3. **Request a meeting with Surej.** Frame it as *"I want to talk about the long-term AI practice strategy and my role in it."* — not *"I'm escalating a comp complaint."* The conversation is the same; the framing protects you. Since Yogesh and Surej are co-CEOs, a conversation with Surej is a peer-level second read, not "going over Yogesh's head."

4. **In parallel, quietly explore external options.** Not because you want to leave. Because a credible external offer is the single most effective lever in any comp conversation, and having one in hand changes every subsequent conversation. You do not need to announce this or use it as a threat — just make sure you have options.
