## Meeting Strategy

### The Approach (From Internal Meeting)

> "We should not pitch. We should just listen and brainstorm." - Rahul

> "These guys are pretty smart. We can't come in... stroke their egos. They're very protective." - Suraj

### Conversation Flow

1. **Let Siva give overview** - He will share everything openly
2. **Listen for pain points** - QA, AI knowledge gaps, integration challenges
3. **Clarify with curiosity** - "What's been your experience with...?"
4. **Brainstorm together** - "What if we approached it this way...?"
5. **Propose small pieces** - Not millions, not comprehensive solutions

### Key Questions to Ask

**About QA/Testing:**
- "How are you thinking about the testing piece alongside code modernization?"
- "What's the validation strategy for the Commerce Tools work?"
- "Has shift-left come up in your discussions?"

**About AI:**
- "What's been the experience with Bold Metrics so far?"
- "How are you thinking about expanding AI beyond inventory?"
- "What does success look like for Kalyan's new role?"

**About Partnership:**
- "What would an ideal partner look like for this?"
- "How do you prefer to work with external teams?"
- "What's worked well in the past? What hasn't?"

### What NOT to Say

| Avoid | Instead |
|-------|---------|
| "You should do X" | "Have you considered X?" |
| "We've done this at Cisco" | "We've seen patterns that might be relevant" |
| "Infosys isn't doing this right" | "What's been working with your current team?" |
| "You're behind on AI" | "What's the biggest priority right now?" |
