# Feedback Log - McGrath RFP Session

## 2026-02-20: RFP Question Strategy Insight (Colin)

**Key insight:** The goal is to ask questions that seem obvious/standard but don't give competitors an advantage.

**Competitive context:**
- Larger MSPs (Wipro, Infosys, etc.) have 100-200 people embedded at McGrath
- They likely get answers through internal channels, not the RFP Q&A
- BayOne has 5 people — information disadvantage
- RFP questions are public; competitors see everything

**The balance:**
1. Ask questions that enlighten US for a strong proposal
2. Don't reveal our gaps or strategy
3. Potentially expose gaps in competitors' thinking
4. Questions should look standard/obvious even when they're strategic

**Implication for skill design:**
- RFP question analysis should always consider: "Would a 200-person embedded team already know this?"
- If yes, asking it doesn't hurt us (they have it anyway) but helps level the field
- If asking reveals WE don't know something obvious, it could hurt positioning
