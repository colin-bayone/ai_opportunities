1. Executive Summary
McGrath RentCorp (MGRC), a publicly traded equipment rental company headquartered in Livermore, CA, has
issued a comprehensive RFP seeking a Managed Services Provider to support their entire enterprise technology
stack. This represents a significant growth opportunity for BayOne.
Deal Size Indicator Enterprise-grade — 10+ application areas, 50+ integrations, 24/7 support required
Current BayOne Footprint 5 resources currently embedded at McGrath
Contract Term Multi-year (pricing requested for Years 1, 2, and 3)
Competition Level Competitive bid — multiple MSPs invited
Strategic Value Anchor account potential; validates BayOne as full-scope MSP
Bottom Line: BayOne has an insider advantage with 5 people already on-site. The RFP explicitly states that MSPs
don't need to cover every service area. Our strategy should emphasize our existing relationship, institutional
knowledge, flexibility, and strength in key areas while partnering or subcontracting for gaps.

6. Responsibility Model Analysis
Model Definition Implication for BayOne
MSP Owned MSP is primarily responsible; MGRC looks to MSP as expert Full staffing &amp; accountability; higher margin potential
Shared Responsibility Jointly managed with defined roles &amp; shared accountability Collaborative model; splits defined in SOW; most common designatioAugmented by MSP MGRC retains ownership; MSP provides flex capacity/expertise Staff aug model under MGRC direction; lower risk, lower margin
MGRC Owned MGRC manages fully; out of scope for MSP No responsibility; do not price
Key Insight: The majority of line items are designated Shared Responsibility. This is favorable for BayOne — it
means MGRC wants a partner, not a full outsource. Our existing 5-person footprint gives us a head start on
understanding their processes and building the shared model.

11. Win Strategy & Competitive Positioning
Strategy 1: Lead with the Insider Advantage
Our 5 people on-site give us something no other bidder has: institutional knowledge of McGrath's systems, people,
and processes. The proposal should quantify this — reduced ramp-up time, lower knowledge transfer risk, and faster
time-to-value. Emphasize continuity vs. disruption of bringing in a completely new vendor.
Strategy 2: Embrace the Shared Model
MGRC explicitly designed most requirements as Shared Responsibility. Position BayOne as the ideal collaborative
partner — not a rigid MSP that imposes its own processes. Highlight our flexibility and ability to work within McGrath's
existing governance. This is a key differentiator from large MSPs who typically push their own frameworks.
Strategy 3: Bid Strong on Core, Partner for Gaps
The RFP says: 'We realize not every MSP will be able to provide all services...we encourage you to respond
regardless.' This is our green light. Bid confidently on areas where we have strength (Salesforce, Oracle Fusion
functional support, integration monitoring, general app admin). For specialized gaps (MuleSoft deep dev, OCI
infrastructure, Snowflake architecture), either subcontract with named partners or propose as augmented capacity —
and be transparent about it.
Strategy 4: Competitive Pricing with Flexibility
MGRC values 'maximum flexibility in overall usage and allocation combined with cost favorability.' Propose a blended
model: a fixed monthly retainer for core operations + a flexible pool of hours for transformation/enhancement work.
Offer a discount for bundled services and prior relationship. Price aggressively for Year 1 to win, with reasonable
escalation in Years 2-3.
Strategy 5: Diversity as a Differentiator
RFP question 1.7 specifically asks about diversity certification. BayOne's leadership and MakeTechPurple initiative
are genuine differentiators. Highlight women-owned/led status, the 500+ women placed in tech careers, and
commitment to diverse hiring. Many enterprise procurement teams have diversity spend targets — this could be a
tiebreaker.
Strategy 6: Demonstrate Technical Depth in the Demo
If shortlisted (week of April 2), the presentation/demo will be critical. Prepare a live walkthrough showing our team's
familiarity with McGrath's specific environment — their Salesforce instance, their Oracle Fusion modules, their
integration landscape. No other MSP can do this on Day 1.
