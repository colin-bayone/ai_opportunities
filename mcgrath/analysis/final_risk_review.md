## Questions with Concerns

### Q4: Phased Go-Live Approach
**Question:** Is MGRC open to a phased go-live approach where application areas are transitioned in priority waves rather than a single go-live date of July 6 for all areas?

**Concern:** Suggesting phases could imply we lack confidence in Big Bang execution — something a 200-person embedded team would handle without asking.

**Recommendation:** **KEEP**

**Rationale:** Phased transitions are industry best practice. This positions us as pragmatic project managers, not as uncertain. Large MSPs would also advocate for de-risking. The question shows sophistication, not weakness.

---

### Q9: July Go-Live Readiness
**Question:** Is the July 6 go-live date firm, or is there potential for schedule adjustment based on current project status? What contingency exists if the Oracle Fusion go-live extends past May 18?

**Concern:** Asking if the date is "firm" could reveal nervousness about the timeline. A 200-person embedded team would know the Oracle Fusion status internally.

**Recommendation:** **KEEP**

**Rationale:** This levels the information field. The Oracle Fusion dependency is real risk that deserves formal clarification. Large MSPs know the answer — asking it formally gets it on record for everyone.

---

### Q11: Documentation State
**Question:** Can MGRC characterize the current state of operational documentation (runbooks, SOPs, architecture diagrams) for each application area? Which areas are well-documented vs. relying primarily on tribal knowledge?

**Concern:** Asking about "tribal knowledge" could imply KT concerns that a large embedded team wouldn't have — they already possess the tribal knowledge.

**Recommendation:** **KEEP**

**Rationale:** Documentation quality affects any vendor's transition risk, including large MSPs bringing in new resources. This is standard transition due diligence. The question positions us as thorough, not as outsiders.

---

### Q26: Testing & QA State
**Question:** What is the current state of test automation for regression, smoke, and integration testing? Would MGRC be interested in the MSP introducing automation capabilities as part of the engagement?

**Concern:** Proactively offering automation capabilities in the question itself tips our hand slightly — we're pitching before the proposal. Could reveal we see this as an upsell angle.

**Recommendation:** **KEEP** (minor concern)

**Rationale:** Large MSPs also position automation as a differentiator. The question is consultative, not pushy. However, if you want to be more neutral:

**Optional revision:** "What is the current state of test automation for regression, smoke, and integration testing? Are there specific QA areas where improvements are desired?"

## Strategic Observations

### Pattern: Risk-Focused Questions
Questions Q9, Q10, Q34, Q35, and Q36 all probe risk, contingency, and scope creep scenarios. Together, they could paint BayOne as cautious or even inexperienced.

**Assessment:** This pattern is actually a **strength**. It signals:
- Mature project management thinking
- Experience with complex transitions
- Sophistication in identifying hidden scope

Large MSPs often assume they can absorb risk through scale. BayOne's risk awareness differentiates us as a partner who won't be blindsided.

### Pattern: Depth Questions
The "Gap — Depth" questions (Q7, Q8, Q20, Q25) build thoughtfully on original questions. This shows we're reading carefully and thinking systematically.

If competitors analyze BayOne's questions, they might infer:
- We're focused on transition risk (true, and appropriate)
- We're thinking about phased approaches (true, and differentiating)
- We're interested in automation/AI (true, but so is everyone)

None of these reveals are damaging. They show sophistication without exposing gaps.

BayOne's questions appropriately probe some of these areas (Q14, Q9, Q21) to level the field.
