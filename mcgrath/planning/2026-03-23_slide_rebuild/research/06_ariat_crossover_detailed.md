# Ariat Crossover Detailed Comparison

## Purpose
Side-by-side content and quality comparison of each McGrath/Ariat crossover pair.
Recommendations indicate which source the slide builder should prefer and what specific content to use.

## Benchmark
The three HTML gold standard slides represent the target quality:
- `slide_01_ai_strategy_innovation.html` (Ariat 10) -- Card-based grid, purple gradient icons, tagline + bullet items per card, Inter font, clean header/footer
- `slide_02_enterprise_ai_solutions.html` (Ariat 11) -- 2x2 card grid, colored top borders, 2-column item lists within cards
- `slide_03_quality_engineering.html` (Ariat 12) -- Chevron flow bar + 3x2 detail card grid, layered purple gradients

Common traits: structured card layouts, bold-then-explain bullet pattern, minimal text density, professional visual hierarchy, BayOne purple palette.

## Ariat-Only Slides: Addition Recommendations

### Ariat 05: Partnership Models -- RECOMMEND ADD

**Content:** 4 engagement models in a 2x2 numbered grid:
1. **Managed Services** -- End-to-end service delivery with SLAs, billed as a fixed monthly fee
2. **Time & Material (T&M)** -- Flexible engagement where billing is based on actual hours worked
3. **Fixed Capacity** -- Pre-allocated team at a fixed cost, ensuring consistent resource availability
4. **Managed Projects** -- Fixed-price delivery with defined scope, budget, and milestones

**Why add it:** This is directly relevant to a managed services proposal. McGrath will want to understand how BayOne structures engagements. The McGrath deck currently has no equivalent -- it jumps from service offerings to solution details without explaining engagement models. This slide bridges that gap.

**Where to place it:** After the Service Offerings slide (McGrath 05), before the solution-specific content begins at slide 10.

**Adaptation needed:** Minimal. The content is generic and works for any proposal. If McGrath has a preferred engagement model, highlight it.
