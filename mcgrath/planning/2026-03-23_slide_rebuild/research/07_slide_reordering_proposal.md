# Slide Reordering & Flow Analysis

## Narrative Arc

BayOne's McGrath deck should tell this story in three sentences: *"We understand your NextGen transformation challenge and we've built a solution around it. AI-driven operations are our core differentiator, and we'll prove it with a phased delivery backed by real metrics and real case studies. Here's what it costs, here's why we're worth it, let's get started."*

The current deck tells a different, weaker story: *"Here's who we are (for 6 slides). Here's a vague solution. Here's a phased plan with AI buried at slide 15. Here are some case studies. Here's pricing. Oh, and here are some KPIs and prerequisites we forgot to put anywhere."*

---

## 1. Current Flow Analysis -- What's Wrong

### Problem 1: Too Much "About Us" Before the Solution
Slides 01-05 + 09 = **six consecutive slides** of company brochure before McGrath hears a single word about their own problem. By slide 6 (Make Tech Purple), the evaluator is thinking "when are they going to talk about us?"

### Problem 2: The AI Story Is Buried
Slide 24 (AI Enablement) -- BayOne's **single biggest differentiator** -- doesn't appear until position 15 of 30. It's sandwiched between a quality audit flowchart (23) and risk mitigation (26). The transcript is clear: *"The AI playbook is huge because that's one of the things they're looking at very carefully and that's our strength."* It needs to be in the first third of the deck.

### Problem 3: Transformation and AI Are Artificially Separated
Slide 14 (Transformation Journey: today vs. tomorrow) and slide 24 (AI Enablement) are separated by **10 slides**. Colin already flagged this. The "today vs. tomorrow" story and the "AI is how we get you there" story are one narrative. Splitting them kills the impact of both.

### Problem 4: Three Orphaned Slides at the End
Slides 46 (KPIs), 47 (Prerequisites), and 48 (Operations Snapshot) come **after** the closing slide (45). This is the deck equivalent of shouting important things at someone who's already walking away. These contain substantive content:
- 46 has 10 measurable KPIs -- belongs with the delivery approach
- 47 has 9 prerequisites -- belongs near the commercial/engagement terms
- 48 has operational proof points (100+ apps, >95% SLA, >5K tickets/qtr) -- belongs with credibility

### Problem 5: Key Asks Duplicates Risk Content
Slide 28 (Key Asks) and slide 26 (Risks Part 1) share nearly identical first two rows (KT facilitation, infrastructure support). This creates a "didn't they already say this?" moment for the evaluator.

### Problem 6: Case Studies Come After Risks
Currently: Risks (26-27) --> Key Asks (28) --> Summary (29) --> Case Studies (30-34). This means BayOne raises concerns about what could go wrong, then asks McGrath for things, then tries to close, THEN proves they can do it. The proof should come **before** the risks -- build confidence first, then address concerns.

### Problem 7: BayOne's Advantage Is Wasted After Pricing
Slide 37 (BayOne's Advantage) sits right after pricing (36). It reads as a defensive "but we're worth it!" rather than a confident differentiator. It would be stronger either immediately after the solution (reinforcing the pitch) or as a penultimate slide before the close (final confidence boost).

### Problem 8: Slide 11 Is Not an Executive Summary
Slide 11 is labeled "Exec Summary" but currently contains a WIP to-do list (escalation matrix, assumptions, case studies, AI enablement...). This is the second slide McGrath will see after the title. It needs to be a real executive summary, not project notes.

### What Works
- **The section divider structure** (10, 21, 30) provides good visual pacing -- keep it
- **Case studies are grouped** (31-34) -- good, they tell a cumulative story
- **Risks are grouped** (26-27) and marked Final -- solid content
- **The closing slide** (45) exists and is clean -- just needs to be last

## 3. Combination Candidates

### Confirmed: Slides 14 + 24 (Transformation Journey + AI Enablement)
**Status:** Already flagged by Colin. Strong recommendation.

**Why:** Slide 14 sets up "today's problems" and "tomorrow's vision." Slide 24 is supposed to explain HOW you get from today to tomorrow (AI). Currently they're 10 slides apart, which kills the narrative. Combined, this becomes the centerpiece slide of the entire deck: "Here's your challenge, here's the transformation, and AI is the engine."

**Note:** Slide 24 is WIP with placeholder content from someone else. The Ariat version (slide 10: AI Strategy & Innovation) is dramatically better and has an HTML gold standard. The combined slide should draw from Ariat, not from McGrath slide 24's current content.

### Candidate: Slides 31 + 32 (Case Study Title + Case Study 1)
**Strength: Medium.** Slide 31 is just a title card: "Case Study -- Oracle Fusion Cloud HCM Implementation for a National Security Agency." Slide 32 is the actual case study. The title could be the header of slide 32, eliminating one slide. However, title cards provide visual breathing room in presentations, so this is a judgment call.

### Candidate: Slides 26 + 28 (Risks Part 1 + Key Asks) -- Content Dedup
**Strength: Low (don't combine, but fix overlap).** The first two rows of slide 28 (Key Asks) are nearly identical to the first two rows of slide 26 (Risks). Rather than combining, the Key Asks slide should be rewritten to remove the duplicated risk framing and focus on what McGrath needs to provide (access, SPOCs, documentation, approvals).

### Candidate: Slides 11 + 12 (Exec Summary + Solution Summary)
**Strength: Low.** Both are WIP and both attempt to summarize the solution. However, they serve different purposes: 11 should be a high-level narrative summary (2-3 paragraphs) while 12 is a structured outcomes table. Keep separate, but they need to complement rather than overlap once rewritten.

## 5. Slides That Might Be Missing

Based on the Ariat crossover mapping (`research/01_ariat_crossover_mapping.md`):

### Ariat Slide 05: Partnership Models (4 engagement types)
**Should consider adding.** McGrath is evaluating engagement models. A slide showing BayOne's flexibility (managed services, project-based, staff augmentation, hybrid) would strengthen the commercial section. Currently, the deck jumps from solution to delivery without explaining the engagement model.

### Ariat Slide 11: Enterprise AI Solutions
**Should consider adding.** No McGrath equivalent exists. Given AI is THE differentiator, having only one AI-focused slide (the combined 14+24) may not be enough. This Ariat slide has an HTML gold standard already built. It could follow the combined transformation+AI slide to provide depth.

### Ariat Slide 12: Quality Engineering
**Lower priority.** Slide 23 (Service Quality Audit) partially covers this territory, but the Ariat version has a different angle. Worth reviewing but not critical.

### Communication / Governance Model
**Gap to flag.** Slides 16 (Communication Model), 17 (Escalation Matrix), and 18 (Governance & Reporting) were all skipped because they were badly executed. However, an RFP evaluator will expect to see SOME governance structure. The concept is valid even though Amit's execution was poor. Consider whether a single, well-designed governance slide should be added to Section 3 (Delivery Approach).
