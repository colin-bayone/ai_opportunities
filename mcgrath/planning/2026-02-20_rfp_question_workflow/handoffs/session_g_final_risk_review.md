## Critical Context (from Colin Moore, Director of AI)

**BayOne's position:**
- 5 people embedded at McGrath
- Positioning as strategic partner, not staffing vendor

**Competitor landscape:**
- Large MSPs (Wipro, Infosys, Cognizant) have 100-200 people embedded
- They likely get answers through internal channels, not the RFP Q&A
- BayOne is at an information disadvantage

**The strategic balance:**
1. Ask questions that enlighten US for a strong proposal
2. Don't reveal our gaps or strategy to competitors
3. Questions should look standard/obvious — even when they're strategic
4. If larger MSPs already know the answer internally, asking it helps level the field without hurting us

**Key insight:** The goal is questions that seem obvious but don't give anyone else an advantage or more information to work with for a great proposal.

## Your Task

Review all 36 questions in the consolidated document. For each question, assess:

1. **Would a 200-person embedded team already know this?**
   - If yes: Safe to ask. Levels the playing field.
   - If no: Consider whether asking reveals we don't know something obvious.

2. **Does this question reveal BayOne's strategy or gaps?**
   - Flag anything that tips our hand

3. **Does this question position us well?**
   - Shows sophistication without showing weakness

4. **Could this question expose gaps in competitors' thinking?**
   - Strategic value if it surfaces something they haven't considered
